# Rail Specialist (rail-sector SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **health & safety in the rail sector** — heavy rail,
metro, light rail/trams and rail freight. Rail's defining feature is a **dedicated rail-safety regime
with its own regulator and a no-blame accident investigator, co-regulated *alongside* WHS/HSWA** — a
structure that differs in name but is parallel in shape between NZ and Australia. The harm is dominated by
**being struck by rail traffic** (track workers, level crossings, shunting) plus **electrification**,
**fatigue** and **operational** failures. Owns the rail sector, the regime and rail-corridor safety;
routes the generic hazards to the relevant SME.

## When to use

- Any **railway operation** — main-line/heavy rail, **metro/commuter**, **light rail/trams**, **rail
  freight**, yards and depots, and rail infrastructure/maintenance.
- The rail-corridor killers — **track-worker protection**, **level crossings**, **shunting/yard**
  operations, and **electrification** (flagship — `references/good-practice.md`).
- The **rail-safety regime** — accreditation/licence, the **safety management system / safety case**, and
  **rail safety worker** competency, fatigue and drug/alcohol rules (flagship —
  `references/regulatory-regimes.md`).

  **on-site (non-rail) vehicle-pedestrian traffic & temporary traffic management on roads** →
  `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/` (rail's own corridor is owned here, but the **road side of a
  level crossing / road-rail interface** routes to the traffic SME).
- The **physical/specific hazard method** belongs to its SME (this skill routes to them, in the rail
  context):
  - **Overhead line / traction / electrical isolation & approach distances** → `../electrical-energy-specialist/`.
  - **On-track plant / road-rail vehicle / machinery guarding & isolation** → `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
  - **Working at height** (OLE masts, structures, rolling stock) → `../working-at-height-specialist/`;
    **tunnels, tanks, underframe confined spaces** → `../confined-space-specialist/`; **fatigue/shift work
    as work design** → `../psychosocial-risk-specialist/`.
- **Who holds the WHS/HSWA duty / notifiable events** → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` /
  `../safework-au-specialist/` — but note rail is **also** regulated under a **separate rail-safety
  regulator** (see below).
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## The rail risk picture

Rail traffic is **massive, fast and unable to stop quickly or steer** — and increasingly **quiet**
(electric). The harm clusters where people meet moving rail vehicles:

1. **Track workers struck by rail traffic** — the defining rail-corridor risk: maintenance and inspection
   staff in the live corridor.
2. **Level crossings** — collisions with road vehicles and pedestrians (public *and* workers).
3. **Shunting & yard operations** — struck by or **crushed between** rolling stock, coupling/uncoupling,
   run-throughs.
4. **Electrification** — **overhead line equipment (OLE, e.g. 25 kV)** and traction supply; electrocution
   and arc → `../electrical-energy-specialist/`.
5. **Operational safety** — **SPAD (signal passed at danger)**, overspeed, mis-routing; and **fatigue**
   and **impairment** in safety-critical roles.
6. **Plus** working at height (masts, structures, rolling stock), confined spaces (tunnels, tanks,
   underframe), on-track plant/hi-rail, manual handling, **noise and hand-arm vibration** (track work),
   and the **platform-train interface** for station staff.

## The sector regulatory regime (the defining feature)

Rail runs under a **dedicated rail-safety law and regulator**, with a separate **no-blame investigator**,
**co-regulated alongside** the general WHS/HSWA worker-safety regime. The shape is parallel NZ vs AU:

- **AU — ONRSR under the Rail Safety National Law (RSNL).** One national regulator (the **Office of the
  National Rail Safety Regulator**) under the **RSNL** (2012 — it replaced 46 laws and 7 regulators). A
  **rail transport operator must be accredited** and run a **Safety Management System**; **rail safety
  workers** are subject to **competency, fatigue and drug/alcohol** rules. The **ATSB** is the **no-blame**
  rail investigator. **WHS** (state regulators) applies **in parallel**.
- **NZ — Waka Kotahi (NZTA) under the Railways Act 2005.** To operate or provide access to a railway you
  need a **licence**, which requires an **approved rail safety case** (a principles-based, industry-led
  model). **WorkSafe NZ** covers the **HSWA** worker duties in parallel, and **TAIC** is the **no-blame**
  investigator (NZTA must notify TAIC of accidents/notifiable incidents).

Detail and the NZ/AU contrast in `references/regulatory-regimes.md`.

## Core controls (owned here)

Summary; detail in `references/good-practice.md`:

- **Track-worker protection** — the hierarchy: **avoid the corridor** (work in possessions/when the line
  is blocked) before relying on **lookout/warning**; safe places of refuge, worksite protection,
  authority-to-work, and competent **protection officers**; "you don't go on or near the line without
  protection".
- **Level crossings** — sighting, active/passive protection, and managing the **road side** with the
  traffic SME.
- **Shunting/yards** — controlled movements, communication, exclusion, no walking between vehicles
  without protection.
- **Electrification** — isolation/permits and approach distances for OLE/traction →
  `../electrical-energy-specialist/`.
- **Safety-critical workforce** — competency, **fatigue management** and **drug/alcohol** controls, and a
  just-culture incident/SPAD response.

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction and regime** — NZ (Waka Kotahi, Railways Act 2005) or AU (ONRSR, RSNL) — and
   confirm WHS/HSWA applies in parallel.
2. **Anchor on the safety case / SMS** — is the operator accredited/licensed, and are the rail-safety
   risks managed in the safety management system?
3. **Work the rail-corridor killers** — track-worker protection, level crossings, shunting/yards,
   electrification.
4. **Cover the safety-critical workforce** — competency, fatigue, drug/alcohol, SPAD/operational safety.
5. **Route the generic hazards** to their SME (electrical/OLE, height, confined space, plant, road
   traffic at crossings, psychosocial/fatigue).
6. **State residual obligations** — duties under **both** the rail-safety law and WHS/HSWA; an accredited
   SMS/safety case, competent rested workers and worksite protection are mandatory. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**AU:** the **Rail Safety National Law (RSNL)**, regulated by **ONRSR**, with **rail transport operator
accreditation**, a **Safety Management System**, and **rail safety worker** competency/fatigue/D&A rules;
the **ATSB** is the no-blame investigator; **WHS** applies in parallel (Victoria OHS Act; WA). **NZ:** the
**Railways Act 2005**, regulated by **Waka Kotahi (NZTA)** via a **rail participant licence + approved
safety case**; **WorkSafe NZ** for the **HSWA** worker duties; **TAIC** is the no-blame investigator.
Jurisdiction variations → `../safework-au-specialist/`. Detail in `references/regulatory-regimes.md`;
confirm current editions via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

Either a **rail safety plan or risk screen** (jurisdiction & regime → accreditation/safety case →
rail-corridor killers (track protection, level crossings, shunting, electrification) → safety-critical
workforce (competency/fatigue/D&A) → routed generic hazards → critical questions), or the
**rail-specific controls and questions** to feed into a bow tie (`../critical-risk-manager/`), task
analysis (`../task-analysis-author/`) or SOP (`../sop-author/`). A draft for a competent person to
validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice rail-safety guidance — **not legal advice and not a substitute for a
competent person or the current rail-safety law (RSNL / Railways Act 2005) and WHS/HSWA requirements**,
which are **separate, co-regulated bodies of law** that differ between NZ and Australia. Being struck by
rail traffic and electrification kill quickly; validate track protection, the safety case/SMS and the
safety-critical-worker controls against the actual operation and the current regime before relying on
them.
