# Rail — good practice, corridor killers & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, the rail risk profile and competency for the rail sector — heavy rail,
metro, light rail/trams and rail freight. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from the AU ONRSR /
Rail Safety National Law framework and the NZ Railways Act 2005 (Waka Kotahi/NZTA), with WHS/HSWA in
parallel. **Not legal advice**; the regime and rules differ by jurisdiction and change — treat references
as verify-pointers and confirm against the current law and a competent person.

> The regulatory regime and the NZ/AU regulator map live in `regulatory-regimes.md`. This file is the
> **risk method**: the rail-corridor killers and how good operations control them.

## How rail safety is managed — the safety case / SMS

Rail runs on an **accredited (AU) or licensed (NZ) safety management system / safety case**: the operator
demonstrates it can identify and control its rail-safety risks **so far as is reasonably practicable**.
Within that, the harm clusters where **people meet moving rail traffic** — which **cannot stop quickly or
steer**, and is increasingly **quiet** (electric).

## The corridor killers (and good-practice control)

### Track workers struck by rail traffic — the defining risk
- Maintenance, inspection and construction staff working **on or near the live line**.
- Controls — the **track-protection hierarchy**:
  1. **Avoid the live corridor** — work in a **possession** or when the line is **blocked**; plan work
     into track-closure windows.
  2. **Physically separate** — barriers / safe places of refuge with adequate clearance and warning time.
  3. **Warning systems** — automatic/lookout-operated warning only where higher controls aren't
     practicable.
  - Always: a documented **worksite protection** plan, an **authority to work / protection authority**, a
    **competent protection officer/lookout**, and the rule that **no one goes on or near the line without
    protection**. Account for **adjacent/opposite lines** and electrification.

### Level crossings
- Collisions with road vehicles and pedestrians (public **and** workers/road-rail movements).
- Controls: sighting and approach management, **active (lights/booms) or passive** protection appropriate
  to the risk, and managing the **road side** with `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.

### Shunting & yard operations
- **Struck by** or **crushed between** rolling stock; coupling/uncoupling; run-throughs; working at the
  ends/between vehicles.
- Controls: controlled, communicated movements; **exclusion** and not walking between vehicles without
  protection; competent shunters; secured/immobilised vehicles before work; lighting and clear walkways.

### Electrification
- **Overhead line equipment (OLE, e.g. 25 kV)** and traction supply — electrocution and arc.
- Controls: **isolation and permits**, **approach distances**, earthing, and competent authorised
  persons → `../../electrical-energy-specialist/`.

### Operational & safety-critical-worker risk
- **SPAD (signal passed at danger)**, overspeed and mis-routing; and **fatigue/impairment** in
  safety-critical roles.
- Controls: signalling and operating rules, **competency and certification** of rail safety workers,
  **fatigue risk management**, **drug & alcohol** controls (zero/limit for rail safety workers), and a
  **just-culture** SPAD/incident investigation that learns rather than only blames.

### Plus
- **Working at height** (OLE masts, structures, on top of rolling stock) → `../../working-at-height-specialist/`;
  **confined spaces** (tunnels, tanks, underframe) → `../../confined-space-specialist/`; **on-track plant
  / road-rail (hi-rail) vehicles** → `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`; **manual handling, noise and
  hand-arm vibration** (track maintenance); and the **platform-train interface** for station staff.

## Competency & the workforce

- **Rail safety workers** must be **competent and certified** for their role, **fit for duty** (fatigue,
  drug/alcohol), and working to the operator's rules and the **safety case / SMS**.
- **Protection officers/lookouts**, shunters, signallers, train drivers and electrical authorised persons
  hold the specific competencies their task requires. Confirm the competency/high-risk-work side via
  `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## Critical questions to consider

- Which **regime** applies — **NZ** (Waka Kotahi, Railways Act 2005 licence + safety case) or **AU**
  (ONRSR, RSNL accreditation + SMS)? Is **WHS/HSWA** being managed in parallel?
- **Track work:** is the **highest practicable protection** used (possession/blockage before lookout), with
  a worksite-protection plan, authority to work and a competent protection officer — accounting for
  adjacent lines and OLE?
- **Level crossings / shunting:** are road-rail and yard movements controlled, with exclusion and no
  walking between vehicles unprotected?
- **Electrification:** isolation, permits and approach distances for OLE/traction?
- **Safety-critical workforce:** competency, **fatigue** and **drug/alcohol** managed; a just-culture SPAD
  response?
- Are the **generic hazards routed** (electrical, height, confined space, plant, road traffic, fatigue)?
