# Rail — the regulatory regimes (NZ + AU)

Rail safety is **co-regulated**: a **dedicated rail-safety law and regulator** governs the *railway and
its operations*, a separate **no-blame investigator** examines accidents, and the general **WHS/HSWA**
regime covers the *workers* — all at once. The **shape is parallel** between NZ and Australia (regulator +
accreditation/licence + safety case/SMS + no-blame investigator + WHS), but the **names and instruments
differ**.

## Australia — ONRSR under the Rail Safety National Law

- **Law & regulator:** the **Rail Safety National Law (RSNL)** (commenced 2012 via South Australia,
  replacing **46** pieces of state/territory/Commonwealth legislation and **7** regulators), regulated by
  the single national **Office of the National Rail Safety Regulator (ONRSR)**.
- **Accreditation:** a **rail transport operator** must be **accredited** by ONRSR before operating —
  demonstrating the competence and capacity to manage its rail-safety risks — and must run a **Safety
  Management System (SMS)**.
- **Rail safety workers:** subject to **competency, fatigue and drug/alcohol** requirements (zero alcohol /
  no prescribed drug while carrying out rail safety work).
- **Investigator:** the **ATSB** is the national **no-blame** rail accident investigator.
- **WHS in parallel:** the model **WHS** law (state/territory regulators; Victoria's OHS Act) covers worker
  health & safety **alongside** the RSNL — ONRSR and the WHS regulators co-ordinate.

## New Zealand — Waka Kotahi (NZTA) under the Railways Act 2005

- **Law & regulator:** the **Railways Act 2005**, with **Waka Kotahi / NZ Transport Agency (NZTA)** as the
  rail safety regulator. The Act is **principles-based and industry-led** — the industry develops its codes,
  standards and safety risk-management, and NZTA oversees and can intervene.
- **Licence + safety case:** to **operate or provide access to** a railway you need a **rail participant
  licence**, which requires an **approved rail safety case**.
- **Investigator:** **TAIC (Transport Accident Investigation Commission)** is the **no-blame** investigator;
  NZTA must **notify TAIC** of accidents/notifiable incidents (Railways Act s 13(4)).
- **HSWA in parallel:** **WorkSafe NZ** covers the **HSWA 2015** worker duties **alongside** the Railways
  Act.

## NZ vs AU at a glance

| | New Zealand | Australia |
|---|---|---|
| **Rail-safety law** | **Railways Act 2005** | **Rail Safety National Law (RSNL)** |
| **Rail-safety regulator** | **Waka Kotahi (NZTA)** | **ONRSR** (national) |
| **Entry control** | Rail participant **licence** + **approved safety case** | **Accreditation** + **Safety Management System** |
| **No-blame investigator** | **TAIC** | **ATSB** |
| **Worker H&S (parallel)** | **WorkSafe NZ** (HSWA 2015) | **State/territory WHS** regulators (model WHS) |
| **Style** | Principles-based, industry-led | National, prescribed duties incl. rail-safety-worker D&A/fatigue |
