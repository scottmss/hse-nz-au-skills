# Commercial fishing & aquaculture — good practice, fatal risks & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, fatal risks, competency and critical questions for commercial fishing
and aquaculture. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from Maritime NZ (the HSWA designated agency
for ships) + MOSS guidance and the Australian AMSA Domestic Commercial Vessel system + state WHS. **Not
legal advice**; the co-regulated regime and rules differ by jurisdiction and change — treat references
as verify-pointers and confirm against the current law and a competent person.

> The regulatory regime and the NZ/AU regulator split live in `regulatory-regimes.md`. This file is the
> **risk method**: the fatal risks and how good operations control them.

## Why fishing is a high-fatality sector

Commercial fishing routinely sits among the **highest-fatality occupations**. Four things combine:
a **moving, unstable workplace**; **heavy gear under tension**; **remoteness from rescue**; and
**fatigue** from long trips and watchkeeping. Aquaculture adds **diving** and **workboat** risk. The
controls below target the events that actually kill people.

## The fatal risks (and good-practice control)

### Man overboard (MOB) & drowning — the leading killer
- Most fishing deaths are **falling/being dragged overboard** and **drowning** (often without a
  lifejacket), and **capsize/sinking**.
- Controls: **wear a lifejacket / PFD on deck** — the single highest-value control (auto-inflating, worn,
  serviced); **stay-aboard** design (bulwarks/guardrails, handholds, non-slip, clip-on points where gear
  runs); keep clear of gear that can drag you over; **MOB alarm**, a **rehearsed recovery** method and
  means to get a person back aboard; work-deck awareness in poor weather/at night.

### Vessel stability, capsize & sinking
- Loss of stability or watertight integrity — overloading, shifting catch/water, free-surface, downflooding,
  weather, and **crossing the bar**.
- Controls: know and respect the **stability limits**; don't overload; **secure catch and gear**; maintain
  **freeboard** and watertight integrity (close hatches/doors, working freeing ports/pumps); manage
  free-surface; **bar-crossing** procedures, weather limits and a go/no-go decision the skipper can make
  without commercial pressure.

### Deck machinery entanglement & gear under tension
- **Winches, line/net haulers, net drums, capstans** — entanglement and drawing-in; **warps and ropes
  under tension** — struck-by and **snap-back**.
- Controls: **guard** machinery and keep emergency stops within reach; trained operators; keep clear of
  **bights** and the snap-back zone; no loose clothing; well-maintained gear. Machinery-guarding method →
  `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`.

### Falls on deck & into holds
- A wet, moving, obstructed deck; open **fish holds**.
- Controls: non-slip surfaces, housekeeping, hold-opening protection, lighting, footwear.

### Emergency preparedness & survival
- Remote from rescue — survival depends on the crew.
- Controls: **EPIRB**, **liferaft**, flares, **immersion/survival suits**, VHF/comms, fire-fighting, and
  **drills** so the crew can raise the alarm and survive immersion until rescue. Cold-water immersion and
  hypothermia method → `../../water-safety-specialist/`.

### Fatigue, lone & remote work
- Long trips, broken sleep, small crews, watchkeeping — fatigue drives errors and MOB.
- Controls: realistic **trip and watch schedules**, adequate crewing, rest quality, and a culture where a
  tired crew can stop. Work-design/psychosocial method → `../../psychosocial-risk-specialist/`.

## Aquaculture-specific

- **Occupational diving** on marine farms (mussel, salmon) — drowning and decompression illness; safe
  diving systems, standby, and competency → `../../water-safety-specialist/`.
- **Workboats/barges** — man-overboard, crush between vessel and structure, manual handling at the rail.
- **Manual handling** — heavy lines, mussel ropes, harvest; design out the load (mechanical aids, layout).
- **Machinery** — graders, harvesters, winches → `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
- **Chemicals & biosecurity** — antifoulants, fuel, refrigerants, husbandry chemicals →
  `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`; coolstores/CA rooms as confined spaces →
  `../../confined-space-specialist/`.

## Competency & the vessel safety system

- The vessel runs under an **approved maritime safety system** — NZ: **MOSS (MTOP)** or a Specified
  Limits Permit; AU: an AMSA **Certificate of Operation/Survey** + **Safety Management System**.
- **Crew** hold the right qualifications — NZ **SeaCert**; AU **certificates of competency** — and are
  **inducted and drilled**. See `regulatory-regimes.md` and `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## Critical questions to consider

- Which **regime** applies — **NZ** (Maritime NZ, one regulator) or **which Australian state** (AMSA + the
  state WHS regulator)? Fishing or aquaculture?
- Does the vessel run under an **approved safety system** (MOSS/MTOP or AMSA Certificate + SMS), and is
  the **crew qualified, inducted and drilled**?
- Are **lifejackets/PFDs worn on deck**, and is there a **rehearsed MOB recovery**?
- Are the **stability limits** known and respected (loading, freeboard, watertight integrity,
  bar-crossing, weather limits)?
- Is **deck machinery guarded**, with crew clear of bights and snap-back?
- Is **emergency equipment** carried, current and **drilled** (EPIRB, liferaft, immersion suits, comms)?
- Is **fatigue** managed (trip/watch schedules, crewing), and can the skipper make a **no-go** call free
  of commercial pressure?
- Aquaculture: are **diving** and **workboat** operations run to a safe system?
