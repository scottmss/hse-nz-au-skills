# Commercial fishing & aquaculture — the regulatory regimes (NZ + AU)

The defining feature of fishing/aquaculture safety is that it is **co-regulated**: **maritime law**
governs the *vessel and navigation*, and **work-health-and-safety law** governs the *people on board*.
**Who regulates which** is where **NZ and Australia diverge most** — NZ uses **one regulator** for both;
Australia **splits** it between two.

## New Zealand — one regulator, two hats

**Maritime NZ** is the single point of contact for both sides:

- **Vessel & navigation safety** — Maritime NZ administers the **Maritime Transport Act 1994** and the
  **Maritime Rules**. Commercial fishing and aquaculture vessels (non-SOLAS, including small vessels)
  operate under an **approved maritime safety system** — generally the **Maritime Operator Safety System
  (MOSS)**, with an approved **Maritime Transport Operator Plan (MTOP)**, or a **Specified Limits Permit
  (SLP)**.
- **The people on board** — Maritime NZ is the **designated agency under HSWA 2015**, performing the
  regulator's functions for **work on board ships** (NZ vessels worldwide, and within NZ's EEZ/continental
  shelf). So **HSWA applies to the workers**, enforced by Maritime NZ — not WorkSafe.
- **How they fit together** — the MTA/Maritime Rules focus on the **vessel**; HSWA focuses on the
  **people**; there is overlap. You **don't duplicate** a control already delivered through the MTOP, but
  HSWA duties to workers apply **in addition** to the MOSS obligations.
- **Crew competency** — **SeaCert** maritime certificates appropriate to the vessel and its operating
  limits.

## Australia — a split between two regulators

A domestic commercial vessel is governed by **two** regimes at once:

- **The vessel — AMSA.** The **Australian Maritime Safety Authority** is the **National Marine Safety
  Regulator** for **Domestic Commercial Vessels (DCVs)** under the **Marine Safety (Domestic Commercial
  Vessel) National Law Act 2012**: survey, **Certificate of Operation / Certificate of Survey**,
  **certificates of competency**, and a vessel **Safety Management System (SMS)**.
- **The people — state/territory WHS regulator.** A DCV is also a **workplace under the state/territory
  WHS law**, so the **WHS regulator in each state/territory** (or **Comcare** in some cases) oversees WHS
  — hazardous tasks like manual handling, noise, confined space and falls sit here. AMSA and the WHS
  regulators coordinate via an **MOU**.
- **The trap:** **complying with the National Law (e.g. being in survey with AMSA) does NOT mean you
  comply with WHS law, and vice versa.** Both apply.
- **Occupational diving** on marine farms sits under the **occupational diving** codes/standards (e.g.
  **AS/NZS 2299**) administered through the WHS regime.

## NZ vs AU at a glance

| | New Zealand | Australia |
|---|---|---|
| **Vessel safety** | Maritime NZ — MTA 1994 + **MOSS (MTOP)** / SLP | **AMSA** — Marine Safety (DCV) National Law 2012 + **SMS** |
| **People (work H&S)** | **Maritime NZ** as the **HSWA designated agency** | **State/territory WHS regulator** (or Comcare) |
| **Number of regulators** | **One** (Maritime NZ wears both hats) | **Two** (AMSA + WHS regulator), coordinated by MOU |
| **Crew competency** | **SeaCert** | AMSA **certificates of competency** |
