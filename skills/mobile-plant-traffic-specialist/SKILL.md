# Mobile Plant & Traffic Specialist (vehicles & traffic SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for vehicles, mobile plant and workplace traffic — where
the killers are **vehicle-pedestrian strikes**, **collisions**, **being crushed/run over** (especially
reversing), and **loss of control**. Provides the good-practice controls (the heart of which is
**separating people from vehicles**), the **competency/licensing** required, and the governing NZ/AU
guidance. Forklifts and mobile plant are among the leading causes of workplace death, and most
involve a pedestrian.

## When to use

- Designing or reviewing **workplace/site traffic management** (vehicle and pedestrian movement).
- Managing **mobile plant** (forklifts, diggers, loaders, telehandlers) around people.
- **Reversing / blind-spot** risk, loading zones, and vehicle-pedestrian interfaces.
- **Driving / fleet** loss-of-control and journey-management risk.
- Work **on or near a live road** (temporary traffic management).

## When NOT to use

- **Fixed machinery guarding / isolation** (presses, conveyors, rotating plant) →
  `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
- **Crane lifts / HRWL licensing** → `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/` (this SME supplies controls/questions).
- The **legal duty / notifiable** detail → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.

## The risk

- **Vehicle-pedestrian strike** — a person hit by a vehicle or mobile plant (the most common fatal
  pattern), often while **reversing** or in a **blind spot**.
- **Collision / loss of control** of a vehicle (on site or on road), rollover, or being crushed
  between plant and a structure.
- **Public** struck near a worksite or asset.

## Critical controls — apply the hierarchy

The single most effective control is **separating people from vehicles**. See
`references/good-practice.md`.

1. **Eliminate** — remove the interaction: do the task without the vehicle; deliver/load away from
   people; design out reversing (drive-through layouts, turning circles).
2. **Substitute / isolate (the core control)** — **separate pedestrians and vehicles**: dedicated
   pedestrian routes and vehicle routes, physical **barriers**, **exclusion / no-go / plant-free
   zones**, and **scheduling** so people and plant aren't in the same place at the same time. Where
   routes must cross, controlled, marked **crossings**.
3. **Engineering** — reversing cameras/sensors, proximity detection, **warning systems** (reversing
   alarms, beacons), good lighting, mirrors, speed limits/calming, firm level ground.
4. **Administrative** — a **traffic management plan (TMP)**; **spotters** where blind spots or
   close-proximity work exist; agreed plant-operator/ground-crew **communication and eye contact**;
   keys/access control; site induction; journey/fatigue management for driving.
5. **PPE** — high-visibility clothing for anyone near vehicle areas; seatbelts worn in vehicles and
   mobile plant where fitted.

> **Reversing kills** — eliminate the need to reverse where possible; where not, use cameras/sensors,
> a spotter, and an exclusion zone behind the plant.

## Qualifications & competency

- **Drivers/operators** hold the right **licence and endorsements** and are competent for the
  vehicle/plant and conditions; **forklifts** require operator competency (NZ: **F endorsement** to
  operate on a road + operator certificate; AU: **HRWL class LF/LO**) → `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.
- **Mobile plant** operators competent for the specific machine.
- **Spotters / traffic controllers** trained for the role; on or near a **live road**, work to the
  **New Zealand Guide to Temporary Traffic Management (NZGTTM)** with a competent supervisor (e.g.
  STMS) — confirm the current requirement.

## Jurisdiction note

NZ: WorkSafe **Managing work site traffic — good practice guidelines** (separate pedestrians and
vehicles; crossings; eye contact); **on/near a live road**, NZTA's **New Zealand Guide to Temporary
Traffic Management (NZGTTM)** applies (it is replacing CoPTTM). AU: Safe Work Australia's **General
Guide for Workplace Traffic Management** + forklift traffic-management guidance, under the model WHS
Regs. Treat as verify-pointers; confirm via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

A **traffic management plan / control set** (vehicle & pedestrian routes → separation & crossings →
exclusion zones → reversing & blind-spot controls → competency → critical questions), or the
vehicle/plant-specific controls to feed a bow tie (`../critical-risk-manager/`) or task analysis
(`../task-analysis-author/`). A draft for a competent person to validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice guidance and structure — **not legal advice and not a substitute for
a competent person or the current guidance (incl. NZGTTM for road work)**. Vehicle-pedestrian risk is
site-specific and lethal; a competent person must validate the separation, routes, plant and traffic
plan against the actual site before work proceeds.
