# Agriculture / farm safety — good practice (NZ/AU)

Methodology reference for `agriculture-specialist`. Grounded in **WorkSafe NZ's agriculture guidance**
and the industry-led **Safer Farms / Farm Without Harm** programme (NZ), and the **WHS framework + ACCC
quad bike standard** (AU). Free government/regulator guidance is summarised with attribution; AS/NZS
standards are **copyright — verify-pointers only**. Confirm current editions/programmes.

> **Not legal advice.** A competent person validates controls against the actual farm, vehicles,
> terrain, animals and people. Farm-safety guidance and the CPD/OPD rules change — confirm current.

---

## 1. The farm safety picture

Agriculture carries a **disproportionately high share of work-related death and serious injury**. Two
features shape it:

- **The workplace is also a home.** Children, family and visitors are present in the work area — they
  must be planned for, not assumed absent.
- **Work is dispersed, remote and often solo**, frequently on **rough terrain with vehicles** — so the
  margins for error are small and help is far away.

The farm **PCBU** (owner/operator/contractor) owes the full primary duty: identify the fatal risks,
control them so far as is reasonably practicable, ensure competency and supervision, and keep a simple
**risk register / farm safety plan** that real people actually use.

---

## 2. The farm fatal risks (screen every operation against these)

1. **Vehicles** — quad bikes, side-by-sides, tractors, two-wheelers: rollover, loss of control, crush,
   run-over. **The leading killer** → `quad-bikes-and-vehicles.md`.
2. **Machinery & mobile plant** — **PTO entanglement**, augers, balers, post drivers, mixer wagons,
   unguarded drives; isolation/lockout for maintenance → `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
3. **Livestock** — crush/kick/trample in yards, races and crushes; bulls; calving; well-designed yards,
   escape routes, never get between a large animal and a barrier.
4. **Working alone / remotely** — a **check-in/communications plan** (radio/phone/satellite/PLB), known
   routes and ETAs, first-aid capability and a way to raise help; consider what happens if a solo rider
   is pinned.
5. **Working at height** — woolshed/shed roofs, silos, hay stacks, ladders →
   `../../working-at-height-specialist/`.
6. **Hazardous substances & health** — agrichemicals/sprays (storage, mixing, PPE, re-entry), fuel,
   fertiliser, dust; **zoonoses** (e.g. leptospirosis), noise, sun/UV, fatigue →
   `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.
7. **Confined spaces** — silos, feed/water/effluent tanks and ponds, augers (engulfment, atmosphere) →
   `../../confined-space-specialist/`.
8. **Electricity** — **overhead lines vs tall machinery, irrigators, augers and tipping trailers**;
   farm wiring → `../../electrical-energy-specialist/`.
9. **Child & visitor safety** — see §4.
10. **Musculoskeletal & manual tasks** — lifting, repetitive yard work, awkward postures.

## 3. Baseline controls (all farm work)

- **Right vehicle/plant for the task and terrain** (the first and often biggest control — see vehicles).
- **Competent, trained, fit operators**; supervision for new/young/seasonal workers; no drugs/alcohol;
  manage **fatigue** (long seasonal hours).
- **Maintained machinery** with guards in place; isolate before clearing/maintaining.
- **Communications & emergency plan** for remote/solo work; first aid; know your location references.
- **A living farm safety plan / risk register** and simple pre-task thinking (e.g. a Take-5) for
  non-routine jobs; report and learn from incidents and near-misses.

---

## 4. Children & visitors

Farm child deaths are most often **vehicles and machinery**. Controls:

- **Designated safe play areas** fenced off from the work area, yards, ponds and vehicle routes.
- **No children on adult quads / two-wheelers**, and not carried as passengers; age/engine-size limits
  for any child vehicle use; kids clear of tractors, PTO, augers and reversing vehicles.
- **Active supervision** — a responsible adult whose job is watching the child, not doing farm work.
- **Visitors/contractors** inducted to the farm's hazards and no-go areas.

---

## 5. Critical questions to consider

- For each task, **which fatal risks apply**, and is the **right vehicle/plant** chosen for the terrain?
- Are **rollover protection (CPD/OPD), helmets and seatbelts** fitted and **used**? (see vehicles ref)
- Is there a **communications/check-in plan** for solo/remote work and a way to raise help fast?
- Are **children and visitors** planned for — safe areas, no-go zones, active supervision?
- Are the specific hazards **routed and controlled** — machinery/PTO, substances, confined space,
  height, electrical? (links above)
- Could an event be **notifiable**? → `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../../safework-au-specialist/`.

## Sources & discipline

- **NZ:** WorkSafe **agriculture** guidance; industry-led **Safer Farms / Farm Without Harm**; WorkSafe
  **policy clarification on crush protection devices** (strongly recommends professionally designed CPDs).
  HSWA 2015 applies. Government/regulator guidance summarised with attribution.
- **AU:** WHS framework; **ACCC mandatory quad bike safety standard** (product safety — see vehicles
  ref); Safe Work Australia **QuadWatch** and **agriculture** guidance; the model WHS Codes for
  **plant** and **hazardous chemicals** apply to farm machinery/agrichemicals; state farm-safety
  programmes (e.g. SafeWork NSW). Jurisdiction variations → `../../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Specific-hazard standards live with the relevant SME (machinery, substances, confined space, height,
  electrical). Confirm current editions/programmes before relying on any figure or rule.
