# Farm vehicles & quad bikes — good practice (NZ/AU)

Flagship reference for `agriculture-specialist`. Farm vehicles are the **leading cause of on-farm
death** in both NZ and Australia, and **quad bike rollovers** dominate. Grounded in WorkSafe NZ
guidance / Safer Farms and the AU WHS + **ACCC quad bike standard**. Confirm current rules — the
CPD/OPD and vehicle requirements have changed recently and continue to evolve.

> **Not legal advice.** Validate vehicle choice and controls against the actual task, terrain, load and
> rider. Confirm the **current** CPD/OPD, helmet and age rules with WorkSafe / Safe Work / the ACCC.

---

## 1. The first control: choose the right vehicle for the task and terrain

Most quad-bike harm starts with the **wrong vehicle for the job**. Before reaching for a quad, ask
whether a safer option does the task:

- **Side-by-side (SSV / ROV / UTV)** with **ROPS + seatbelt** — inherently safer for carrying loads,
  passengers, spraying and steep/rough ground. Often the right answer.
- **Two-wheel farm bike** — lighter, different skill, no rollover-crush in the same way (but other
  risks).
- **Light utility vehicle / ute or tractor** for heavier or road tasks.

A **quad bike** is a *rider-active* machine best suited to lighter, mustering-type work on suitable
terrain by a trained rider — **not** heavy loads, towing on slopes, or carrying people.

---

## 2. Quad bikes (ATVs)

**Why they roll:** a quad has a high centre of gravity, narrow track and no protective structure — on a
**side-slope, in a ditch, over a hidden obstacle, or when overloaded/towing** it can roll, and the
rider is then **pinned and crushed/suffocated** under the machine.

**Controls (stack them — no single one is enough):**

- **Crush / Operator Protection Device (CPD/OPD):** a professionally designed device that creates a
  survival space or lets the bike roll off the rider.
  - **NZ:** WorkSafe **strongly recommends** professionally designed and manufactured CPDs on
    work quad bikes (per its policy clarification), alongside the other controls.
  - **AU:** an **Operator Protection Device (OPD) is mandatory on new general-use quad bikes from 11
    October 2021** under the ACCC mandatory standard; since **October 2020** new quads must also meet a
    US/EU stability standard and carry a **lateral-stability hang-tag** and rollover warning label.
- **Helmet:** wear an **approved helmet** sized and fastened correctly, every ride.
- **No passengers** on a single-rider quad (it destroys the rider's ability to shift weight and changes
  stability). **No children on adult-sized quads** — observe age/engine-size limits; children must not
  ride or be carried.
- **Rider training & capability:** trained, fit, not fatigued/impaired; **active riding** (shifting body
  weight); ride to the conditions.
- **Speed & terrain:** slow for slopes, wet ground, unfamiliar or hidden hazards; avoid steep
  side-slopes; pick the route.
- **Loads & towing:** stay within the manufacturer's limits — **front/rear racks and towed/implement
  loads sharply raise the rollover risk** and change handling; secure loads low and balanced.
- **Maintenance:** correct **tyre type and pressures** (critical to stability), brakes, throttle, and
  CPD correctly fitted.

---

## 3. Side-by-sides (SSV / ROV / UTV)

- **ROPS + seatbelt — and the seatbelt must be worn**: the rollover structure only protects an occupant
  who stays inside it. Fit and use **doors/nets/hip restraints**.
- **Rated occupants only**, each belted; **no overloading** the tray (raises CoG, affects braking and
  stability on slopes).
- Still rolls if driven beyond its limits on steep ground — match the machine and route to the task.

---

## 4. Tractors

- **ROPS/FOPS + seatbelt** — the leading tractor death is **rollover/overturn** (side and rearward);
  the belt keeps the operator in the protected zone.
- **Safe hitching:** tow only from the **drawbar / hitch point at the correct height** — hitching high
  (e.g. to the axle or top link) can cause the tractor to **flip rearward**.
- **No passengers; no run-over** — beware mounting/dismounting, bystanders and reversing; never get off
  with the engine/PTO running.
- **PTO and implement guarding** — entanglement is a major machinery risk →
  `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
- **Road travel, slopes, front-end loaders** (don't travel with the bucket high), and **overhead lines**
  with raised implements/tipping trailers → `../../electrical-energy-specialist/`.

---

## 5. Two-wheel farm motorbikes

- **Approved helmet**, training and skills for the terrain; ride to conditions; maintenance (tyres,
  brakes); fatigue and lone-rider check-in.

---

## 6. Children & vehicles

- **No children on adult quads or two-wheelers**, not as riders or passengers; respect age/engine-size
  limits for any youth vehicle.
- Keep children **clear of all vehicle and machinery work areas** (tractors, PTO, augers, reversing) —
  designated **safe play areas** away from the work zone, with active adult supervision.

---

## 7. Critical questions to consider

- Is a **quad even the right vehicle**, or is a **side-by-side / other** safer for this task and terrain?
- Is a **CPD/OPD fitted** (mandatory OPD on new AU general-use quads from Oct 2021; strongly recommended
  in NZ), and is an **approved helmet worn**?
- Any **passengers or children** on single-rider machines? (Should be none.)
- Are **load/tow limits** respected, **tyres/pressures** correct, and the **route/speed** matched to the
  terrain?
- For **tractors/SSVs**, is **ROPS + seatbelt** fitted and **worn**, and is hitching done safely?
- Is there a **lone-worker check-in** plan for someone riding out alone?
