# Electrical & stored energy — good practice, standards & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, governing guidance, qualifications and critical questions for
electrical safety and stored/released energy. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from NZ NZECP 34
/ WorkSafe Energy Safety and the AU model Code *Managing electrical risks in the workplace*, aligned
with **AS/NZS 4836**. **Not legal advice**; standards/regs change — treat references as verify-pointers.
**Electrical work is licensed work** — this reference is for risk management, not a licence to do it.

## Governing guidance & standards

- **NZ:** **NZECP 34:2001** — New Zealand Electrical Code of Practice for **Electrical Safe Distances**
  (overhead-line approach distances and consents); WorkSafe **Energy Safety**; the **Electricity
  (Safety) Regulations**; **AS/NZS 3000** (Wiring Rules). Electrical workers are registered/licensed
  (EWRB).
- **AU:** model **Code of Practice "Managing electrical risks in the workplace"** (adopted per
  state/territory; several have their own electrical-safety Acts; Victoria differs).
- **Both:** **AS/NZS 4836** — *Safe working on or near low-voltage electrical installations and
  equipment* (isolation, arc flash/blast, shock).

## The harm

- **Electric shock / electrocution** — contact with live conductors/equipment.
- **Arc flash / arc blast** — an explosive release of electrical energy: intense heat (severe burns),
  pressure wave and molten/projectile debris — can injure without contact.
- **Stored-energy release** — electrical (capacitors), **pressure** (hydraulic/pneumatic, steam,
  high-pressure fluid/oil/water), **thermal**; plus **hot-work** ignition and **lightning**.

## Critical controls (hierarchy order)

### Eliminate — de-energise and work dead (the primary electrical control)
The isolation sequence (align with AS/NZS 4836):
1. **Identify** the equipment and **all** energy sources.
2. **Isolate** at the source (open and secure the isolator/switch/breaker).
3. **Lock out and tag** the isolation point — personal lock; **one worker, one lock**; group lockout
   for multiple workers.
4. **Discharge / earth** stored electrical energy; **release** pressure and allow thermal cool-down.
5. **Prove dead** — **test before touch** with a proven tester; **treat all conductors (including
   neutral and earth) as live until proven de-energised.**
6. **Restore** only when everyone is clear and locks are removed by their owners.

> **Live work** is a last resort, only where de-energising is not reasonably practicable, by specially
> trained and authorised people under a permit with full controls.

### Substitute / isolate
- Lower-energy designs, remote/robotic switching, **safe zones** to create distance from the hazard.
- **Over-pressure protection, isolation and shut-off valves** to prevent sudden stored-energy release;
  good system design.

### Engineering
- Insulation, barriers and **guarding of live parts**; **RCDs**; **arc-flash boundaries**.
- **Overhead lines:** physical means to keep clear; **"tiger tails"/line markers are visual aids only**
  — they do **not** insulate or make contact safe.

### Administrative
- **Permit to work** for electrical work and **hot works**; isolation procedures and verification.
- **Minimum approach distances** to live lines and the **network operator's consent** for closer work.
- **Arc-flash study/labelling** (available short-circuit level, system voltage, protection boundary,
  PPE category) and signage in arc-flash areas.
- Spotters near live equipment; notification and signage where work is close to live wiring.

### PPE
- **Arc-rated PPE** to the assessed category in arc-flash areas (and **natural-fibre undergarments**);
  insulated tools and gloves rated for the voltage; eye/face protection. PPE is the last line.

## Overhead & underground power lines (NZ — NZECP 34)
- Keep **all activity at least 4 m** from overhead lines (people, plant, scaffold, equipment).
- To work **closer than the minimum distance**, obtain a **Close Approach Consent** from the line owner;
  reduced distances only where safety can be assured and approved.
- **Excavation within ~5 m** of a power pole, tower or stay wire requires the owner's **written
  consent**. Identify **underground** services before digging → `../../excavation-specialist/`.

## Qualifications & competency

- **Electrical work** by a **licensed/registered electrical worker** competent for the task and
  voltage; isolation and "prove dead" by a competent person.
- **Live work** only by specifically trained, **authorised** people under permit.
- Work near lines per the **line owner's** requirements and any **consent**; spotters trained.
- See `../../high-risk-work-specialist/` and `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.

## Critical questions to consider

- Can the equipment be **de-energised** and the work done dead? If not, why, and what authorises live
  work?
- Has every source been **isolated, locked, tagged, discharged/earthed and proven dead** (test before
  touch)?
- How do you **verify** you're working on the **correct** equipment, and that it can't be re-energised?
- What is the **arc-flash** rating of the PPE, and is it appropriate?
- What is the **minimum approach distance** to live lines, and is a **consent** in place?
- Are **overhead/underground services** identified before any digging/plant movement?
- Is there a **permit** (electrical and/or hot work), and a rescue/emergency plan?
