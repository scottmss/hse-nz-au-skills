# Electrical & Energy Specialist (stored/released energy SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for electrical safety and **stored/released energy** —
where the killers are **electric shock/electrocution**, **arc flash/blast** (severe burns), and the
uncontrolled **release of stored energy** (electrical, pressure, thermal). Provides the good-practice
controls (de-energise and isolate first; keep clear of live lines), the **competency/licensing**
required, and the governing NZ/AU guidance and standards. Electrical work is licensed, high-hazard
work — this SME helps make it dead, isolated and verified before anyone touches it.

## When to use

- Work **on or near energised electrical equipment**, or **de-energising/isolating** supply.
- Working **near overhead or underground power lines** (approach distances, consents).
- **Arc-flash/blast** risk assessment and PPE.
- **Hot works** (ignition sources) and **high-pressure fluid** release.

## When NOT to use

- **Mechanical** machine guarding / mechanical LOTO → `../machinery-safety-specialist/` (this SME
  covers the *electrical* isolation; they overlap — use both for combined isolations).
- **HRWL/competency systems** generally → `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/` (this SME supplies controls/questions).
- The **legal duty / notifiable / licensing** detail → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` /
  `../safework-au-specialist/`.

## The risk

- **Electric shock / electrocution** — contact with live conductors or equipment.
- **Arc flash / arc blast** — an explosive release of energy causing severe burns, pressure and
  projectiles, even without direct contact.
- **Uncontrolled release of stored energy** — electrical (capacitors), **pressure** (hydraulic/
  pneumatic, steam, high-pressure fluids), **thermal**; plus **hot works** ignition and **lightning**.

## Critical controls — apply the hierarchy

See `references/good-practice.md` for detail.

1. **Eliminate — de-energise and work dead.** The primary control for electrical work is to **isolate
   and de-energise**: identify the source, isolate, **lock out and tag**, **discharge/earth** stored
   energy, and **prove dead** (test before touch — treat everything as live until proven de-energised).
   Live work is a last resort only where de-energising is not reasonably practicable, under strict
   controls by competent people.
2. **Substitute / isolate** — design out the hazard (lower-energy systems, remote/robotic switching,
   "safe zones"); over-pressure protection, isolation and shut-off valves to prevent stored-energy
   release.
3. **Engineering** — barriers/insulation, RCDs, guarding of live parts, arc-flash boundaries, and for
   **overhead lines** keeping clear by physical means (goalposts, tiger tails as *visual aids only*).
4. **Administrative** — **permit to work** for electrical and hot works; **minimum approach distances**
   to live lines and the network operator's **consent** for closer work; arc-flash labelling and study;
   spotters; signage; isolation procedures and verification.
5. **PPE** — **arc-rated PPE** to the assessed category for arc-flash areas; insulated tools/gloves;
   eye/face protection. (Natural-fibre undergarments under arc-rated PPE.)

> **Overhead lines (NZ):** keep **all activity at least 4 m** from overhead lines (NZECP 34); to work
> closer you need a **Close Approach Consent** from the line owner. Excavation within ~5 m of a pole/
> tower/stay wire needs the owner's written consent.

## Qualifications & competency

- **Electrical work** done only by a **licensed/registered electrical worker** competent for the task
  and voltage; isolation/verification by a competent person.
- **Live work** (where unavoidable) only by specifically trained, authorised people under permit.
- Work near lines: follow the line owner's requirements and any **consent**; spotters trained.
- Confirm the licensing/competency system via `../high-risk-work-specialist/` /
  `../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.

## Jurisdiction note

NZ: **NZECP 34:2001** (electrical safe distances — the 4 m rule, close-approach consent); WorkSafe
**Energy Safety**; the **Electricity (Safety) Regulations**; **AS/NZS 3000** (wiring rules). AU: the
model **Code of Practice "Managing electrical risks in the workplace"** (adopted per jurisdiction;
several states have their own electrical-safety Acts; Victoria differs). **AS/NZS 4836** (safe working
on or near low-voltage installations — arc flash, isolation) applies in both. Treat as verify-pointers;
confirm via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice guidance and structure — **not legal advice and not a substitute for
a licensed electrical worker or the current standards/regulations**. Electrical and stored-energy work
is licensed, high-hazard work; a competent/licensed person must isolate, prove dead and validate every
control against the actual installation before any work proceeds. **Treat all conductors as live until
proven dead.**
