# Container & terminal mobile-plant operations — good practice (NZ/AU)

Flagship reference for `stevedoring-specialist`. The container terminal is where stevedoring's signature
killer lives: **very large mobile plant working among people on foot, with suspended loads overhead**.
Grounded in the **AU model Code _Managing risks in stevedoring_** and NZ port-sector guidance. Standards
are **verify-pointers**; confirm current editions.

> **Not legal advice.** Validate the terminal traffic-management and exclusion system against the actual
> site, plant and operation. The wider on-site traffic method lives in
> `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`; this is the stevedoring-specific overlay.

---

## 1. The hazard: big plant, blind spots, people on foot

A container terminal runs **straddle carriers, reach stackers, ship-to-shore (quay) cranes, terminal
tractors and forklifts** — large, fast and with **substantial blind spots** — in the same space as
**workers on foot** (lashing crews, checkers, lashers, supervisors). The defining fatality is a
**pedestrian struck or run over** by plant whose operator could not see them, or a person caught
**under a suspended container**. **Separation is the central control.**

---

## 2. Separate plant from people (the spine)

Work **down the hierarchy**:

- **Eliminate/reduce people on the ground** — automate or remote where possible; design the operation so
  lashing/checking happens in **defined, protected zones**, not loose in the plant run.
- **Physical separation** — **designated pedestrian walkways, crossings and refuges**; barriers; one-way
  plant routes; keep walkways out of plant turning/reversing paths.
- **Exclusion zones** — around **operating plant** and **under/within range of suspended loads**; nobody
  enters without positive permission.
- **Positive communication & permission** — agreed **eye-contact / radio / permission-to-approach** before
  any pedestrian enters a plant zone; the operator confirms. **Never assume the driver has seen you.**
- **Visibility & detection** — manage **blind spots**: cameras/360 systems, **proximity detection**,
  good lighting for 24-7 work, clean mirrors/windows, **hi-vis** appropriate to the light conditions.
- **Speed & layout** — plant speed limits, clear sightlines at intersections, segregated stacking and
  travel lanes; manage the **wharf edge** and apron.

## 3. Suspended loads

- **No one under or in the path** of a suspended container/cargo; exclusion under the quay crane and
  along the load path.
- **The lift itself** — the quay/ship-to-shore crane, spreader, ground/rail and lift plan →
  `../../crane-specialist/`; the **slinging/rigging** of non-containerised or awkward loads →
  `../../lifting-rigging-specialist/`.
- **Spreader/twistlock integrity** — confirm the container is properly engaged before lifting; manage
  the risk of a dropped or **disengaged** container.

---

## 4. Lashing & securing (often at height)

- Lashing crews fit/remove **twistlocks, lashing rods and bars** to secure containers on the ship and in
  stacks — heavy, repetitive work **often at height** on container tops and lashing platforms.
- **Falls** are a major risk → fall protection, **lashing platforms/cages**, safe access, and well-
  maintained lashing gear → `../../working-at-height-specialist/`.
- **Manual handling/ergonomics** — twistlocks and lashing bars are heavy and awkward; mechanical aids,
  rotation and good design reduce musculoskeletal injury.

---

## 5. Reach stackers, straddles & forklifts (specifics)

- **Straddle carriers** — tall, narrow, fast, large blind spots; pedestrian exclusion is critical; manage
  stack stability and container handling.
- **Reach stackers / forklifts / empty handlers** — load-centre/stability and the **load-centre
  de-rating** apply → `../../forklift-specialist/`; container handling raises the centre of gravity.
- **Terminal tractors / trailers** — coupling, reversing and the plant–pedestrian interface.
- **Pre-start checks and maintenance** for all plant; only **competent, authorised operators**.

---

## 6. Critical questions to consider

- Are **people on the ground minimised**, and are **walkways, crossings, refuges and exclusion zones**
  defined and used?
- Is there a **positive permission/eye-contact** system before a pedestrian enters a plant zone, and are
  **blind spots** managed (cameras/proximity/lighting/hi-vis)?
- Is **no one under or in the path** of suspended containers, and is the **lift** controlled? (→ crane/
  lifting SMEs)
- Is **lashing-at-height** fall-protected, and the lashing gear sound?
- Are **reach stackers/straddles/forklifts** operated within stability/load-centre limits by **competent**
  operators, pre-start-checked?
