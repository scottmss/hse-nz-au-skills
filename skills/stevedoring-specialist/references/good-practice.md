# Stevedoring & port cargo-handling safety — good practice (NZ/AU)

Methodology reference for `stevedoring-specialist`. Grounded in the **AU model Code of Practice
_Managing risks in stevedoring_** and **NZ HSWA 2015 + WorkSafe port-sector / Maritime NZ** guidance.
Government/regulator guidance is summarised with attribution; AS/NZS standards are **verify-pointers
only**. Confirm current editions.

> **Not legal advice.** A competent person validates controls against the actual terminal, vessel, plant
> and people. The **vessel** is also under **maritime** jurisdiction (Maritime NZ / AMSA) alongside
> WHS/HSWA.

---

## 1. Why stevedoring is its own sector

Stevedoring covers **loading/unloading a ship's cargo, stacking/storing on the wharf, and receival/
delivery within the terminal**. Its **serious-incident rate is many times the all-industry average** —
because a working wharf uniquely combines:

- **Very large mobile plant** (straddle carriers, reach stackers, quay cranes, forklifts) operating
  **continuously, 24-7, among people on foot**;
- **Suspended loads** (containers/cargo) overhead;
- A **moving ship** with falls, holds and the water; and
- A **multi-PCBU** environment — stevedore, port company, shipping line and labour-hire all on one wharf.

This skill owns the **port cargo-handling system** and the **plant–pedestrian interface**; it routes the
crane lift, falls, confined-space holds and over-water risks to their SMEs.

---

## 2. The killers (screen every operation against these)

1. **Mobile plant striking people** — the signature stevedoring fatality; huge blind spots on straddles/
   reach stackers → flagship `container-and-terminal-ops.md`.
2. **Falls** — from containers, ship structures and lashing platforms, and **into open holds/hatches** →
   `../../working-at-height-specialist/`.
3. **Suspended loads** — dropped/swinging containers and cargo → `../../crane-specialist/` +
   `../../lifting-rigging-specialist/`.
4. **The ship interface** — gangway access, **holds as confined spaces**, **falling into the water** →
   `../../confined-space-specialist/` + `../../water-safety-specialist/`.
5. **Crush & engulfment** — between cargo/plant/structures; **bulk cargo** engulfment and dust.

---

## 3. The central control — separate plant from people

The terminal's defining hazard is **large plant working among workers**, so separation is the spine of
every safe system here (detail in `container-and-terminal-ops.md`):

- **Exclusion zones** around operating plant and **under suspended loads**; **safe zones/refuges** and
  **designated walkways**; keep people out of the operating area where possible.
- **Positive communication** — agreed permission/eye-contact before a pedestrian enters a plant zone;
  no assumptions that the driver has seen you.
- **Visibility/detection** — manage **blind spots** (cameras, proximity detection, lighting, hi-vis).

---

## 4. The ship interface

- **Access** — safe, secured, **tide-adjusted gangways**; safe transfer to/from the vessel.
- **Vessel inspection before work** — confirm the ship's **cargo gear, accesses, hatches and lighting**
  are safe; defects stop work.
- **Holds & enclosed spaces** — treat ship **holds and enclosed spaces as confined spaces** (atmosphere
  testing, ventilation, standby, rescue) → `../../confined-space-specialist/`; **guard open hatches**
  against falls in.
- **Over water** — drowning risk at the ship/quay edge (PFDs, edge protection, rescue) →
  `../../water-safety-specialist/`.

---

## 5. Lashing, securing & suspended loads

- **Lashing/securing** containers (twistlocks, lashing rods/bars) is heavy, repetitive and often **at
  height** on the ship or stacks — fall protection, platforms and good systems →
  `../../working-at-height-specialist/`; manual-handling/ergonomics also apply.
- **Suspended loads** — no one **under or in the path**; the **quay-crane/spreader lift, ground and lift
  plan** → `../../crane-specialist/`, the **slinging/rigging** → `../../lifting-rigging-specialist/`.

---

## 6. Fatigue & the multi-PCBU wharf

- **Fatigue** — 24-7 operations, night shift and irregular ship arrivals; manage as a hazard (rosters,
  rest, the ability to stop).
- **Consult, cooperate & coordinate** across the **stevedore, port company, shipping line and labour-
  hire** — overlapping duties on a shared, fast-changing workplace → `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` /
  `../../safework-au-specialist/`. Induct and verify competency, including **labour-hire/casual** crews.

---

## 7. Critical questions to consider

- Are **mobile plant and people separated** — exclusion zones, walkways/refuges, positive permission,
  blind-spot controls? (→ flagship)
- Is **ship access** safe and tide-adjusted, are **holds treated as confined spaces**, and are **open
  hatches** guarded against falls in?
- Is the **over-water/drowning** risk controlled at the edge?
- Are **suspended loads** controlled (no one under/in path) and **lashing-at-height** fall-protected?
- Is **fatigue** managed, and are the **multiple PCBUs** consulting/cooperating/coordinating, with
  competent (incl. labour-hire) crews?
- Could an event be **notifiable** (WHS/HSWA) and is the **vessel** a Maritime NZ / AMSA matter?
