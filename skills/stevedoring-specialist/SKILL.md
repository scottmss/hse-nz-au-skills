# Stevedoring Specialist (port cargo-handling SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **stevedoring and port cargo-handling** — loading and
unloading ships, the **container terminal**, and the wharf. Stevedoring has a **serious-injury rate
many times the all-industry average**: huge **mobile plant working among people**, **suspended loads**,
**falls** on and into ships, and the **ship/wharf interface**. Owns the port cargo-handling system and
routes the physical hazards to the relevant SME.

## When to use

- **Container & terminal mobile-plant operations** — **straddle carriers, reach stackers, quay
  (ship-to-shore) cranes, forklifts** working among workers; the plant–pedestrian interface (flagship —
  `references/container-and-terminal-ops.md`).
- **Lashing & securing** — twistlocks, lashing rods/bars, securing containers (often at height).
- **The ship interface** — gangways, working on/into **ship holds** (confined space, falls into holds),
  hatches, working **over water**.
- **Suspended loads & lifting** over the quay and ship.
- **Bulk cargo** — engulfment, dust, mobile plant.
- **Fatigue / 24-7 shift operations** and the multi-PCBU wharf.

## The stevedoring risk picture

A working wharf is one of the most hazardous workplaces — **continuous, 24-7, multi-PCBU**, mixing
**very large mobile plant** with **people on foot**, **suspended loads** overhead, and the **moving
ship**. The killers:

1. **Mobile plant striking people** — straddle carriers/reach stackers/forklifts have huge blind spots;
   pedestrian strike/run-over is the signature stevedoring fatality → flagship.
2. **Falls** — from containers, ship structures and lashing platforms, and **into open holds/hatches**.
3. **Suspended loads** — containers/cargo dropped or swinging from quay cranes.
4. **The ship interface** — gangways, holds (**confined space**), and **falling into the water**.
5. **Crush** between cargo, plant and structures; **bulk-cargo engulfment**.

## Flagship — container & terminal mobile-plant operations

The terminal mixes **straddle carriers, reach stackers, quay cranes and forklifts** with **people on
foot** — separation is the central control:

- **Separate plant from people** — exclusion zones around operating plant and under suspended loads;
  designated **walkways and refuges**; minimise people on the ground in the operating area; positive
  communication and permission systems between drivers and pedestrians.
- **Visibility & detection** — manage the **blind spots** on straddles/reach stackers (cameras,
  proximity detection, lighting, hi-vis); agreed eye-contact/permission before anyone approaches.
- **Lashing/securing** is often **at height** on containers and ships — fall protection, platforms, and
  good twistlock/lashing systems → `../working-at-height-specialist/`.
- **Suspended loads** — no one under or in the path; the quay-crane/spreader lift →
  `../crane-specialist/` + `../lifting-rigging-specialist/`.

Full detail in `references/container-and-terminal-ops.md`.

## The ship interface (owned with hand-offs)

- **Gangways/access** — safe, secured, tide-adjusted access to the vessel.
- **Holds & enclosed spaces** — treat ship **holds and enclosed spaces as confined spaces** (atmosphere,
  access, rescue) → `../confined-space-specialist/`; guard **open hatches** against falls in.
- **Over water** — drowning risk at the ship/quay edge → `../water-safety-specialist/`.
- **Vessel inspection** — confirm the ship's cargo gear, accesses and hatches are safe before work; the
  **ship is also under Maritime jurisdiction** (NZ: Maritime NZ; AU: AMSA) alongside WHS/HSWA.

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction** (NZ/AU) and the operation (container/bulk/RoRo; ship-side vs quay-side).
2. **Separate plant from people** — the central terminal control (flagship).
3. **Control the ship interface** — access, holds (confined space), open hatches, over-water.
4. **Control suspended loads, lashing-at-height and crush** — route to crane/lifting/height SMEs.
5. **Manage fatigue and the multi-PCBU wharf** — consult/cooperate/coordinate across stevedore, port,
   shipping line and labour-hire.
6. **State residual obligations** — each PCBU keeps its duty; competency and a safe-system-of-work are
   required. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**AU:** the model **Code of Practice _Managing risks in stevedoring_** (adopted by the states) — the
primary anchor; plus WHS duties and **AMSA / Marine Order** jurisdiction for the vessel. **NZ:** **HSWA
2015** with **WorkSafe port-sector** guidance and the **post-2022 port-safety reform** (industry
leadership after port fatalities), and **Maritime NZ** for the ship/vessel interface. Jurisdiction
variations (Victoria OHS Act, WA) → `../safework-au-specialist/`. Confirm current editions via
`../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

task analysis (`../task-analysis-author/`) or SOP (`../sop-author/`). A draft for a competent person to
validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice stevedoring guidance — **not legal advice and not a substitute for a
competent person or the current stevedoring Code, WHS/HSWA and maritime requirements**, which change.
Validate every control (plant–pedestrian separation, ship access, hold entry, lashing and lifting)
against the actual terminal, vessel, plant and people before relying on it.
