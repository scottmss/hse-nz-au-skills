# Mining & quarrying — good practice, principal hazards & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, principal hazards, competency and critical questions for mining,
quarrying and extractives. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from the NZ HSW (Mining Operations
and Quarrying Operations) Regulations 2016 + WorkSafe extractives guidance and the Australian
state-based mining safety regimes. **Not legal advice**; the regime, plans and competencies differ by
jurisdiction and change — treat references as verify-pointers and confirm against the current law and a
competent person.

> The regulatory regime, statutory roles and the AU state matrix live in `regulatory-regimes.md`. This
> file is the **risk method**: the principal hazards and how good operations control them.

## How mining safety is managed — principal hazards + a management system

Beyond the general hierarchy of controls, the extractives regimes require the operator to:

1. **Run a documented health & safety management system** for the operation.
2. **Identify its principal hazards** — those with the potential to cause **multiple fatalities** — and
   manage each through a **principal hazard management plan**: *identify → analyse/assess → control →
   monitor & verify*, with standard operating procedures and trigger-action responses.
3. **Appoint competent statutory positions** and keep the workforce competent (see `regulatory-regimes.md`).

## The principal hazards (and good-practice control)

### Ground or strata failure
- Collapse, rockfall, **highwall/batter failure**, pillar failure, subsidence, and **rock/coal/gas
  bursts**. The leading cause of mining fatalities.
- Controls: **geotechnical assessment and design**; ground support/reinforcement (bolts, mesh, shotcrete);
  designed **batter/bench angles** and highwall management; pillar design; **keep people out of
  unsupported ground**; monitoring (slope radar, extensometers, convergence); scaling; exclusion zones
  after blasting/rain.

### Inrush
- Sudden ingress of **water, gas, slurry, or other material** into the workings.
- Controls: **know what's behind and above** (old/abandoned workings, aquifers, surface water bodies,
  filled voids); maintain **barrier/standoff distances**; **drill ahead** to probe; control surface water
  and tailings proximity.

### Ventilation, gas & spontaneous combustion
- **Methane (firedamp) explosion**, **oxygen deficiency/enrichment**, diesel exhaust, and **spontaneous
  combustion** of coal.
- Controls: designed **ventilation circuit** with adequate quantity/quality; **gas monitoring** (fixed,
  personal, tube-bundle/real-time); strict **ignition-source control** in potentially explosive
  atmospheres; **spon-com** detection (CO trends) and **inertisation**; gas drainage in gassy coal.

### Explosives & blasting
- Storage, transport, handling, **shotfiring**, **misfires**, post-blast **fume**, and **flyrock**.
- Controls: secure licensed storage; **competent shotfirer**; blast design and **exclusion zones**;
  positive accounting of charges; **misfire procedures**; controlled **re-entry** after fume clears.

### Mine mobile plant & haul roads
- Large **haul trucks**, loaders, dozers, graders — **vehicle interaction**, **runaway**, and
  **rollover**; light-vehicle/heavy-vehicle conflict; operator **fatigue** on long shifts.
- Controls: **separation** and positive communication; **haul-road design** (width, grade, windrows,
  drainage, sight lines); **proximity-detection**; park-up/brake/chock procedures; fatigue management.
  Scale-specific detail routes to `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.

### Airborne dust & occupational lung disease
- **Respirable coal dust** (coal workers' pneumoconiosis — "black lung"), **respirable crystalline
  silica** (silicosis), **diesel particulate matter**, plus noise and whole-body vibration.
- Controls: suppression (water, ventilation, enclosed cabs with filtration) **at source**; **real-time
  and personal exposure monitoring** against the exposure standard; **respiratory health surveillance**
  for exposed workers (the re-emergence of black lung shows monitoring must be real). Exposure-control
  method via `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.

### Tailings storage facilities (TSF)
- **Dam/impoundment failure** — a community-scale, multiple-fatality, environmental catastrophe
  (Brumadinho, Mount Polley).
- Controls: **competent geotechnical design and construction**, a **TSF management/dam-safety system**,
  instrumentation and monitoring, an emergency action plan, and independent review.

### Emergency management, self-escape & rescue
- The **Pike River** lesson: people must be able to **get out or be reached**.
- Controls: **second means of egress**, **self-rescuers**, **refuge chambers**, gas-monitoring for
  re-entry, **mines-rescue** capability, and **rehearsed** escape and rescue.

## Competency & statutory roles

- **Appoint the statutory positions** the regime requires and verify **certificates of competence** —
  NZ: **Site Senior Executive** + CoC for every safety-critical role (Mining Board of Examiners);
  AU: the positions and tickets the **state** regime defines. See `regulatory-regimes.md`.
- Workers and **contractors** trained, inducted and competent for the operation and its principal
  hazards; competency current and recorded.

## Critical questions to consider

- Which **regime** applies — NZ 2016 Regs, or **which Australian state**? Surface or underground? Coal
  or metalliferous?
- Are the **statutory positions appointed** and the **certificates of competence** current?
- Is there a **health & safety management system**, and does **each principal hazard have a management
  plan** with monitoring and trigger-action responses?
- **Ground:** is unsupported ground excluded and the highwall/strata monitored?
- **Gas/ventilation:** is the ventilation designed and gas monitored, with ignition control (and
  spon-com management in coal)?
- **Inrush:** is what lies behind/above the workings known, with barriers and probe-drilling?
- **Mobile plant:** are haul roads designed and people/vehicles separated?
- **Dust/health:** is exposure monitored and is **respiratory health surveillance** in place?
- **Emergency:** second egress, self-rescuers, refuge, mines-rescue — **rehearsed**?
