# Mining & quarrying — the regulatory regimes (NZ + AU state by state)

The defining feature of extractives safety is that it sits under a **bespoke regime** — separate law, a
**specialist regulator**, and **mandated statutory roles and certificates of competence** — not just the
baseline HSWA/WHS duty. This is also where **NZ and Australia diverge most**: NZ has one national
regime; **Australia is not harmonised** and varies by state.

> **Not legal advice.** Every Act, regulation, regulator, statutory position and certificate below is a
> **verify-pointer** — confirm the current instrument and its detail before relying on it. Duty/notifiable
> detail → `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../../safework-au-specialist/`.
>
> **Sources last verified: 2026-06-30.**

## New Zealand — one national regime

- **Law:** the **Health and Safety at Work (Mining Operations and Quarrying Operations) Regulations 2016**
  (in force 4 April 2016), sitting under **HSWA 2015**. It is the Government's response to the **Royal
  Commission on the Pike River Coal Mine Tragedy**.
- **Regulator:** **WorkSafe NZ** (extractives / high-hazards), with dedicated inspectors.
- **Statutory appointment:** the operator must appoint a **Site Senior Executive (SSE)** with overall
  responsibility for the operation's health & safety.
- **Certificates of Competence (CoC):** **everyone in a safety-critical role must hold a current CoC**
  (e.g. SSE, underground mine manager, underground deputy, ventilation, winding-engine, quarry/alluvial
  managers). Issued by the **NZ Mining Board of Examiners**; **WorkSafe sets the competencies** (via
  Safe Work Instruments), with **continuing professional development** requirements.
- **Management system:** a documented **health & safety management system**, with **principal hazard
  management** for the operation's principal hazards, emergency management, and worker participation.
- **Graduated requirements:** the regime **scales with risk** — underground mines (most prescriptive),
  then higher-risk **quarries/alluvial/tunnels**, then **lower-risk quarries** (proportionate). Confirm
  which tier an operation falls into.

## Australia — state by state (NOT harmonised)

Mining is **excluded from or supplemented well beyond** the model WHS. Establish the **state first** —
the Act, regulator, statutory positions and certificates all differ. Common to all: a **safety (and
health) management system** and **Principal Hazard Management Plans (PHMPs)**.

| State | Principal mining safety law | Regulator | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Queensland** | **Coal Mining Safety and Health Act 1999** + Regulation 2017 (coal); **Mining and Quarrying Safety and Health Act 1999** + Regulation 2017 (mineral mines & quarries) | **Resources Safety & Health Queensland (RSHQ)** | Standalone Acts, **separate from the WHS Act**. SHMS + PHMPs; statutory positions (Site Senior Executive, underground mine manager, ventilation officer). See the Resources Safety and Health Legislation Amendment Act 2024. |
| **NSW** | **WHS (Mines and Petroleum Sites) Act 2013** + **WHS (Mines and Petroleum Sites) Regulation 2022** | **NSW Resources Regulator** | **Supplements** the general WHS Act 2011. Mine operator registration; safety management system; PHMPs / principal control plans (incl. ground/strata, inrush, etc.). |
| **WA** | **WHS (Mines) Regulations 2022** under the **WHS Act 2020** | **DEMIRS** (Energy, Mines, Industry Regulation & Safety) / WorkSafe WA | Replaced the old **Mines Safety and Inspection Act 1994** (from 31 March 2022). Requires a **Mining Safety Management System (MSMS)**. |
| **Victoria** | **OHS Act 2004** + Mineral Resources (Sustainable Development) Act | **WorkSafe Victoria** + Earth Resources Regulation | Victoria does **not** use the model WHS at all. |
| **SA / Tas / NT / ACT** | Model WHS with mining provisions / own arrangements | State regulator (e.g. SA, WorkSafe Tasmania, NT WorkSafe) | Confirm the specific instrument and regulator for the state. |
