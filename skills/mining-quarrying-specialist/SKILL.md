# Mining & Quarrying Specialist (extractives sector SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **health & safety in mining, quarrying and
extractives** — surface and underground. Mining is a **high-consequence sector with its own dedicated
safety regime** that sits *separate from* the baseline HSWA/WHS duties, its own **statutory roles and
certificates of competence**, and a defined set of **principal hazards** that can kill many people at
once (the Pike River and Brumadinho lessons). Owns the sector regime, the principal hazards and the
competency system; routes generic hazards to the relevant SME.

## When to use

- Any **mine, quarry, alluvial or extractives** operation — surface (open cut/pit, quarry) or
  **underground** (coal or metalliferous).
- The **principal hazards** — ground/strata failure, inrush, gas/ventilation/spontaneous combustion,
  explosives & blasting, mine mobile plant & haul roads, airborne dust & lung disease, tailings storage
  (flagship — `references/good-practice.md`).
- The **sector regulatory regime** and **statutory competency** — who must be appointed, what
  certificates of competence are required (flagship — `references/regulatory-regimes.md`).
- Building or reviewing the **health & safety management system / principal hazard management plans**.

## When NOT to use

- The **physical/specific hazard detail** belongs to its SME (this skill routes to them, at mining scale):
  - **Surface excavation / batter & trench collapse off the mine** → `../excavation-specialist/`;
    **underground voids/ore passes as confined spaces** → `../confined-space-specialist/`.
  - **The dust/diesel/chemical exposure method, SDS, silica** → `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.
  - **Haul trucks, loaders, dozers & vehicle interaction** → `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`;
    **processing-plant guarding & isolation/LOTO** → `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
  - **Electrical / arc flash** → `../electrical-energy-specialist/`; **highwall/conveyor falls** →
    `../working-at-height-specialist/`; **settling ponds / water** → `../water-safety-specialist/`.
- **Who holds the duty / notifiable events** → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`
  (but note: mining safety is regulated under a **separate regime and regulator** — see below).
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## The mining risk picture

Extractives concentrate **catastrophic, multiple-fatality potential** with **chronic occupational
disease**:

1. **Catastrophic / principal hazards** — events that can kill several people at once: **ground or
   strata failure** (collapse, rockfall, highwall/pillar failure, subsidence, rock/coal/gas bursts),
   **inrush** (sudden water/gas/material ingress), **gas & ventilation** (methane explosion, oxygen
   deficiency, **spontaneous combustion** in coal), **explosives & blasting**, **mine mobile plant**
   (large haul trucks on haul roads), and **tailings-dam failure**.
2. **Chronic disease** — **respirable coal dust** (coal workers' pneumoconiosis / "black lung"),
   **respirable crystalline silica** (silicosis), **diesel particulate**, noise, whole-body vibration.

The defining sector feature is that these are managed as **principal hazards** under a **bespoke legal
regime** with **statutory roles**, not just under the general duty.

## The sector regulatory regime (the defining feature)

Mining safety is **carved out of, or layered on top of, baseline HSWA/WHS** — a separate body of law,
a specialist regulator, and mandated competency. This is where NZ and AU diverge most:

- **NZ — one national regime:** the **HSW (Mining Operations and Quarrying Operations) Regulations 2016**
  (the Government's response to the **Pike River** Royal Commission). The operator must appoint a **Site
  Senior Executive (SSE)**, run a **health & safety management system** with **principal hazard
  management** for the operation's principal hazards, and ensure **everyone in a safety-critical role
  holds a current Certificate of Competence** (issued by the **NZ Mining Board of Examiners**; WorkSafe
  sets the competencies). A **graduated** system scales the requirements across mines, higher-risk
  quarries/alluvial mines, and lower-risk quarries.
- **AU — state by state, NOT harmonised:** mining is **excluded from or supplemented beyond** the model
  WHS. **Queensland** has standalone Acts (Coal Mining Safety and Health Act 1999; Mining and Quarrying
  Safety and Health Act 1999) under **RSHQ**; **NSW** has the WHS (Mines and Petroleum Sites) Act 2013 +
  Regulation 2022 under the **Resources Regulator**; **WA** has the WHS (Mines) Regulations 2022 under
  the WHS Act 2020. All require a **safety (and health) management system** and **Principal Hazard
  Management Plans (PHMPs)**, with **statutory positions** (e.g. site senior executive, mine manager,
  ventilation officer) — but the Act, regulator, positions and certificates **differ by state**. Always
  confirm the state. Detail and the state matrix in `references/regulatory-regimes.md`.

## Principal hazards & core controls (owned here)

Manage each **principal hazard** with a documented plan (identify → analyse → control → verify), in
addition to the general hierarchy. Summary here; detail in `references/good-practice.md`:

- **Ground / strata control** — geotechnical design, support/reinforcement, highwall/batter angles,
  pillar design, exclusion of people from unsupported ground, monitoring (radar, extensometers).
- **Inrush** — know what's behind/above the workings (old workings, aquifers, surface water); barriers,
  drilling-ahead, standoff distances.
- **Ventilation, gas & spontaneous combustion** — adequate ventilation, gas monitoring/tube bundles,
  ignition control in explosive atmospheres, spon-com detection and inertisation (coal).
- **Explosives & blasting** — secure storage, competent shotfirer, exclusion zones, misfire procedures,
  post-blast fume re-entry.
- **Mine mobile plant & haul roads** — separation, haul-road design, fatigue management, proximity
  detection, park-up/runaway controls → scale-specific, routes to `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.
- **Airborne dust & lung disease** — suppression and ventilation at source, real-time + personal
  monitoring against the exposure standard, **respiratory health surveillance** → exposure method via
  `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.
- **Tailings storage facilities** — competent engineering, the designer's/operator's dam-safety
  management, monitoring; failure is a community-scale event.
- **Emergency management, self-escape & rescue** — second egress, **self-rescuers**, refuge chambers,
  **mines rescue** capability, and rehearsed escape — the core Pike River lesson.

## Competency & statutory roles

- **Appoint the statutory positions** the regime requires and hold the **certificates of competence** —
  **NZ:** SSE + CoC for every safety-critical role (Mining Board of Examiners). **AU:** the statutory
  positions and tickets defined by the **relevant state** regime.
- Workers trained, inducted and **competent** for the operation and its principal hazards; contractors
  brought into the management system.
- Confirm the certificate/competency system and who may do high-risk tasks via
  `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction and regime** — NZ (2016 Regs) or **which Australian state**? Surface or
   underground? Coal or metalliferous? This decides the law, regulator and statutory roles.
2. **Confirm the statutory appointments & competencies** (SSE / mine manager / ventilation officer; CoCs).
3. **Identify the principal hazards** for this operation and check each has a **management plan**.
4. **Work the principal-hazard controls** (ground, inrush, gas/ventilation, explosives, mobile plant,
   dust/health, tailings, emergency/self-escape).
5. **Route the generic hazards** to their SME (excavation, confined space, substances/dust method,
   mobile plant, machinery, electrical, height, water).
6. **State residual obligations** — the operator owes the duty under the mining regime; a documented
   management system, competent persons and emergency capability are mandatory. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**NZ:** **HSWA 2015** + the **HSW (Mining Operations and Quarrying Operations) Regulations 2016**,
regulated by **WorkSafe NZ** (extractives/high-hazards), with the **Mining Board of Examiners** for
certificates of competence. **AU:** **not harmonised** — a **state-based** regime: **Qld** (Coal Mining
Safety and Health Act 1999 / Mining and Quarrying Safety and Health Act 1999, **RSHQ**), **NSW** (WHS
(Mines and Petroleum Sites) Act 2013 + Reg 2022, **Resources Regulator**), **WA** (WHS (Mines) Regs 2022,
WHS Act 2020) and others. Always establish the **state** first. Detail and verify-pointers in
`references/regulatory-regimes.md`; confirm current editions via `../hse-advisor/` /
`../safework-au-specialist/`.

(`../critical-risk-manager/`), task analysis (`../task-analysis-author/`) or SOP (`../sop-author/`). A

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice extractives-safety guidance — **not legal advice and not a substitute
for a competent person or the current mining/quarrying safety law, which is a separate regime that
differs between NZ and each Australian state**. Mining principal hazards can cause multiple fatalities;
geotechnical, ventilation, explosives, tailings and emergency-management controls require **competent
specialist engineering**. Validate every appointment, control and management plan against the actual
operation and the current regime before relying on it.
