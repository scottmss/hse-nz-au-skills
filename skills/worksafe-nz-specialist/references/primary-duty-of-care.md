# Primary duty of care (HSWA 2015 s 36)

The central duty of the Act — what a PCBU must do, and for whom. Based on WorkSafe NZ guidance and
HSWA 2015. **Not legal advice**; section numbers are verify-pointers — confirm the current text.

## The duty (s 36)

Every **PCBU** has a **primary duty of care**: it must ensure, **so far as is reasonably practicable
(SFAIRP)**, the health and safety of workers and that others are not put at risk by its work. It is
the duty against which most enforcement is framed, and it **cannot be transferred or contracted out**.

The duty has **two limbs**:

1. **Workers (s 36(1))** — ensure SFAIRP the health and safety of:
   - workers the PCBU **employs or engages**, while they are at work in the business; and
   - workers whose work is **influenced or directed** by the PCBU, while they carry out that work
     (e.g. contractors, labour-hire workers).
2. **Other persons (s 36(2))** — ensure SFAIRP that the health and safety of **other people is not put
   at risk** by the work of the business (e.g. visitors, members of the public, passers-by).

### Who is a "PCBU", "worker", "workplace"?

- **PCBU (s 17)** — a person conducting a business or undertaking; can be an individual (e.g. a
  self-employed tradesperson) or an organisation (company, partnership, etc.). Not a worker acting
  only as a worker, and not (usually) an officer in their personal capacity.
- **Worker (s 19)** — broadly defined: an individual who carries out work **in any capacity** for a
  PCBU, including an **employee, contractor or subcontractor (and their employees), labour-hire
  employee, outworker/homeworker, apprentice or trainee, person on work experience, and a volunteer
  worker.**
- **Workplace (s 20)** — a place where work is carried out, or where a worker goes or is likely to be
  while at work.

## The specific aspects of the duty (s 36(3))

Without limiting the two limbs, the PCBU must ensure **so far as is reasonably practicable**:

- (a) a **work environment** without risks to health and safety;
- (b) **safe plant and structures**;
- (c) **safe systems of work**;
- (d) the **safe use, handling and storage** of plant, substances and structures;
- (e) **adequate facilities** for the welfare of workers at work (and access to them);
- (f) the **information, training, instruction or supervision** necessary to protect everyone from
  risks arising from the work; and
- (g) that the **health of workers and the conditions at the workplace are monitored** to prevent
  injury or illness.

## The standard — "so far as is reasonably practicable"

- **Reasonably practicable (s 22)** — weigh likelihood and severity of harm against what is known
  about the risk and the availability, suitability and cost of controls; **cost only where grossly
  disproportionate**. (Five-question framing in `managing-risk.md` / `overlapping-duties.md`.)
- **Management of risks (s 30)** — **eliminate** SFAIRP; if not, **minimise** SFAIRP via the control
  hierarchy. See `managing-risk.md`.

The duty is about doing what is reasonable in the circumstances — not a guarantee against all harm,
but a positive, ongoing obligation that a PCBU must be able to **demonstrate** it has met.

## Other PCBU duties (beyond the primary duty)

A PCBU may hold further, more specific duties that can overlap with the primary duty:

- **Manages or controls a workplace (s 37)** — ensure SFAIRP the workplace, the means of entering and
  exiting it, and anything arising from it are without risks to **anyone** (not just its own workers).
- **Manages or controls fixtures, fittings or plant at a workplace (s 38)**.
- **Upstream duties (ss 39–43)** — those who **design, manufacture, import, supply, or
  install/construct/commission** plant, substances or structures used at work owe duties to ensure
  they are without risks SFAIRP. (Confirm exact section per role.)

Where more than one PCBU owes a duty for the same matter, the **overlapping-duties** rules apply —
they must consult, cooperate and coordinate (**s 34**). See `overlapping-duties.md`.

## Officers and workers

- **Officers** do not hold the primary duty — they hold a **personal due-diligence duty (s 44)** to
  ensure the PCBU meets *its* duties. For governance depth → `../../officer-governance-advisor/`.
- **Workers (s 45)** and **other persons (s 46)** owe their own (lesser) duties to take reasonable
  care and follow reasonable instructions.

## How to use this

- Given a scenario, identify the PCBU(s) and confirm **who the duty is owed to** (workers under both
  limbs, plus other persons). Then test the **s 36(3) aspects** as a checklist of what "ensuring H&S
  SFAIRP" concretely requires here, and frame the **reasonably-practicable** judgement for each
  significant risk.
- For multiple PCBUs → `overlapping-duties.md`. To build/score the controls → `../../critical-risk-manager/`.
