# Notifiable events (HSWA 2015 ss 23–25, 55–57)

When a serious work-related event happens, a PCBU has duties to **notify WorkSafe**, **preserve the
site**, and **keep records**. Based on WorkSafe NZ's "What events need to be notified?" guidance and
HSWA 2015. **Not legal advice** — section numbers and lists are verify-pointers; confirm against the
current WorkSafe guidance and the Act, which are the authority for a real event.

## What is a notifiable event? (s 25)

A **notifiable event** is one of the following, arising from work:

- a **death**;
- a **notifiable injury or illness** (s 23); or
- a **notifiable incident** (s 24).

The duty to notify is **not** limited to events involving workers — it covers any person, where the
event arises out of the conduct of the business or undertaking.

## Notifiable injury or illness (s 23)

A specified serious work-related injury or illness. As a rule of thumb, anything that **requires (or
would usually require) admission to hospital for immediate treatment** is notifiable. The specified
list includes:

- any injury or illness requiring the person to be **admitted to hospital as an inpatient** for
  immediate treatment;
- **amputation** of any body part;
- **serious head injury**;
- **serious eye injury**;
- **serious burn**;
- **separation of skin from underlying tissue** (e.g. **degloving or scalping**);
- **spinal injury**;
- **loss of a bodily function**;
- **serious lacerations**;
- injury or illness requiring, or that would usually require, **medical treatment within 48 hours of
  exposure to a substance**;
- **contracting a serious infection** (including occupational zoonoses) to which carrying out work is
  a **significant contributing factor**.

(Each of the injury types above is qualified by "that requires immediate treatment **other than
first aid**".)

## Notifiable incident (s 24)

An **unplanned or uncontrolled** incident that **exposes any person to a serious risk** to health or
safety arising from an immediate or imminent exposure to, among others:

- an **escape, spillage or leakage** of a substance;
- an **implosion, explosion or fire**;
- an **escape of gas or steam**;
- an **escape of a pressurised substance**;
- an **electric shock** from a source with potential for lethal harm;
- the **fall or release from height** of any plant, substance or thing;
- the **damage to, collapse, overturning, failure or malfunction of plant** (where required to be
  authorised for use);
- the **collapse or partial collapse of a structure**;
- the **collapse or failure of an excavation**, or of any shoring supporting it;
- the **inrush of water, mud or gas** in workings of an underground excavation or tunnel;
- the **interruption of the main system of ventilation** in an underground excavation or tunnel;
- the **collision, capsize, sinking, or taking on of water** of a vessel (and related events).

> A notifiable incident is about a **serious-risk near-miss as much as an actual injury** — the test
> is the seriousness of the risk exposed, not whether anyone was hurt.

## The PCBU's duties when a notifiable event occurs

1. **Notify WorkSafe as soon as possible (s 56).** For a death or serious injury/illness, **notify
   immediately by phone — 0800 030 040 (24/7).** If WorkSafe requests, follow up with **written
   notice within 48 hours**.
2. **Preserve the site (s 55).** Take all reasonable steps to ensure the site where the event
   occurred is **not disturbed** until a WorkSafe inspector directs otherwise. Exceptions: to help an
   injured person, remove a deceased person, make the site safe, or where Police/an inspector
   authorise it.
3. **Keep records (s 57).** Keep a record of each notifiable event for **at least 5 years** from the
   date WorkSafe is notified.

Failing these duties is an offence. More than one PCBU may share the duty for the same event — they
should **agree who notifies** (see `overlapping-duties.md`).

## How to use this

- **Triage**: given an event, work through death → s 23 injury/illness list → s 24 incident list to
  give a **provisional** notifiable / not-notifiable view, and flag the three duties (notify /
  preserve / record).
- Always tell the user to **confirm against current WorkSafe guidance** and, if in doubt, **notify** —
  it is better to over-notify than to miss a notifiable event. WorkSafe can advise on a borderline
  case via 0800 030 040.
