# Australian WHS jurisdictions — which law, which regulator

Australia is **not one WHS jurisdiction**. The model WHS law is enacted separately by each
state/territory and the Commonwealth, **Western Australia has its own numbering**, and **Victoria
uses a different statute entirely**. This reference is for **Step 0 — establishing which law and
regulator apply** before citing any duty. **Not legal advice**; confirm current detail with the
relevant regulator.

## The nine jurisdictions

| Jurisdiction | Enacting Act | Regulator | Framework |
|---|---|---|---|
| Commonwealth | WHS Act 2011 (Cth) | **Comcare** | Model WHS |
| New South Wales | WHS Act 2011 (NSW) | **SafeWork NSW** | Model WHS |
| Queensland | WHS Act 2011 (Qld) | **Workplace Health and Safety Queensland** (Office of Industrial Relations) | Model WHS |
| South Australia | WHS Act 2012 (SA) | **SafeWork SA** | Model WHS |
| Tasmania | WHS Act 2012 (Tas) | **WorkSafe Tasmania** | Model WHS |
| ACT | WHS Act 2011 (ACT) | **WorkSafe ACT** | Model WHS |
| Northern Territory | WHS (National Uniform Legislation) Act 2011 (NT) | **NT WorkSafe** | Model WHS |
| Western Australia | **WHS Act 2020 (WA)** | **WorkSafe WA** (DEMIRS) | Model WHS — **own numbering / modifications** |
| **Victoria** | **OHS Act 2004 (Vic)** | **WorkSafe Victoria** | **NOT model WHS** |

> The **Commonwealth** scheme (Comcare) covers Commonwealth agencies and corporations licensed to
> self-insure under the Comcare scheme — not all national businesses. Confirm coverage.

## Western Australia — same model, different numbering

WA adopted the model WHS law via its **Work Health and Safety Act 2020 (WA)** (commenced **31 March
2022**), replacing the old OSH Act. The **concepts match the model** (PCBU, officers, primary duty,
overlapping duties), **but the section numbers and some provisions differ** from the model Act and
from the eastern states. Do **not** assume a model section number is the enacted WA number — check the
WA Act.

## Victoria — a different statute

Victoria did **not** adopt the model WHS law. It runs the **Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004
(Vic)**, with different terminology and duties. Key differences to watch:

- It uses **"employer"** and **"self-employed person"**, not the WHS term **"PCBU"**.
- The **employer's primary duty** is at **OHS Act s 21** (to employees) and **s 23** (to other
  persons) — *not* model WHS s 19.
- There is **no identical positive "officer due diligence" duty**; officer liability runs through
  **s 144** (officers liable where a body-corporate offence is attributable to them).
- Consultation, incident notification, penalties and Codes of Practice (Compliance Codes) differ.

**Never apply model-WHS section numbers or Codes to a Victorian matter.** For anything beyond the
basics in Victoria, treat this skill as out of depth and recommend Victoria-specific advice.

## Codes of Practice

- Safe Work Australia develops **model Codes of Practice**; each jurisdiction **approves/adopts** them
  (Victoria has its own **Compliance Codes**).
- An approved Code is **admissible in proceedings** as evidence of what is known about a hazard/risk
  and what is reasonably practicable to control it. Following an approved Code is a strong way to show
  a duty was met, though duty holders may use other ways that achieve an equal or better standard.
- Always cite the **jurisdiction's approved Code**, not just the model Code.

## High-risk work licensing (HRWL)

Australia operates a **national High Risk Work Licence** system administered by each jurisdiction's
regulator, with **mutual recognition** across jurisdictions. Licence **classes** include cranes (e.g.
CN, CV, C2–C6, CO, CB, CT), dogging (DG), rigging (RB, RI, RA), scaffolding (SB, SI, SA), elevating
work platforms (WP, boom-type ≥ 11 m), forklifts (LF, LO), and pressure-equipment/reach-stacker
classes. This is an **Australian construct** — NZ does not use the same licence-class system. For
plant/competency depth → `high-risk-work-specialist`.

## Other things that vary by jurisdiction

- **Penalties** and offence categories (Category 1–3) — amounts differ and are periodically increased.
- **Industrial manslaughter** is now an offence in **most** Australian jurisdictions, but the elements,
  penalties and commencement **vary** — verify the current position in the jurisdiction.
- **Regulations and authorisations** (licences, registrations, high-risk work, hazardous substances)
  differ in detail.

## How to use this

1. Pin down the **jurisdiction** from the facts (where the work/workplace is; who the employer/PCBU
   is; whether the Commonwealth/Comcare scheme applies).
2. State the **Act and regulator**, and whether it is **model WHS** or **Victoria (OHS Act 2004)**.
3. If **WA**, flag that enacted section numbers differ from the model. If **Victoria**, switch to OHS
   Act framing and note the limits of this skill.
4. Then proceed to the relevant duty (`model-whs-duties.md`, `overlapping-duties-au.md`,
   `notifiable-incidents-au.md`), citing the **model section as a verify-pointer** and reminding the
   user to confirm the enacted provision.
