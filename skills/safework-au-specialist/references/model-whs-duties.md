# Model WHS duties (Australia)

The core duties under the **Safe Work Australia model WHS Act**, as the basis for each
state/territory and Commonwealth enactment. **Not legal advice.** Section numbers are **model-law
verify-pointers** — each jurisdiction enacts its own version, the **enacted numbers can differ**
(Western Australia notably), and **Victoria uses the OHS Act 2004 instead** (see
`jurisdiction-variations.md`). Always confirm the provision in the jurisdiction that applies.

## Who is who

- **PCBU (s 5)** — a person conducting a business or undertaking; an individual or an organisation.
  The primary duty holder.
- **Worker (s 7)** — broadly defined: anyone who carries out work **in any capacity** for a PCBU —
  employee, contractor/subcontractor (and their employees), labour-hire worker, outworker, apprentice
  or trainee, work-experience person, or volunteer.
- **Officer** — a person who makes, or participates in making, decisions affecting the whole or a
  substantial part of the business (drawing on the Corporations Act meaning). Holds the due-diligence
  duty (s 27), **not** the primary duty.

## PCBU — primary duty of care (s 19)

A PCBU must ensure, **so far as is reasonably practicable (SFAIRP)**:

- the **health and safety of workers** it engages, or whose work it influences or directs, while they
  are at work; and
- that the **health and safety of other persons is not put at risk** from work carried out by the
  business (e.g. visitors, the public).

Without limiting that, the PCBU must ensure SFAIRP (the model s 19(3) aspects, closely mirroring NZ
HSWA s 36(3)): a safe **work environment**; safe **plant and structures**; safe **systems of work**;
safe **use, handling and storage** of plant/substances/structures; adequate **facilities** for
welfare; necessary **information, training, instruction and supervision**; and **monitoring** of
worker health and workplace conditions to prevent injury or illness.

## Reasonably practicable (s 18)

What is reasonably able to be done, weighing all relevant matters:

1. the **likelihood** of the hazard or risk occurring;
2. the **degree of harm** that might result;
3. what the person **knows, or ought reasonably to know**, about the hazard/risk and ways to
   eliminate or minimise it;
4. the **availability and suitability** of ways to eliminate or minimise the risk; and
5. **after** assessing 1–4, the **cost**, including whether it is **grossly disproportionate** to the
   risk.

## Management of risks (s 17)

A duty holder must **eliminate** risks SFAIRP; and if that is not reasonably practicable, **minimise**
them SFAIRP. The WHS Regulations set out the **hierarchy of control** (eliminate → substitute →
isolate → engineering → administrative → PPE), mirroring the NZ approach in
`../../worksafe-nz-specialist/references/managing-risk.md`. To build and score controls →
`../../critical-risk-manager/`.

## Officers — due diligence (s 27)

If a PCBU has a duty, an **officer** must exercise **due diligence** to ensure the PCBU complies. It
is a **personal, positive duty**. The **six elements (s 27(5))** — closely aligned with NZ HSWA
s 44(4):

1. acquire and keep up-to-date **knowledge** of WHS matters;
2. understand the **operations** of the business and their **hazards and risks**;
3. ensure the PCBU has and uses **appropriate resources and processes** to eliminate or minimise
   risks;
4. ensure the PCBU has processes to **receive and consider information** on incidents, hazards and
   risks, and to **respond** in a timely way;
5. ensure the PCBU has and implements **processes for complying** with its duties; and
6. **verify** the resources and processes in 3–5 are in place and in use.

For governance practice (board reporting, self-assessment, induction) → `../../officer-governance-advisor/`.

## Workers (s 28) and other persons (s 29)

- **Workers** must take **reasonable care** for their own and others' safety, **comply** with
  reasonable instructions, and cooperate with reasonable policies.
- **Other persons at a workplace** (e.g. visitors) must take reasonable care and comply with
  reasonable instructions.

## Upstream duties (ss 22–26)

PCBUs who **design (s 22)**, **manufacture (s 23)**, **import (s 24)**, **supply (s 25)**, or
**install/construct/commission (s 26)** plant, substances or structures used at a workplace must
ensure, SFAIRP, that they are without risks to health and safety. These "upstream" duties put
responsibility on those best placed to design risk out before products reach the workplace.

## How NZ and the model law compare (orientation)

| Matter | Model WHS (AU) | HSWA 2015 (NZ) |
|---|---|---|
| PCBU defined | s 5 | s 17 |
| Worker defined | s 7 | s 19 |
| Reasonably practicable | s 18 | s 22 |
| Management of risks | s 17 | s 30 |
| Primary duty of care | s 19 | s 36 |
| Officer due diligence | s 27 (s 27(5)) | s 44 (s 44(4)) |
| Workers' duties | s 28 | s 45 |
| Other persons' duties | s 29 | s 46 |
| Overlapping duties (CCC) | s 46 | s 34 |
| Notifiable events/incidents | ss 35–39 | ss 23–25, 55–57 |

> The concepts align because NZ's HSWA was modelled on the same law — but the **numbers, penalties,
> regulations and Codes differ**, and Victoria sits outside this scheme entirely. Treat the table as
> orientation, not authority.
