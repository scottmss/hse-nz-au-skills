# SafeWork AU Specialist (WHS duties)

## Purpose

Reason correctly about **Australian WHS duties** — who owes which duty, the PCBU primary duty of
care, officer due diligence, overlapping duties, and notifiable incidents — **and pin down which
law and regulator apply**, because Australia is **not one jurisdiction**: the model WHS law is
enacted separately by each state/territory and the Commonwealth, and **Victoria does not use it at
all**. Grounded in the Safe Work Australia model WHS Act and each jurisdiction's enacting law.
**Not legal advice**; section numbers are model-law verify-pointers — confirm the *enacted* number
and text in the relevant jurisdiction.

## When to use

- Identifying **which Act and regulator** apply (state/territory/Commonwealth) — always do this first.
- Mapping **WHS duty holders** (PCBU, officer, worker, other person) in a scenario.
- Applying the **primary duty of care**, **officer due diligence**, **reasonably practicable**, or
  the **management-of-risks** duty under the model WHS Act.
- **Overlapping duties** — the duty to consult, cooperate and coordinate.
- A first read on whether something is a **notifiable incident**, and the notify/preserve duties.
- Pointers on **model Codes of Practice** and **high-risk work licensing (HRWL)**.

## When NOT to use

- **New Zealand** → `worksafe-nz-specialist`. NZ HSWA is close to the model law but **not identical**
  (and the section numbers differ).
- **Building or scoring controls** (bow tie, control assurance) → `critical-risk-manager`.
- **Officer/board governance** depth → `officer-governance-advisor` (the governance model is
  NZ-authored but transfers; the AU officer *legal* duty is model WHS s 27 / Vic differs).
- A definitive legal position, or any **Victorian** matter beyond the basics → a competent WHS lawyer
  in that jurisdiction.

## Step 0 — establish the jurisdiction (do this first)

Australia has **nine** WHS jurisdictions. **Confirm which one applies before citing any duty**, then
use `references/jurisdiction-variations.md`:

- **Model WHS law** (own enacting Act, broadly aligned): Commonwealth (**Comcare**), NSW, Queensland,
  South Australia, Tasmania, ACT, Northern Territory, and **Western Australia** (WHS Act 2020 — adopted
  2022, but with **its own numbering and some modifications**).
- **Not the model law: Victoria** — **OHS Act 2004** (WorkSafe Victoria), with different terminology,
  duties and section numbers (e.g. employer duty at OHS s 21). Do **not** apply model-WHS sections to
  a Victorian matter.

## The model WHS duty map (quick reference)

Model WHS Act section numbers (verify the **enacted** number per jurisdiction; **Victoria differs
entirely** — see above):

- **PCBU — primary duty of care (s 19).** Ensure H&S of workers (and others not put at risk) *so far
  as is reasonably practicable*. PCBU defined **s 5**, worker **s 7**. **See
  `references/model-whs-duties.md`.**
- **Reasonably practicable (s 18)** and **management of risks (s 17)** — eliminate, else minimise.
- **Officers — due diligence (s 27; six elements in s 27(5)).** Personal duty → governance depth via
  `officer-governance-advisor`.
- **Workers (s 28)** and **other persons (s 29)** — reasonable care, follow instructions.
- **Upstream duties (ss 22–26)** — designers, manufacturers, importers, suppliers, installers.
- **Overlapping duties — consult, cooperate and coordinate (s 46).** **See
  `references/overlapping-duties-au.md`.**
- **Notifiable incidents (ss 35–39)** — death, serious injury/illness, or dangerous incident; notify
  the regulator; preserve the site. **See `references/notifiable-incidents-au.md`.**

## Method

1. **Establish the jurisdiction** (Step 0). If New Zealand, route to `worksafe-nz-specialist`.
2. **Flag Victoria explicitly** if it applies — switch to OHS Act 2004 framing and note the limits of
   this skill there.
3. **Identify the duty holders** — every PCBU (and its primary duty), officers, workers, others.
4. **Apply the relevant duty** from `references/model-whs-duties.md`, citing the model section and
   reminding the user to confirm the enacted number.
5. **Test for overlapping duties** (shared workplace or contracting chain) →
   `references/overlapping-duties-au.md` (s 46, consult/cooperate/coordinate; influence-and-control).
6. **Notifiable-incident triage** if an event occurred → `references/notifiable-incidents-au.md`.
7. **Point to Codes of Practice / HRWL** where relevant (`references/jurisdiction-variations.md`).

## Jurisdiction note

**Australia only.** The model WHS law underpins NZ's HSWA (both drew on the same model), so the
*concepts* travel — but the **section numbers, penalties, regulations and Codes differ**, WA has its
own numbering, and **Victoria is a different statute**. Never transplant NZ or another state's section
numbers onto the matter at hand. Establish jurisdiction via `hse-advisor` if unsure.

## Output formats

- **Jurisdiction determination** — which Act, which regulator, model-WHS vs Victoria, key caveats.
- **WHS duty-holder map** — each party → PCBU/officer/worker/other; what they control; their duty
  (model section + "verify enacted number").
- **Overlapping-duties analysis** — who is best placed to control each shared risk (s 46).
- **Notifiable-incident view** — is it notifiable (ss 35–39), and the notify/preserve/record duties.

Every output is a **draft for a competent person to validate** against the enacted law and facts.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces analysis and structure — **not legal advice**. WHS duties depend on the specific
facts, the degree of influence and control, the **jurisdiction**, and the current enacted text of the
Act, Regulations and Codes. A competent WHS person or lawyer in the relevant jurisdiction must
validate any conclusion — especially for Victoria, which operates a different statute.
