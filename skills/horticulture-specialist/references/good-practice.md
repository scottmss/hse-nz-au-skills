# Horticulture safety — good practice (NZ/AU)

Methodology reference for `horticulture-specialist`. Grounded in **WorkSafe NZ horticulture / working-
at-height / overhead-line guidance** and the **AU WHS framework + state horticulture guidance** (e.g.
Agriculture Victoria / SafeWork). Government/regulator guidance is summarised with attribution; AS/NZS
standards are **copyright — verify-pointers only**. Some WorkSafe horticulture guidance has been
withdrawn/replaced — **confirm current** before relying on it.

> **Not legal advice.** A competent person validates controls against the actual orchard/packhouse,
> equipment, chemicals and people. The sibling pastoral SME is `../../agriculture-specialist/`.

---

## 1. The horticulture picture

Horticulture (orchards, vineyards, kiwifruit, pipfruit, market gardens, glasshouses) plus the
**packhouse and coolstore** is **high-volume, highly seasonal, and labour-intensive**. Its risk profile
differs from pastoral farming:

- **Work at height is routine** — orchard platforms (H/MEWPs), ladders, picking and pruning aloft —
  often **near overhead power lines**.
- **Intensive spray programmes** — agrichemicals applied repeatedly across a season.
- **A processing tail** — packhouses and coolstores with machinery, forklifts and **controlled-
  atmosphere (low-oxygen) rooms**.
- **A large seasonal/migrant workforce** (incl. RSE) — new, transient and sometimes non-English-speaking
  workers who are owed the same duty of care as permanent staff.

The horticulture **PCBU** owes the full primary duty: identify the risks, control them so far as is
reasonably practicable, ensure competency/supervision (including for seasonal workers), and keep a
simple **risk register / safety plan** people actually use.

## 2. The horticulture risks (screen every operation against these)

1. **Falls / MEWPs** — orchard H/MEWPs (often **self-built or modified — a known WorkSafe concern**),
   ladders, picking platforms; falls and overturns → `../../working-at-height-specialist/`. Detail in
   `orchard-and-packhouse.md`.
2. **Overhead power lines** — tall MEWPs/sprayers/bin-loaders contacting lines (electrocution) →
   `../../electrical-energy-specialist/`.
3. **Agrichemicals & spray drift** — mixing/applying, PPE, **re-entry intervals**, airblast off-target
   drift, bystander/community exposure → `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.
4. **Mobile plant** — narrow orchard/vineyard tractors, sprayers, harvesters, bin trailers, quads →
   `../../agriculture-specialist/` (vehicles) + `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.
5. **Packhouse machinery** — graders, conveyors, balers, palletisers (entanglement, crush) →
   `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`; **forklifts** → `../../forklift-specialist/`.
6. **Controlled-atmosphere coolstores** — **oxygen-depleted confined spaces** that can kill on entry;
   plus cold exposure → `../../confined-space-specialist/`.
7. **Seasonal / migrant / RSE workers** — vulnerable workers (see §3).
8. **Manual handling** — picking, pruning, bin/box lifting; repetitive strain.
9. **Health** — sun/UV, heat, fatigue (long seasonal hours), noise, dust/agrichemical exposure.
10. **Child & visitor safety** — orchard gate sales, family on site; keep children clear of vehicles,
    machinery, sprays and coolstores.

## 3. The seasonal & migrant workforce (a sector-defining control)

Seasonal, migrant and **RSE** workers are often new to the work, the country and the language, and are
**owed exactly the same duty of care** as permanent staff:

- **Induct everyone before they start** — hazards, controls, no-go areas, emergency and reporting — in a
  **language and form they understand** (interpreters, visuals, demonstrations).
- **Competency before plant/spray** — only trained, authorised workers operate MEWPs, tractors,
  forklifts or apply agrichemicals.
- **Supervision & buddy systems** for new/seasonal crews; clear, reachable supervisors.
- **Speak-up culture** — workers must be able to **stop and report** without fear; check understanding,
  don't assume it.
- **Welfare** — heat/sun, hydration, rest breaks, accommodation/transport where the PCBU influences it.

## 4. Baseline controls (all horticulture work)

- **Right equipment for the task** — purpose-built, certified MEWPs (not home-made); rated ladders;
  maintained mobile plant and packhouse machinery with guards in place.
- **Plan around overhead lines** — map them, set exclusion distances, use spotters (→ electrical SME).
- **Manage the spray programme** — labels, re-entry intervals, weather/drift, PPE, secure storage (→
  hazardous-substances SME).
- **Treat CA coolstores as confined spaces** — never enter an oxygen-controlled room without the confined-
  space controls (→ confined-space SME).
- **Competent, inducted, supervised people** — especially seasonal crews; manage fatigue.
- **A living risk register / safety plan** and simple pre-task thinking for non-routine jobs; report and
  learn from incidents and near-misses.

---

## 5. Critical questions to consider

- Is **work at height** done on a **purpose-built, fit-for-use MEWP/ladder** — not a self-built or
  modified platform? (→ height SME)
- Are MEWPs, sprayers and bin-loaders kept **clear of overhead lines** at the current approach distance?
  (→ electrical SME)
- Is the **spray programme** controlled — re-entry intervals, drift, PPE, storage? (→ haz-subs SME)
- Are **CA coolstores** treated as **confined spaces**, never entered unprotected? (→ confined-space SME)
- Are **packhouse machines guarded/isolated** and **forklifts separated from people**? (→ machinery/forklift SMEs)
- Are **seasonal/migrant/RSE workers** inducted in a language they understand, competent for their plant,
  and supervised?
- Could an event be **notifiable**? → `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../../safework-au-specialist/`.

## Sources & discipline

- **NZ:** WorkSafe **horticulture**, **working-at-height / mobile elevating work platform**, and
  **overhead power line** guidance (some older horticulture guidance has been withdrawn/replaced —
  confirm current). HSWA 2015 applies. Summarised with attribution.
- **AU:** WHS framework with the model Codes most relevant to horticulture — **_Managing the risk of
  falls_** + **elevating work platforms** guidance (orchard MEWPs), **_Confined spaces_** (CA
  coolstores), **_Managing risks of hazardous chemicals_** + **_Labelling..._** (spray), and
  **_Hazardous manual tasks_** — plus **state horticulture guidance** (e.g. Agriculture Victoria /
  SafeWork NSW) and the agvet-chemical control-of-use regime; jurisdiction variations →
  `../../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Specific-hazard standards live with the relevant SME (height, electrical, substances, confined space,
  machinery, forklift). Confirm current editions/guidance before relying on any figure or rule.
