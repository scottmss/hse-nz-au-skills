# Orchard/vineyard field work & packhouse/coolstore — good practice (NZ/AU)

Flagship reference for `horticulture-specialist`, covering the two sector-distinct operational areas:
**(A) field work** (orchard/vineyard — height, overhead lines, spray, mobile plant) and **(B) the
processing tail** (packhouse and coolstore). Routes each specific hazard to its SME. Grounded in
WorkSafe NZ / AU state guidance; standards are **verify-pointers** (copyright). Confirm current rules.

> **Not legal advice.** Validate against the actual site, equipment and chemicals. Approach distances,
> re-entry intervals and CA-room rules **change** — confirm with the regulator / the relevant SME.

---

## A. Orchard & vineyard field work

### A1. Work at height — orchard MEWPs (H/MEWPs) and ladders

- **Use purpose-built, certified equipment.** WorkSafe has repeatedly flagged **self-made or modified
  mobile elevating work platforms** in horticulture (and hops) — these must be **properly designed and
  constructed**, fit for use, and maintained. A home-built picking platform is a serious red flag.
- **Operate competently:** trained operators, pre-start checks, level/stable ground, rated load, no
  overreach, controls and emergency lowering understood.
- **Ladders & picking platforms** for lower work — the right type, footing and angle; minimise time on
  ladders where a MEWP is the better control. Full fall-protection method →
  `../../working-at-height-specialist/`.
- **Dropped objects** (tools, fruit bins) onto people below — exclusion below, secured loads.

### A2. Overhead power lines (a signature horticulture electrocution risk)

- Tall **MEWPs, sprayers, bin-loaders and tipping trailers** can contact overhead lines in an orchard.
- **Identify the lines first** and keep machinery, people and equipment **clear**. WorkSafe guidance for
  orchard MEWPs points to staying outside roughly **4 m of distribution lines and 6 m of transmission
  lines** — but **confirm the current minimum approach distances** (these vary by voltage and are set by
  **NZECP 34**) via `../../electrical-energy-specialist/`.
- Use spotters, route planning and physical markers; treat a downed line as live and keep clear.

### A3. Spraying & agrichemicals

- **Spray drift** can harm workers, bystanders, neighbouring crops and the community; airblast sprayers
  in high-density systems are prone to off-target drift.
- Controls: read and follow the **label**, manage **weather/wind**, calibrate equipment, observe
  **re-entry intervals** (no one back in the block until it's safe), correct **PPE**, and secure
  **storage/mixing**. The agvet-chemical control-of-use regime applies. Full method →
  `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.

### A4. Mobile plant

- **Narrow orchard/vineyard tractors** (higher rollover tendency on slopes/headlands), sprayers,
  harvesters and bin trailers — ROPS + seatbelt, safe hitching, no passengers/run-over, PTO guarding.
- **Quad bikes** for spraying/checking — same controls as pastoral use (CPD/OPD, helmet, no passengers).
  Shared vehicle detail → `../../agriculture-specialist/` (`quad-bikes-and-vehicles.md`).

## B. Packhouse & coolstore

### B1. Packhouse machinery

- **Graders, conveyors, sorters, balers, palletisers, strapping machines** — entanglement, crush and
  nip points. **Guard** them, and **isolate/lock out** before clearing jams or maintaining →
  `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
- **Forklifts** moving bins and pallets — separate trucks from people, load-centre/stability discipline →
  `../../forklift-specialist/`; the wider traffic system → `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.
- **Manual handling** — bins, boxes, repetitive packing tasks; rotate tasks, mechanical aids, workstation
  design.

### B2. Coolstores & controlled-atmosphere (CA) rooms — a confined-space killer

- **Controlled-atmosphere (CA) coolstores deliberately lower the oxygen** (and raise CO₂) to preserve
  fruit. **The atmosphere will kill an unprotected person who enters** — and there may be no warning.
- **Treat every CA room as a confined space:** locked-out/permit entry, atmospheric testing, ventilation/
  purge to safe oxygen before entry, standby person, rescue plan, and clear signage/interlocks so no one
  enters a "live" CA room. Full method → `../../confined-space-specialist/`.
- **Cold exposure** in all coolstores — clothing, time limits, and not working alone where a person could
  be trapped in the cold.

### B3. Other packhouse hazards

- **Slips/trips** (wet floors, fruit), **noise**, **fire/egress**, **electrical** (washdown areas), and
  **fatigue** during peak-season long shifts.

## Critical questions to consider

- Is every elevated work task on a **purpose-built, certified MEWP/ladder** — no home-built/modified
  platforms?
- Are MEWPs/sprayers/bin-loaders kept **clear of overhead lines** at the current approach distance?
- Is the **spray programme** controlled (label, weather, re-entry, PPE, storage)?
- Are **packhouse machines guarded and isolated** for clearing, and **forklifts separated from people**?
- Are **CA coolstores controlled as confined spaces** — tested, ventilated, permit/standby/rescue, no
  unprotected entry?
- Are **seasonal/migrant workers** inducted (in their language), competent and supervised for these tasks?
