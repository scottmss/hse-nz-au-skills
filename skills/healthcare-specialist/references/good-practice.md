# Healthcare, aged & disability care — good practice, convergence risks & guidance

Detailed good-practice controls, the sector risk profile, person-handling and named guidance for the
**workforce** in healthcare, aged residential care, and disability & community support. Generic and
company-agnostic, distilled from WorkSafe NZ's healthcare & social-assistance approach + ACC
moving-and-handling guidance and the Australian model WHS framework. **Not legal advice and not clinical
advice**; guidance changes — treat references as verify-pointers and confirm against the current rules
and a competent person.

> **Lane check:** this is **worker** health & safety, not **patient** safety / clinical-care quality
> (which has its own regulators). Keep the two apart.

## Why healthcare is a convergence sector

Care work rarely kills in a single event the way mining or maritime can — instead it produces a very
**high share of total workplace injury and harm**, because several hazards land on the **same worker at
once**: lifting people, being assaulted, working under-staffed night shifts, and handling sharps and
chemicals. The job is also increasingly done **alone, in people's homes**. So the method is to **name
each converging hazard, give it an owner, and make sure they are managed together** — not in silos.

## The risks (owner in brackets)

### Moving and handling people — the #1 injury source (owned here)
- Musculoskeletal injury from **lifting, repositioning and transferring** patients/residents; **bariatric**
  handling; assisting a falling/fallen person.
- Controls (a **program**, not a one-off):
  - **Eliminate the manual lift** — a **no-lift / minimal-lift policy**.
  - **Engineering** — **ceiling and mobile hoists**, **slide sheets**, transfer boards, electric
    profiling beds, bariatric equipment, and **enough space** (designed into facilities; assessed in the
    home).
  - **Individual handling assessment** per person, kept current and communicated.
  - **Trained handlers**, safe staffing, two-person procedures, and equipment **maintained and available
    where care happens** (including community/in-home).

### Occupational violence & aggression (→ `../../violence-aggression-specialist/`)
- Among the **highest-violence sectors**: assaults from patients/residents — often with **dementia,
  delirium or acute mental illness** — visitors, EDs and mental-health units, and **lone in-home** visits.
- Owned by the violence SME; healthcare context to bring: clinical de-escalation, behavioural-support
  plans, secure design of EDs/units, duress/lone-worker systems for community staff, and post-incident
  support.

### Psychosocial load (→ `../../psychosocial-risk-specialist/`)
- High **job demands**, **shift work and fatigue**, emotional labour, repeated exposure to **death and
  trauma**, moral injury, understaffing and **burnout**.

### Biological & sharps
- **Needlestick/sharps** injuries and exposure to **bloodborne viruses (HBV/HCV/HIV)**, **TB**,
  respiratory pathogens and **drug-resistant organisms**.
- Controls: **safer-engineered sharps** and safe disposal at point of use, **no recapping**,
  **infection prevention & control** (standard/transmission-based precautions), **immunisation**,
  **PPE/RPE** (fit-tested respirators where airborne), exposure-response (PEP) procedures. The
  substance/disposal method → `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.

### Hazardous substances (→ `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`)
- **Cytotoxic/chemotherapy** drugs (preparation, administration, spills, waste), **anaesthetic gas**
  scavenging, **glutaraldehyde/sterilants**, and cleaning chemicals.

### Plus
- **Slips/trips/falls** (wet floors, spills); **ionising radiation** (imaging/radiotherapy — separately
  regulated, below); lasers; **compressed/medical gases**; latex/dermatitis; and **lone & community
  work** (driving → `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`).

## Governing guidance & adjacent regulation (verify-pointers)

> **Sources last verified: 2026-06-30.**

- **NZ — general duty + named guidance:** no separate healthcare regime — **HSWA 2015**, regulated by
  **WorkSafe NZ**, which publishes an approach to the **healthcare & social assistance (HCSA)** sector and
  guidance on **moving and handling people**, facilities, and **violence in the health & disability
  sector**. Person handling is anchored in the **ACC *Moving and Handling People — The Guidelines***
  (ACC6075). **Ionising radiation** sits under the **Radiation Safety Act 2016**.
  - https://www.worksafe.govt.nz/topic-and-industry/healthcare/
  - ACC *Moving and Handling People* guidelines (confirm current edition).
- **AU — model WHS + priority-sector focus:** the **model WHS** primary duty (**Victoria** OHS Act 2004;
  **WA**), with the **state/territory WHS regulators** treating healthcare/aged care as a **priority**
  (occupational violence and hazardous manual tasks are standing campaigns). The model Codes
  **_Hazardous manual tasks_** and **_Managing the risks of hazardous chemicals in the workplace_** apply.
  **Ionising radiation** under **ARPANSA** + state radiation regulators. Aged-care **quality** regulation
  (ACQSC) is a separate, patient-facing regime — not worker WHS.
- Standards (e.g. safer-sharps, radiation) are **verify-pointers** — confirm the current instrument.

## Critical questions to consider

- Is this clearly **worker** safety (not patient/clinical safety)? Which **setting** (hospital, aged
  residential, disability, home/community, ambulance, mental health)?
- **Person handling:** is there a **no-lift policy**, the right **hoists/aids**, an **individual handling
  assessment**, trained staff and **enough people/space** — including in the home?
- **Violence:** is occupational violence assessed and controlled (design, behavioural-support plans,
  duress/lone-worker systems, post-incident support)? → violence SME.
- **Psychosocial:** are shift/fatigue, demands, trauma exposure and staffing managed? → psychosocial SME.
- **Biological/sharps:** safer-engineered sharps, safe disposal, IPC, immunisation, fit-tested RPE, and
  an exposure-response (PEP) pathway?
- **Substances:** are cytotoxics, anaesthetic gases and sterilants controlled? → substances SME.
- **Lone/community:** are in-home and solo workers covered (driving, violence, comms, equipment)?
- **Radiation/controlled drugs:** are the separately-regulated hazards handled under their own regime?
