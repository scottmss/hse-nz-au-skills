# Machinery Safety Specialist (plant & machinery SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for machinery and plant safety — keeping people away from
**rotating/moving parts** and preventing **unexpected start-up or stored-energy release** during
operation, servicing, cleaning and unjamming. Provides good-practice **guarding**, **isolation and
lockout/tagout (LOTO)** controls, the **competency** required, and the governing NZ/AU guidance and
standards (AS/NZS 4024). Most serious machinery harm is entanglement/crushing at an unguarded part, or
a machine starting while someone is inside it.

## When to use

- Designing or reviewing **guarding** for fixed/major plant and rotating equipment.
- Setting up **isolation / lockout-tagout** for operation, maintenance, cleaning or unjamming.
- Assessing **entanglement, crush, shear, cutting** points on machinery.
- Confirming **who is competent** to operate, service or isolate the plant.

## When NOT to use

- **Mobile plant / vehicles & traffic** (forklifts, diggers moving around a site) →
  `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.
- **Cranes/rigging/EWP licensing** → `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.
- A machine space that is also a **confined space** → also `../confined-space-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/` (this SME supplies controls/questions).
- The **plant-registration / legal duty** detail → `../safework-au-specialist/` / `../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.

## The risk

Contact with **rotating or moving parts** (entanglement, crushing, shearing, cutting, drawing-in),
**unexpected start-up** while someone is in the danger zone, and **uncontrolled release of stored
energy** (springs, hydraulics/pneumatics, gravity, electrical, thermal). Highest during servicing,
cleaning and clearing blockages — when guards are off and people are close.

## Critical controls — apply the hierarchy

See `references/good-practice.md` for detail.

1. **Eliminate / substitute** — design out the hazard: fully enclosed processes, inherently safe
   design, remove the need to reach into the machine (auto-feed, clearing without entry).
2. **Engineering — guarding & safeguards** (the core control): fixed guards over fixed-position
   hazards; **interlocked guards** where access is needed (machine stops when the guard opens);
   presence-sensing (light curtains) where appropriate; and **emergency stops** maintained, tested and
   in reach. Use manufacturer-installed guarding; **replace guards before resuming** after maintenance.
3. **Isolation / lockout-tagout (LOTO)** for any servicing, cleaning or unjamming: **isolate every
   energy source** (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, gravity, thermal, chemical), **release/de-energise
   stored energy**, apply a **personal lock and tag** at the isolation point, and **verify zero energy**
   (try-to-start) before work. One worker, one lock; group lockout where several work on the plant.
4. **Administrative** — safe operating procedures, no reaching past guards, supervision, exclusion/
   separation zones around plant, permit-to-work for high-risk isolation.
5. **PPE** — last line (and beware loose clothing/gloves/jewellery/long hair near rotating parts —
   often a *hazard*, so tie back/avoid).

## Qualifications & competency

- **Operators** trained and competent for the specific machine, per the manufacturer's instructions.
- **Isolation/LOTO** performed only by **competent, authorised** people; **electrical isolation** by a
  competent/licensed electrical worker.
- Maintenance/servicing by competent persons; guard and safeguard performance verified.
- Some plant must be **registered/authorised** (AU plant design/item registration) and overseas-sourced
  plant must meet NZ/AU standards → `../high-risk-work-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.

## Jurisdiction note

NZ: WorkSafe **Safe use of machinery** good practice + machine-lockout guidance, benchmarked to
**AS/NZS 4024**. Key NZ/AU difference — **NZ has no general plant registration** (no design/item
register); it relies on the HSWA SFAIRP duty + GPG, with only the narrow **PECPR 1999** regime (pressure
equipment, cranes, passenger ropeways) requiring **design verification + inspection** by accredited
bodies. Full NZ anchor: `references/nz-gpg.md`. AU: the model **Code of Practice "Managing the risks of
plant in the workplace"** (adopted per jurisdiction; Victoria differs) **plus a plant design and item
registration** scheme. Standard: **AS/NZS 4024** (Safety of machinery series — e.g. 4024.1201 risk
assessment, 4024.1601 controls/interlocks/guarding, 4024.1603 prevention of unexpected start-up);
copyright — treat as verify-pointers; confirm via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

A draft for a competent person to validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice guidance and structure — **not legal advice and not a substitute for
a competent person, the manufacturer's instructions, or the current standards (AS/NZS 4024)**.
Guarding and isolation are life-critical and machine-specific; a competent person must validate the
safeguards and the energy-isolation procedure against the actual plant before work proceeds.
