# Machinery & plant safety — good practice, standards & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, governing guidance, qualifications and critical questions for
machinery and plant safety (guarding and isolation/LOTO). Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from
WorkSafe NZ **Safe use of machinery** guidance and the AU model Code *Managing the risks of plant in
the workplace*, aligned with **AS/NZS 4024**. **Not legal advice**; standard editions and guidance
change — treat references as verify-pointers.

## Governing guidance & standards

- **NZ:** WorkSafe **Safe use of machinery** good-practice guidelines; machine-lockout guidance
  ("keeping workers safe with machine lockouts" / *Lockout!*).
- **AU:** model **Code of Practice "Managing the risks of plant in the workplace"** (adopted per
  state/territory; Victoria differs). The WHS Regs require **registration** of certain plant designs
  and items.
- **Standard — AS/NZS 4024 (Safety of machinery, series):** current state of knowledge for safeguarding,
  including **4024.1201** (risk assessment and risk reduction), **4024.1601** (design of controls,
  interlocks and guarding), **4024.1602** (interlocking devices associated with guards), **4024.1603**
  (prevention of unexpected start-up).

## The harm

Contact with **rotating/moving parts** — entanglement, crushing, shearing, cutting, drawing-in/nip
points — plus **unexpected start-up** while a person is in the danger zone and **uncontrolled release
of stored energy**. The highest-risk moments are **servicing, cleaning and clearing blockages**, when
guards are removed and people are close.

## Critical controls (hierarchy order)

### Eliminate / substitute
- Design out the hazard: enclosed processes, inherently safer machines, automated feeding/clearing so
  no one needs to reach into the danger zone.

### Engineering — guarding & safeguards (the core control)
- **Fixed guards** over hazards that don't need routine access — secured so they need a tool to remove.
- **Interlocked guards** where access is needed — opening the guard **isolates power/stops the machine**
  and it cannot restart until the guard is closed (AS/NZS 4024.1601/1602).
- **Presence-sensing** (e.g. light curtains, mats) where guards are impractical.
- **Emergency stops** maintained, regularly tested, in reach, and effective for immediate shutdown.
- **Always replace guards before resuming** after maintenance; inspect guards and safeguards regularly
  for effectiveness. Use **manufacturer-installed** guarding.

### Isolation & lockout/tagout (LOTO) — for servicing, cleaning, unjamming
The sequence:
1. **Identify and shut down** the machine and **every energy source** (electrical, hydraulic,
   pneumatic, mechanical/gravity, thermal, chemical, stored process material).
2. **Isolate** each source at its isolation point (disconnect/isolator, valve, blank/blind).
3. **Release or restrain stored energy** (bleed hydraulics/pneumatics, block raised parts against
   gravity, discharge capacitors, allow thermal cool-down).
4. **Lock and tag** each isolation point with a **personal lock and tag** — **one worker, one lock**;
   use **group lockout** (lock box) where several people work on the plant.
5. **Verify zero energy** before work — test that the machine **cannot start** (try-to-start) and
   confirm no residual energy.
6. **Restore** only when all people are clear, guards replaced, and locks removed by their owners.

> A tag alone is a warning, not an isolation. Locks physically prevent re-energising; **electrical
> isolation must be done by a competent/licensed electrical worker.**

### Administrative
- Safe operating procedures; **never reach past a guard** or into a running machine; clear an
  unjam only after isolation/LOTO.
- Separation/exclusion zones around plant; supervision; permit-to-work for high-risk isolations.

### PPE / individual
- PPE per the task — but near **rotating parts, loose clothing, gloves, jewellery and long hair are a
  hazard**: tie back/secure or avoid. Hearing/eye protection as required.

## Qualifications & competency

- **Operators** trained/competent for the specific machine, per manufacturer instructions.
- **LOTO/isolation** by **competent, authorised** persons only; **electrical isolation** by a
  competent/licensed electrical worker.
- Maintenance by competent persons; guard/safeguard performance verified after any change.
- **Plant registration/authorisation** where required (AU); overseas plant must meet NZ/AU standards.
  See `../../high-risk-work-specialist/` and `../../safework-au-specialist/`.

## Critical questions to consider

- Is every dangerous part **guarded**, and are guards **fixed or interlocked** as appropriate?
- Can the machine **start unexpectedly** while someone is in the danger zone — and what prevents it?
- For servicing/cleaning/unjamming, is there a documented **LOTO procedure** that isolates **all**
  energy and **releases stored energy**, with **personal locks** and a **zero-energy verification**?
- Are **emergency stops** working, tested and in reach?
- Who is **competent and authorised** to operate, service and isolate this plant?
- Have **guards been replaced** and people cleared before restart?
- Could the machine/space also be a **confined space**? → `../../confined-space-specialist/`.
