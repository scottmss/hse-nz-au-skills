# Working in, on or above water — good practice, standards & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, governing guidance, qualifications and critical questions for work in,
on or above water. Generic and company-agnostic. **Not legal advice**; standards/guidance change —
treat references as verify-pointers.

## Governing guidance & standards

- **NZ:** WorkSafe **occupational-diving** guidance; **Maritime NZ** rules for vessels and lifejackets.
  The duty to manage drowning/hypothermia risk flows from the **general HSWA duties** (primary duty,
  management of risks) — there is no single dedicated Code; manage it as a critical risk.
- **AU:** Safe Work Australia **diving** guidance and the general WHS duties (no single dedicated water
  Code in most jurisdictions).
- **Standards:** **AS 4758** (personal flotation devices — lifejackets, by level); **AS/NZS 2299**
  (occupational diving operations — incl. SSBA/SCUBA).

## The harm

- **Drowning** — falling into water (from an edge, structure or boat) or being swept/engulfed by
  changing levels/flows.
- **Hypothermia** — immersion or exposure in/near cold water; reduces the time available to self-rescue.
- **Man overboard**, capsize, entrapment underwater (diving / entanglement).

## A common trigger

When workers must access or work **within ~2 m of a water body without edge protection**, and the water
is deep enough that a person could reasonably **drown** if they fell in, do a **risk assessment** and
implement all controls before starting.

## Critical controls (hierarchy order)

### Eliminate
- Do the work **away from the water**, or with the water **isolated / diverted / drained**; use **ROVs**
  for inspections in hazardous or challenging environments to keep people out of/over the water.

### Substitute / isolate — prevent the fall in
- **Edge protection / barriers** where someone works near or above water; **fall restraint/arrest**
  where edge protection isn't practicable (the fall hazard is managed as for height, *plus* the
  drowning dimension).
- Maintain **water edges** to a high standard — free of trip hazards; test ground stability; fence
  where appropriate.

### Engineering
- Guardrails, catch nets/platforms over water; for diving, **surface-supplied breathing apparatus** and
  ROVs where direct diver entry is hazardous.

### Administrative
- A **safety observer** stationed whenever there's a drowning risk — to watch the worker and **initiate
  rescue**.
- A **rescue plan in place and understood before work begins**, with rescue equipment **readily
  deployable**; for dive operations, **oxygen resuscitation** available.
- **Monitor conditions** — weather, and water **levels/flows** (including how plant/dam operations can
  change them) and warning systems for sudden changes.
- **Lone-worker** communication/check-ins near water.
- **Boats:** a competent **skipper** following maritime rules and safe boating practice; the boat
  suitable for the task and well maintained.

### PPE / individual
- An appropriate **lifejacket (PFD)** worn whenever there's a drowning risk — typically **Level 100 or
  above** (AS 4758) — maintained/serviced, with users **trained** in its correct use.
- **Rescue floatation readily deployable** — life rings, life floats, **throw bags with retriever
  rope**, rescue buoys where people are likely to access water.
- Suitable **footwear** (ankle support, good tread) and **thermal protection** against hypothermia.

## Diving operations
- Carried out by **qualified, medically-fit dive personnel** (qualified supervisor + experienced
  divers), per **AS/NZS 2299.1**.
- **Dive equipment certified, tested and checked by the dive supervisor before use.**
- A dive-specific rescue plan and oxygen resuscitation on hand.

## Qualifications & competency

- Workers trained in **lifejacket use** and the **rescue plan**; competent **safety observer**.
- **Skipper** competent in maritime rules and safe boating (Maritime NZ).
- **Divers/supervisor** qualified and medically fit (AS/NZS 2299); equipment checked by the supervisor.

## Critical questions to consider

- How could someone **fall in** — and what prevents it (edge protection, restraint)?
- If someone fell in, how is **drowning prevented** (PFD worn, rescue floatation ready, observer)?
- Is there a **rescue plan**, with equipment and people on hand, and has it been considered for the
  actual conditions?
- Could **water levels/flows** change (weather, plant/dam operations) during the work?
- For **lone work** near water, what check-in/communication is in place?
- For **boats**: is the vessel suitable and the skipper competent? For **diving**: are personnel
  qualified, medically fit, and is the equipment checked?
