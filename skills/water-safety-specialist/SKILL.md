# Water Safety Specialist (working on/near water SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for work **in, on or above water** — where the killers
are **drowning** and **hypothermia**. Provides the good-practice controls (prevent the fall in; if
someone does, keep them afloat and recover them fast), the **competency** required, and the relevant
NZ/AU guidance and standards. Covers work at water edges and over water, on/from boats, and diving.

## When to use

- Work **near a water edge** or **over/above water** where a person could fall in and drown.
- Work **on or from a boat/vessel**, or where plant operates near water.
- **Diving** operations.
- **Lone or remote** work near water.

## When NOT to use

- The **fall hazard itself** (edge protection/harness for height over land) → `../working-at-height-specialist/`
  (this SME adds the drowning dimension where the fall is into water).
- The **vessel's maritime operation/certification** → Maritime NZ / a competent skipper.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/` (this SME supplies controls/questions).
- The **legal duty / notifiable** detail → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.

## The risk

- **Drowning** — falling into water (often from an edge, structure or boat), or being swept/engulfed by
  changing water levels/flows.
- **Hypothermia** — from immersion or prolonged exposure in/near cold water.
- **Man overboard**, capsize, and being trapped underwater (diving / entanglement).

## Critical controls — apply the hierarchy

See `references/good-practice.md` for detail.

1. **Eliminate** — do the work **away from the water** or when water is **isolated/diverted/drained**;
   use remote methods (ROVs for inspection) to avoid people entering or being over water.
2. **Substitute / isolate (prevent the fall in)** — **edge protection / barriers / fall restraint**
   where someone works near or above water; manage ground stability and trip hazards at water edges.
3. **Engineering** — guardrails, catch nets/platforms; for diving, surface-supplied air and ROVs in
   challenging environments.
4. **Administrative** — a **safety observer** whenever there's a drowning risk; a **rescue plan** in
   place and understood **before work**, with rescue equipment readily deployable; **monitoring of
   conditions** (weather, water levels/flows, and any plant operations that change them); lone-worker
   communication/check-ins; for boats, a competent **skipper** following maritime rules.
5. **PPE / individual** — an appropriate **lifejacket (PFD)** worn whenever there's a drowning risk
   (a **Level 100 or above** for most work over/near water), maintained and serviced, with users
   trained in its use; suitable footwear; thermal protection against hypothermia.

> **Rescue is as important as prevention** — a person in cold water has little time. Have rescue
> floatation **readily deployable** (life rings, life floats, **throw bags with retriever rope**) and a
> rehearsed plan; don't rely on emergency services alone.

## Qualifications & competency

- Workers trained in **lifejacket use** and the **rescue plan**; a competent **safety observer**.
- **Boats:** a competent **skipper** who understands maritime rules and safe boating (Maritime NZ).
- **Diving:** carried out by **qualified, medically-fit dive personnel** (qualified supervisor +
  experienced divers); dive equipment **certified, tested and checked by the dive supervisor** before
  use, per **AS/NZS 2299** occupational diving operations.

## Jurisdiction note

NZ: WorkSafe **occupational-diving** guidance; **Maritime NZ** rules for vessels and lifejackets.
Standards: **AS 4758** (personal flotation devices), **AS/NZS 2299** (occupational diving). The duty to
manage drowning/hypothermia risk flows from the general HSWA/WHS duties (no single dedicated Code in
either country — manage under the primary duty). Treat references as verify-pointers; confirm via
`../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

A **water-work control plan** (eliminate/isolate water → prevent the fall in → PFD & thermal →
safety observer → rescue plan & equipment → conditions monitoring → competency → critical questions),
or the water-specific controls to feed a bow tie (`../critical-risk-manager/`) or task analysis
(`../task-analysis-author/`). A draft for a competent person to validate.

## Hand-offs

- **Bow tie / control assurance** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.
- **The fall-from-height aspect** (edge protection/restraint) → `../working-at-height-specialist/`.
- **Notifiable / duty** → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`. Route via
  `../hse-advisor/`.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice guidance and structure — **not legal advice and not a substitute for
a competent person, Maritime NZ rules, or the current standards (AS 4758, AS/NZS 2299)**. Drowning and
hypothermia happen fast; a competent person must validate the prevention, flotation and rescue
arrangements against the actual water, conditions and task before work proceeds.
