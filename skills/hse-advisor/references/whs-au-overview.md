# WHS model law (AU) — orchestration overview

A high-level framing of the Australian **Work Health and Safety (WHS) model law** for routing
and jurisdiction-setting. This is not a substitute for the Act/Regulations *as enacted in each
jurisdiction*, nor for Safe Work Australia model Codes, and it is not legal advice.

> **Section numbers below are for the model WHS Act and are verify-pointers, not quotes.** Each
> state/territory enacts its own version, so the *enacted* section numbers and penalties can
> differ — and **Victoria does not use the model law at all** (see below). Always confirm the
> provision in the specific jurisdiction. Deep statutory detail belongs in the
> `safework-au-specialist` skill; this overview stays at the routing level.

## Model law, enacted separately by each jurisdiction

Safe Work Australia maintains the **model WHS Act and model WHS Regulations**. Each state and
territory enacts its own version. Most have adopted the model law, but **Victoria has not** — it
runs its own *Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004 (Vic)* with materially different
terminology and duties (e.g. the primary employer duty sits at **OHS Act s 21**, duty to other
persons at **s 23** — *not* the model-law sections below). Always confirm which jurisdiction
applies.

| Jurisdiction | Regulator (typical) | Framework |
|---|---|---|
| NSW | SafeWork NSW | Model WHS |
| Queensland | Workplace Health and Safety Queensland | Model WHS |
| Western Australia | WorkSafe WA / DEMIRS | Model WHS (adopted 2022) |
| South Australia | SafeWork SA | Model WHS |
| Tasmania | WorkSafe Tasmania | Model WHS |
| ACT | WorkSafe ACT | Model WHS |
| Northern Territory | NT WorkSafe | Model WHS |
| Victoria | WorkSafe Victoria | **OHS Act 2004 (not model WHS)** |
| Commonwealth / national employers | Comcare | Model WHS (Cth) |

## The core duty holders (model WHS Act)

- **PCBU — Person Conducting a Business or Undertaking** (meaning: **s 5**). Primary duty of care:
  ensure health and safety **so far as is reasonably practicable (SFAIRP)** — **s 19**.
- **Officers** (**s 27**). Owe a **due diligence** duty to ensure the PCBU complies — closely
  aligned with the NZ officer duty (both jurisdictions drew on the same model law).
- **Workers** (**s 28**) and **other persons at a workplace** (**s 29**) — take reasonable care,
  follow reasonable instructions.
- **Reasonably practicable** is defined at **s 18**; the **management-of-risks** duty (eliminate
  SFAIRP, else minimise) at **s 17**.

The model-law concepts map closely onto NZ HSWA (which was modelled on them), but the wording,
section numbers, penalties and regulations are **not identical**, and Victoria diverges
substantially.

## Notifiable incidents (model WHS Act)

A **notifiable incident** — a death (**s 35**), a serious injury or illness (**ss 36–37**), or a
**dangerous incident** as defined — must be reported to the relevant WHS regulator (**duty to
notify, s 38**) and the **site preserved (s 39)**. The precise triggers and the regulator differ
by jurisdiction — route to `safework-au-specialist` for the specific tests.

## High-risk work licensing (HRWL)

Australia operates a national **High Risk Work Licence** system with defined classes (e.g. crane
classes CN/CV/C2–C6/CO/CB/CT, dogging DG, rigging RB/RI/RA, scaffolding SB/SI/SA, EWP WP, forklift
LF/LO). This is an Australian construct — route HRWL questions to `high-risk-work-specialist`.
NZ does **not** use the same licence-class system.

## Standards

Australia uses **AS** and joint **AS/NZS** standards, and Safe Work Australia **model Codes of
Practice** (which, once approved in a jurisdiction, are admissible and indicate what is known
about a hazard and the reasonably practicable controls).

## Routing cues

A state/territory name, SafeWork/WorkSafe (state), Comcare, "model WHS", "Code of Practice", or
HRWL licence classes → treat the matter as **Australian** — and immediately pin down **which**
jurisdiction, because Victoria and the others differ.

## Authoritative sources

Always confirm the current text in the relevant jurisdiction — start from:

- **Safe Work Australia — model WHS Act**:
  https://www.safeworkaustralia.gov.au/doc/model-work-health-and-safety-act
- **Safe Work Australia — model WHS Regulations & Codes of Practice**:
  https://www.safeworkaustralia.gov.au/law-and-regulation/model-whs-laws
- **Victoria (does NOT use model law) — OHS Act 2004 (Vic)** via WorkSafe Victoria:
  https://www.worksafe.vic.gov.au/occupational-health-and-safety-act-and-regulations
- Then the **enacting Act in the specific state/territory** (each regulator publishes its own
  consolidated Act and Regulations) — confirm the enacted section numbers, which can differ from
  the model numbers cited above.
