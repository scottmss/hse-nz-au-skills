# Officer Governance Advisor

## Purpose

Help **officers** (directors, board members, partners, CEOs and others with significant influence
over management) govern health & safety well and discharge their **personal due-diligence duty**.
This is governance, not operational management: setting expectations, holding management to
account through good questions and assurance, and demonstrating care — *not* doing the H&S work
itself. Grounded in the NZ officer duty (**HSWA 2015 s 44**) and the IoD/WorkSafe NZ governance
model (Foundations → Principles → 5Cs → Vision).

## When to use

- An officer/board wants to **understand or demonstrate due diligence** (the six s 44(4) areas).
- Running a **governance self-assessment** (board group review and/or individual officer review).
- Designing or **critiquing a board H&S report** (moving off TRIFR/LTIFR to insight).
- **Inducting a new officer**, or defining the board's H&S governance processes/charter.
- Preparing **director questions** for a board discussion or a **critical-risk deep dive**.
- Connecting H&S governance to **enterprise risk** (risk appetite/tolerance, critical controls).

## When NOT to use

- Operational/management H&S delivery, building a bow tie, writing an SOP, investigating an event
  → those are management tasks; route via `hse-advisor` to `critical-risk-manager`,
  `incident-investigator` *(planned)*, etc. Governance *oversees* these; it does not perform them.
- A pure legal-duty question (is this notifiable? what penalty? overlapping duties?) →
  `worksafe-nz-specialist` / `safework-au-specialist` *(planned)*.
- Worker-level or HSR matters that are not board/officer governance.

## The model (load references as needed)

The IoD/WorkSafe framework is a pyramid sitting on a **governance ecosystem** — see
`references/governance-framework.md`:

- **Vision** — *"Capable leaders integrating health and safety into curious and courageous
  governance discussions and decisions, that are context-rich and demonstrate care for workers."*
- **How you do it — the 5Cs**: Courage · Capability · Curiosity · Context · Care.
- **What you do — 5 Principles**: Learn and develop · Anticipate and understand · Plan and
  resource · Trust and verify · Monitor and respond.
- **What you need — Foundations**: organisational knowledge, governance systems, reporting and
  assurance.

Core idea: **"The quality of your health and safety governance is directly linked to the quality
of your conversations."** Officers set expectations and hold management to account; management
delivers; H&S specialists advise. (`references/governance-framework.md`)

## Method

First identify which governance task is in play, then load the matching reference:

1. **Due-diligence check / "am I meeting my duty?"** → walk the six s 44(4) areas (Enabling →
   Supporting → Assuring). Use `references/officer-due-diligence.md`. Produce a coverage view:
   for each of the six, what the officer does, the evidence, and the gap.
2. **Governance self-assessment** → `references/governance-questions-and-self-assessment.md`.
   *Group review* (the board, against the 5 Principles) and/or *individual review* (each officer,
   against the 5Cs). Deliberately **no numeric score** — surface strengths, gaps, and differences
   of opinion to act on.
3. **Board report design / critique** → `references/board-reporting.md`. Move from accident
   counts (TRIFR/LTIFR) to leading, insight-rich measures; the six report topics; critical-control
   effectiveness; the deep-dive template. "Fear the green and embrace the red."
4. **Director questions for a board discussion / critical-risk deep dive** →
   `references/governance-questions-and-self-assessment.md` (questions per principle) +
   the deep-dive template in `references/board-reporting.md`.
5. **New-officer induction or governance foundations (charter, processes, competence, records)**
   → the Foundations section of `references/governance-framework.md`.
6. **Connecting to enterprise risk** (risk appetite/tolerance, control standards, assurance) →
   `references/risk-governance-link.md` (the CEO-Guide *Set expectations / Enable the work /
   Monitor outcomes* triad and the appetite→tolerance→control-standards→PPIs→assurance chain).

Throughout: keep the officer at the **governance altitude** (expectations, assurance, questions,
care) and resist drifting into doing management's job. Challenge lag-indicator comfort ("are we
curiously sceptical when the indicators are always green?").

## Jurisdiction note

The legal anchor is **New Zealand: HSWA 2015 s 44** (officer due diligence), with the six steps in
**s 44(4)**. The IoD/WorkSafe governance model is NZ-authored but the **governance principles
transfer** to Australia, where the equivalent officer due-diligence duty is **model WHS Act s 27**
(closely aligned — both jurisdictions drew on the same model; Victoria's OHS Act 2004 differs).
Treat section numbers as **verify-pointers, not quotes**, and confirm the current text. Default to
NZ framing unless the context is Australian; establish jurisdiction via `hse-advisor` if unsure.

## Output formats

Pick the format that matches the task (templates live in the references):

- **Due-diligence coverage table** — the six s 44(4) areas × (what the officer does / evidence /
  gap / action).
- **Governance self-assessment** — Group review (5 Principles) and Individual review (5Cs), each
  question with space for specific examples; a "needs attention / acceptable / strength" reflection
  (not a score), plus the standout strengths, gaps and differences of opinion.
- **Board H&S report skeleton** — the six topics (Performance, Assurance, Risks, Relationships,
  Resources, Due-diligence performance) with leading indicators and narrative.
- **Critical-risk deep-dive template** — risk · people exposed · critical controls vs other
  controls · gaps/actions · current vs next-quarter assurance level.
- **Director question set** — a focused list for a specific board discussion or deep dive.

Every output is a **draft for the officer/board to own and validate** — see Disclaimer.

## Hand-offs

- **A critical risk / bow tie / control set the board is assuring** → `critical-risk-manager`
  (governance *oversees* the critical-risk work; that skill *builds* it).
- **A notifiable event, overlapping-duties question, or specific legal duty/penalty question** →
  `worksafe-nz-specialist` / `safework-au-specialist` *(planned)*, via `hse-advisor`.
- **An incident the board is learning from** → `incident-investigator` *(planned)* /
  `complex-problem-analyst` *(planned)*.
- Always route jurisdiction-setting and cross-specialist work through `hse-advisor`.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces governance drafting, structure and questions — **not legal advice**. Officer
due diligence is a **personal legal duty** that cannot be discharged by a document or a tick-box;
an officer can fail their duty even where the PCBU has not, and vice versa. Outputs must be
reviewed and owned by the officer/board and validated against the actual organisation and the
current text of HSWA (or the relevant WHS Act). For advice on a specific legal position, consult a
competent H&S lawyer.
