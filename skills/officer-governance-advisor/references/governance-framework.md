# The H&S governance framework (IoD/WorkSafe NZ, 2024)

The model from the Institute of Directors NZ / WorkSafe NZ **"Health and Safety Governance — A
Good Practice Guide" (2024)** (with the Business Leaders' Health and Safety Forum). It replaces the
2016 "Blue Book". This is a guide to good governance, *not* legal advice — officers must satisfy
themselves directly against HSWA. Section numbers here are verify-pointers, not quotes.

## Governance vs management

Governance is **not** managing the organisation — it is keeping an overview and holding management
to account so the organisation operates productively, ethically and legally. H&S governance is one
component of overall governance, **inter-woven** with the others, not a standalone process.

- **Officers** set expectations and hold to account.
- **Management** delivers on those expectations (with input from workers).
- **H&S specialists** advise so the right activities are done, and done effectively.

> **"The quality of your health and safety governance is directly linked to the quality of your
> conversations."** To do it well: think about what is happening, what your critical risks are,
> what key controls manage them, whether you are confident those controls work, and **what you base
> that confidence on**.

## The governance ecosystem (the loop)

A continuous loop between Governance and Company Management, centred on **industry good practice**
informed by **independent** and **internal** H&S advice:

- **Set expectations** (Governance) — charter and policy; strategy and objectives.
- **Deliver on expectations** (Management) — set targets; incorporate into management practices;
  allocate resources.
- **Inform** (Management → Governance) — performance insights; H&S reports; worker participation.
- **Understand performance** (Governance) — know what questions to ask; recognise what good looks
  like; have an assurance plan.

## The pyramid

From the base up — *what you need*, *what you do*, *how you do it*, and the vision at the apex:

```
                        ▲  VISION
                           Capable leaders integrating health and safety into curious and
                           courageous governance discussions and decisions, that are
                           context-rich and demonstrate care for workers.

            HOW YOU DO IT — the 5Cs
            Courage · Capability · Curiosity · Context · Care

         WHAT YOU DO — principles
         Learn and develop · Anticipate and understand · Plan and resource ·
         Trust and verify · Monitor and respond

      WHAT YOU NEED — foundations
      Organisational knowledge, governance systems, reporting and assurance
```

- **Foundations** (*what you need*) — the systems and building blocks that support governance work.
  Detail below.
- **Principles** (*what you do*) — the five core activities of good governance. See
  `governance-questions-and-self-assessment.md` for each principle's director questions.
- **5Cs** (*how you do it*) — the mindset/behaviours officers model to set the "tone from the top".
  See `governance-questions-and-self-assessment.md` for the 5Cs self-reflection questions.
- **Vision** — the integrating aim all of the above serves.

## What defines "good" health and safety

H&S thinking has moved from counting injuries to a **work-focused** view that accounts for the
complexity and variability of real work. Systems and rules cannot predict every eventuality;
workers constantly adapt to make imperfect systems succeed. So:

- **Lagging counts (TRIFR, LTIFR) are weak measures** — people can escape unhurt from very unsafe
  work, so the absence of an accident says little about how safe the work was. Such measures can be
  random, non-predictive and unhelpful.
- **Focus on positive contributors** — resources, designing work well, training people, and
  **testing key controls**. (See `board-reporting.md`.)
- Good H&S governance also improves engagement, working environment and performance more broadly.

## Foundations — what you need (the 7 building blocks)

Review these are in place and fit-for-purpose; for smaller orgs without a formal board, find a way
to get the same visibility, assurance and outcomes.

| Foundation | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Set expectations** | Define that officers/executives own and prioritise H&S; make clear ownership of risks sits with officers and management — **not** the H&S team or H&S reps. |
| **New officer induction** | What happens when an officer starts and who is responsible. Cover: what the org does; key people; **worker demographics incl. higher-risk groups**; other orgs relied on; key H&S issues and how managed; the officer's due-diligence obligations and the reasonable steps to meet them. (Best done face-to-face with early site visits, focused on critical risks.) |
| **Governance processes** | Charters and terms of reference: H&S vision/policy/strategy/objectives; meeting frequency; board/committee structure; programme of work and approvals; what reports the board routinely sees; the board's role in non-routine events/emergencies. |
| **Competence framework** | Define the H&S skills/experience needed at governance level and assess the gap; enable access to **competent independent advice** to supplement internal capability and provide assurance. |
| **Continuous improvement** | Regular self-review of how governance is working: team effectiveness; openness of the CEO/management relationship; quality of information received; keeping abreast of good practice; how well issues/concerns are called out. |
| **Assurance** | A structured assurance programme drawing on multiple sources: internal/external audits; inspections and site visits; topic reports / **deep dives** into critical-risk management. |
| **Records** | Keep good records of governance activity: meeting minutes; decision logs; activities (e.g. site visits); and **the officer's own activities** (e.g. H&S training received) — evidence of due diligence. |

### Foundational questions (useful for smaller orgs)

- **Processes** — *What have we got?* (does the process meet good practice?) · *Does it work?* (is
  it effective and actually applied — how do we know?) · *Is it enough?* (any gaps?).
- **Information** — *So what?* (what insight does it give?) · *Is it meaningful?* (robust, honest,
  representative?) · *What now?* (next step if it's a concern, or notably good?).

## Equity in H&S outcomes (Appendix 3)

Some groups carry higher risk — **Māori and Pasifika** workers, migrant and different-first-language
workers, workers with disability, and young/older workers. Governance levers: **representation**
(board diversity and dynamics that enable robust kōrero), **language** (multiple languages,
translation, visuals), **culture** (cultural norms in feedback — "simply telling people they can
speak up is not enough"), and **accessibility** (literacy/numeracy support, physical/sensory
access). Holistic wellbeing lenses offered include **Te Whare Tapa Whā** (taha tinana / hinengaro /
wairua / whānau). Whakataukī: *Nā tō rourou, nā taku rourou, ka ora ai te iwi.*
