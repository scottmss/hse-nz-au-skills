# Connecting H&S governance to enterprise risk

How board/officer H&S governance ties into broader business risk governance. Based on the Business
Leaders' Health and Safety Forum **"CEO Guide to Risk"** (NZ), the practical companion to the
IoD/WorkSafe governance guide. Same NZ governance ecosystem; this view adds the **risk-management
spine**. Not legal advice.

## The leadership triad

The CEO/officer's leadership of H&S risk is framed as three repeating verbs — a plan-do-check-learn
loop:

1. **Set expectations** — clarify commitment, expectations and approach to managing H&S risks
   ("sets the tone").
2. **Enable the work** — ensure risks are understood, controls implemented, and risk management
   properly resourced ("so intentions become reality"), built on functional, technical and
   organisational capability.
3. **Monitor outcomes** — monitor activity, verify, and continually look for ways to learn and
   improve ("to maximise resilience").

These map directly onto **HSWA s 44 officer due diligence**, which the guide restates as:
**understand the risks · manage the risks · verify your risk management.** (See
`officer-due-diligence.md`.) The guide also positions completing its workbook as part of your
**due-diligence records**.

## The nine essentials

Each triad domain decomposes into three essentials (assess each "going ok / needs attention"):

| Domain | Essentials |
|---|---|
| **Set expectations** | Clarify attitude · Set expectations · Develop approach |
| **Enable the work** | Identify risks · Control risks · Resource the work |
| **Monitor outcomes** | Monitor and verify · Reflect · Learn and improve |

Diagnostic example (*Control risks*): "Have we removed or managed risks, **especially critical risks
that can cause death, permanent injury / life-shortening illness?**"

## The risk-management chain (appetite → assurance)

The bridge from enterprise risk language to H&S critical-control assurance:

- **Risk appetite** — the degree of risk the organisation will accept and pursue to achieve its
  objectives; sets boundaries for activities. *Set with the board.*
- **Risk tolerance** — the **maximum level of risk** the business will operate within; translates
  appetite into **operational limits** for day-to-day management.
- **Risk criteria** — define each risk's causes/consequences and how measured, how "likelihood" is
  defined, timeframes, the level at which a risk **becomes acceptable**, and whether **combinations
  of risks** should be considered. *Decided with the board.*
- **Control standards for critical risks** — the performance standard each critical control must
  meet. *Set by the leadership team and board.*
- **Positive performance indicators (PPIs)** — leading measures for **key risk controls**, monitored
  in the executive/CEO programme (feeds the board report — see `board-reporting.md`).
- **Assurance programme** — internal/external audit and verification confirming controls operate as
  intended. *"Don't assume the system is working. Verify."*

Supporting concepts the guide defines: **risk profile** (the org's whole risk landscape); **key risk
controls** (critical to managing a risk — monitor their performance); **escalation requirements**
(when/how critical risks are brought to those accountable); **risk velocity** (how fast a risk
travels from event to consequence — tells you how much time you have and whether controls are
adequate); **recovery control** (lessens consequences after an event — e.g. a fall-arrest harness);
**risk control effectiveness** (operates in a consistent, repeatable, defined way).

## Control taxonomy

Controls span **prevention · protection · detection · recovery** (remove the risk or prevent
exposure first). Peer-CEO steer: focus on the **most effective controls first** — *"administrative
controls like training and PPE shouldn't be the focus"* — and **focus attention on critical risks**:
"don't sweat the small stuff." (Consistent with the hierarchy of controls used in
`../../critical-risk-manager/`.)

## Integration mandate

**Integrate H&S into broader business risk management.** Consider H&S risk as part of broader
business-risk discussions, and consider the **opportunities and impacts of business activities on
H&S — and vice versa.** Set **roles, responsibilities and accountabilities** for the board, senior
executives, management, supervisors, workers **and the supply chain**, each clear on what they are
accountable for, the standard expected, and how they are held to account.

## Practical CEO/officer heuristics (peer advice)

- Lead by example; make H&S leadership visible.
- Keep a **direct, unfiltered line between the CEO and the H&S manager**.
- Include frontline workers and contractors in the conversation.
- Learn to **hold a good safety discussion and walk the site**.
- Risk management is **ongoing, not an annual event**.
- **Verify — don't assume the system is working.** Paperwork and checklists are not how you manage
  risk.
