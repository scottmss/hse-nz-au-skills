# Forestry Specialist (forestry & harvesting SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **forestry and harvesting** — one of the highest-
fatality sectors, with **deadly core tasks no other skill covers**: manual and mechanised **tree
felling**, **breaking out / cable (hauler) logging**, work on the **cutover**, and the **landing/skid**.
Owns those forestry-specific hazards directly and routes the generic ones to the relevant SME. The
modern shift — **mechanise to get people out of the danger zone** — is the strongest control and the
spine of this skill.

## When to use

- Any **forestry / logging / harvesting / silviculture** safety question.
- **Tree felling** — manual chainsaw felling (assessment, escape routes, scarf/back-cut, hung-up trees,
  windthrow) and **mechanised felling** (feller-bunchers, harvesters).
- **Breaking out & cable/hauler logging** — strops/chokers, ropes under tension, the break-out crew,
  skyline/guylines, signalling, exclusion zones.
- **Landing/skid work** — log stacks, loaders, log-truck loading, rolling logs.
- **Steep-slope harvesting** — winch-assist/tethered machines, keeping people off the slope.
- **Silviculture** (planting, pruning, thinning) and the **remote/lone-worker** picture.

## When NOT to use

- The **specific hazard detail** belongs to its SME (this skill routes to them):
  - **Mobile plant / log-truck traffic on roads & skids** → `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.
  - **Machine guarding / chainsaw & processor mechanical safety, isolation** → `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
  - **Tree climbing / arboriculture / work at height** → `../working-at-height-specialist/`.
  - **Fuel, oils, agrichemicals (vegetation/pest control)** → `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.
- **Farm woodlots as part of a pastoral operation** → also `../agriculture-specialist/` (cross-link).
- **Who holds the duty / notifiable events** → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## The forestry fatal & serious risks

Plan against the recurring killers (detail in `references/good-practice.md`; the two deadliest tasks in
`references/felling-and-breaking-out.md`):

1. **Tree felling** — struck by the falling tree, limbs, or a **hung-up / windthrow / barber-chair**
   tree; the single deadliest task → flagship.
2. **Breaking out / cable logging** — struck by logs, and **ropes/strops under tension** (failure,
   snap-back); working in the **bight** or under a suspended log → flagship.
3. **Mobile plant & harvesting machines** — skidders, forwarders, feller-bunchers, harvesters, loaders;
   rollover, struck-by, machine exclusion zones → `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/` + `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
4. **Landing / skid** — rolling/sliding logs, log-truck loading, stacks, traffic.
5. **Steep, remote terrain** — slips/falls, machine stability, distance from rescue.
6. **Chainsaws** — kickback, cuts (the most common injury source).
7. **Log transport** — loading/unloading, load security, forestry roads.
8. **Working alone / remotely** — communications, first aid, evacuation (often helicopter).
9. **Health** — **hand-arm vibration (HAVS)**, noise, fatigue, manual handling, sun/UV.
10. **Fire & weather** — vegetation fire; **wind raises windthrow and felling risk**.

## Flagship — the two deadliest tasks

**Eliminate the exposure first: mechanise.** Mechanised felling and **winch-assist (tethered) machines
on steep slopes** remove the faller and breaker-out from the most lethal positions — this is the
single biggest control. Where people are still on the cutover:

- **Manual tree felling:** only **trained, certified fallers**; assess the tree (lean, head, defects,
  windthrow) and surrounds; clear **two escape routes**; correct scarf/back-cut and hinge; **never
  drive or work under a hung-up tree**; maintain the **no-go / safe working distance (commonly two tree
  lengths)** — no one else in the zone.
- **Breaking out & cable logging:** trained breaker-outs and head breaker-out; treat every **rope/strop
  as lethal under tension** (stand clear of the **bight** and snap-back path); agreed **signals** before
  any pull; **no one under a suspended or moving log**; controlled exclusion zones around the hauler and
  ropes.

Full detail, including landing/skid log handling and mechanised harvesting, in
`references/felling-and-breaking-out.md`.

## Competency (a defining control)

Forestry competency is task-specific and certified — confirm before work:

- **NZ:** **Safetree** certification and **NZQA forestry unit standards** for fallers, breaker-outs,
  hauler/machine operators and silviculture workers; trainees only under direct supervision of a
  competent person. (See `../high-risk-work-specialist/` for the competency-system framing.)
- **AU:** nationally recognised forestry/harvesting competencies (RTO-delivered) per the state code.

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction** (NZ/AU) and the operation (felling / extraction / landing / silviculture).
2. **Screen against the forestry risks** above; identify which apply.
3. **Apply elimination first** — can the task be **mechanised** / done with **winch-assist** to remove
   people from the cutover/danger zone? Then the felling and breaking-out controls.
4. **Route generic hazards** to their SME (mobile plant, machinery, height, substances).
5. **Cover the sector essentials** — competency/certification, exclusion zones and signalling,
   communications and rescue for remote/lone work, fatigue and HAVS.
6. **State residual obligations** — the forestry PCBU owes the primary duty; competent crews,
   supervision, a harvest plan and a risk register are required. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**NZ:** WorkSafe's **Approved Code of Practice "Safe practice for forestry and harvesting operations"**
(**2025 — current and HSWA-aligned**, unlike older sector ACOPs), used with the **Safetree** best-
practice guides (e.g. Tree Felling Best Practice Guide; windthrow), **FISC** guidance, and the **Small
Forest Harvest GPG** for woodlots. **AU:** Safe Work Australia's **General guide for managing risks in
forestry operations** and **Guide to managing risks of timber harvesting operations**, plus
state/territory Codes (e.g. **SafeWork NSW "Safety in forest harvesting operations"**, **WorkSafe Qld
"Forest harvesting Code of Practice"**) and the AFPA industry body. **Note:** the AU **tree-work /
arboriculture** Codes (SafeWork NSW Tree work; SWA *Guide to managing the risks of tree work*) **exclude
commercial forestry** — use them for urban/arboriculture, not harvesting. Jurisdiction variations
(Victoria OHS Act, WA) → `../safework-au-specialist/`. Confirm current editions via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

Either a **forestry safety plan / risk screen** (operation → applicable risks → elimination via
mechanisation → felling/breaking-out controls → routed hazards → competency, exclusion zones, comms/
rescue → critical questions), or the **forestry-specific controls and questions** to feed into a bow
tie (`../critical-risk-manager/`), task analysis (`../task-analysis-author/`) or SOP (`../sop-author/`).
A draft for a competent person to validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice forestry-safety guidance — **not legal advice and not a substitute
for a competent person or the current ACOP/Codes and Safetree/industry best practice**, which change.
Validate every control (felling method, exclusion zones, cable-logging set-up, machine and competency
requirements) against the actual site, terrain, trees, equipment and crew before relying on it.
