# Forestry & harvesting safety — good practice (NZ/AU)

Methodology reference for `forestry-specialist`. Grounded in **WorkSafe NZ's Approved Code of Practice
"Safe practice for forestry and harvesting operations" (2025)** and the **Safetree / FISC** industry
best practice (NZ), and the **Safe Work Australia forestry guides + state harvesting Codes** (AU).
Government/regulator guidance is summarised with attribution; AS/NZS standards are **copyright —
verify-pointers only**. Confirm current editions/guidance.

> **Not legal advice.** A competent person validates controls against the actual site, terrain, trees,
> equipment and crew. The forestry ACOP and Safetree best-practice guides are the operational authority.

---

## 1. The forestry picture

Forestry and harvesting is consistently **one of the highest-fatality sectors**. Its hazards are
**unique** — a tree under tension, a rope under load, a machine on a steep slope — and the work is
**remote, weather-exposed and physically extreme**. Two features shape the safety approach:

- **The deadliest tasks are forestry-specific** — manual **tree felling** and **breaking out / cable
  logging** — and are owned directly by this skill (`felling-and-breaking-out.md`).
- **Mechanisation is the headline control.** Getting people **off the cutover** — mechanised felling and
  **winch-assist (tethered) machines** on steep slopes — removes fallers and breaker-outs from the most
  lethal positions. Eliminate the exposure before relying on procedure.

The forestry **PCBU** (forest owner, manager, contractor) owes the full primary duty, often through a
**contracting chain** — plan the harvest, control the fatal risks so far as is reasonably practicable,
ensure **certified competency** and supervision, and manage the overlapping duties between owner,
manager and crews (→ `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` for overlapping duties).

## 2. The forestry fatal & serious risks (screen every operation)

1. **Tree felling** — struck by the tree/limbs or a **hung-up, windthrow or barber-chair** tree → flagship.
2. **Breaking out / cable logging** — struck by logs; **ropes/strops under tension** (failure, snap-back);
   the **bight**; suspended/moving logs → flagship.
3. **Mobile plant & harvesting machines** — skidders, forwarders, feller-bunchers, harvesters, loaders;
   rollover, struck-by; **machine exclusion zones** (no one near a working machine) →
   `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/` + `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
4. **Landing / skid** — rolling/sliding logs, log-truck loading, unstable stacks, traffic (`felling-and-breaking-out.md` §C).
5. **Steep, remote terrain** — slips/falls, machine stability, distance from rescue.
6. **Chainsaws** — kickback and cuts (most common injury source); guarding/maintenance →
   `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
7. **Log transport** — loading/unloading, load security, narrow forestry roads.
8. **Working alone / remotely** — comms (radio/satellite/PLB), first aid, **helicopter evacuation**.
9. **Health** — **hand-arm vibration (HAVS)** from chainsaws, **noise**, fatigue, manual handling, sun/UV.
10. **Fire & weather** — vegetation fire risk; **wind raises windthrow and felling danger** — stop felling
    in dangerous wind.

## 3. Competency & supervision (a defining control)

Forestry work is **task-certified** — the right ticket for the exact task, every time:

- **NZ:** **Safetree** certification and **NZQA forestry unit standards** for **fallers, breaker-outs,
  head breaker-outs, hauler/machine operators and silviculture workers**; **trainees only under the
  direct supervision** of a competent person until assessed competent. Competency-system framing →
  `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.
- **AU:** nationally recognised forestry/harvesting competencies (RTO-delivered) per the state code.
- Confirm crews are competent, inducted to **this site's** plan, fit and not fatigued.

## 4. Baseline controls (all forestry work)

- **Eliminate first — mechanise / winch-assist** to remove people from the cutover and danger zones.
- **Harvest/operation plan** for the site — terrain, extraction method, machine and crew positions,
  **exclusion zones**, escape routes, services/overhead lines, weather triggers.
- **Exclusion zones & separation** — keep people clear of felling, ropes, the bight, suspended logs and
  working machines; one person never in two danger zones.
- **Agreed communications/signals** — between faller, breaker-out crew, hauler/machine operators (whistle/
  horn/radio); anyone can call STOP.
- **Competent, certified, supervised crews**; manage **fatigue** (long remote days) and **HAVS/noise**.
- **Remote-work plan** — comms, first aid trained to the injuries that occur (crush, chainsaw cut,
  bleeding), and a **realistic rescue/evacuation** plan (often helicopter).
- **Weather discipline** — stop felling/hauling in dangerous wind; manage windthrow specifically.
- **A living risk register / plan** and pre-task thinking; report and learn from incidents/near-misses.

---

## 5. Critical questions to consider

- Can the task be **mechanised / winch-assisted** to get people **off the cutover**?
- Are **fallers and breaker-outs certified** (Safetree/NZQA or the state competency) and supervised?
- Are **exclusion zones, escape routes and the no-go (two-tree-length) distance** set and respected?
- For cable logging, is every **rope/strop treated as lethal**, the **bight and snap-back path clear**,
  and **signals agreed** before any pull? (→ `felling-and-breaking-out.md`)
- Are **working machines separated from people**, and **log-truck loading / the landing** controlled?
- Is there a **comms and helicopter-capable rescue plan** for this remote site?
- Are **wind/weather triggers** set, and **windthrow** specifically managed?
- Could an event be **notifiable**? → `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../../safework-au-specialist/`.

## Sources & discipline

- **NZ:** WorkSafe **ACOP "Safe practice for forestry and harvesting operations" (2025 — current,
  HSWA-aligned)**; **Safetree** best-practice guides (tree felling, windthrow) and **FISC** guidance;
  **Small Forest Harvest GPG** for woodlots. Summarised with attribution.
- **AU:** Safe Work Australia **General guide for managing risks in forestry operations** and **Guide to
  managing risks of timber harvesting operations**; state Codes (**SafeWork NSW Safety in forest
  harvesting operations**; **WorkSafe Qld Forest harvesting COP**); AFPA. The AU **tree-work/arboriculture**
  Codes **exclude commercial forestry** — don't use them for harvesting. Jurisdiction variations →
  `../../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Specific-hazard standards live with the relevant SME (machinery, mobile plant, height, substances).
  Confirm current editions/guidance before relying on any figure or rule.
