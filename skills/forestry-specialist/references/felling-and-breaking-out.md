# Tree felling, breaking out & the landing — good practice (NZ/AU)

Flagship reference for `forestry-specialist`, covering the **two deadliest forestry tasks** plus the
landing/skid: **(A) tree felling**, **(B) breaking out & cable logging**, **(C) the landing / log
handling**. Grounded in WorkSafe NZ's forestry ACOP (2025) and **Safetree** best practice, aligned to
the AU harvesting Codes. Standards are **verify-pointers** (copyright). Confirm current rules.

> **Not legal advice.** These are the highest-consequence tasks in the sector — only **trained,
> certified** people do them, to the current ACOP/Safetree method, validated by a competent supervisor.
> **The first and best control is to mechanise the task and remove people from the danger zone.**

---

## Eliminate first: mechanise and get people off the cutover

The single biggest advance in forestry safety is **removing people from where they get killed**:

- **Mechanised felling** (feller-bunchers, harvesters) instead of manual chainsaw felling.
- **Winch-assist / tethered machines** for **steep-slope harvesting** — machines work the slope so
  fallers and breaker-outs don't.
- **Grapple extraction** instead of manual breaking out where practicable.

Manual felling and breaking out below are for where mechanisation is not (yet) possible — and even then,
**minimise the number of people exposed and the time they're exposed**.

---

## A. Tree felling (manual)

The **deadliest task** — most forestry deaths involve a person struck by a tree, a limb, or a tree under
stored energy.

- **Only certified fallers.** Trainees fell only under the **direct supervision** of a competent faller.
- **Assess before cutting** — the tree's **lean, head weight, defects, dead limbs, and any windthrow/
  hung-up neighbours**; ground, wind and what's around (people, machines, roads, lines).
- **Plan and clear two escape routes** — roughly **45° back from the intended fall line**, cleared of
  obstacles, so the faller can retreat as the tree goes.
- **Cut to the method** — a correct **scarf (face cut)** and **back-cut** leaving an adequate **hinge**
  to control direction; never cut through the hinge.
- **Stored-energy traps:** a **"barber chair"** (a tree that splits vertically) and **spring-poles** can
  kill instantly — recognise and use the right technique.
- **Hung-up trees:** **never work or travel under, or drive another tree into, a hung-up tree** — use
  approved mechanical means to bring it down; barricade the area until it's safe.
- **Windthrow** (storm-felled tangled stems) is exceptionally dangerous — separate Safetree guidance; do
  not tackle it as ordinary felling.
- **Exclusion / safe working distance** — keep everyone outside the **no-go zone (commonly two tree
  lengths)** of a tree being felled; only the faller is in the felling area.
- **Stop in dangerous wind.**

## B. Breaking out & cable (hauler) logging

Hauling felled stems to the landing by cable — strops/chokers attached, logs dragged or flown on a
**skyline** — with the **break-out crew** (breaker-outs and a **head breaker-out**) working the ropes.

- **Treat every rope, strop and choker as lethal when under tension.** Wire ropes fail and **snap back**;
  a strop release or break can kill. Stand **clear of the bight** (the inside of any rope angle) and out
  of the **snap-back path** at all times.
- **No one under, or in line with, a suspended or moving log.** Logs roll, swing, snag and release
  suddenly.
- **Agreed signals before any pull** — clear whistle/horn/radio communication between the breaker-outs
  and the **hauler operator**; the operator pulls only on the signal; **anyone can signal STOP** and it's
  obeyed.
- **Set up the system correctly** — skyline/guylines, ropes, blocks and anchors rigged and inspected by
  competent people to the manufacturer's and ACOP requirements; tail-holds and guylines adequate.
- **Exclusion zones** around the hauler, ropes and the haul path; keep the crew in safe positions and in
  sight/communication.
- **Mechanised/grapple options** remove people from the rope danger zone — prefer them where practicable.

## C. The landing / skid

Where stems are processed, stacked and loaded onto trucks — busy, with machines, people and moving logs.

- **Rolling/sliding logs** — stable stacks, chocking, never stand below a stack or a log that could move;
  keep clear of the **fleeting/processing area**.
- **Machine–people separation** — loaders, processors and trucks working; **exclusion zones** and agreed
  movements; high-vis and eye contact; no one in a machine's blind spot. →
  `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/` + `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
- **Log-truck loading** — driver in a safe position (out of the cab clear of the loading zone unless in a
  protected position per the plan); secure the load to the transport rules.
- **Housekeeping & ground** — the landing firm, level and clear; manage slips/trips and the drop to edges.

## Critical questions to consider

- Can felling/extraction be **mechanised or winch-assisted** to remove people from the danger zone?
- Are the **faller and break-out crew certified** and supervised, and is **only the faller** in the
  felling area?
- Are **two escape routes** cleared, the **scarf/back-cut/hinge** method right, and **hung-up/windthrow**
  trees handled by approved means (never driven, never worked under)?
- In cable logging, is every **rope treated as lethal**, the **bight and snap-back path clear**, **no one
  under suspended logs**, and **signals agreed** before each pull?
- Is the **landing** controlled — stable stacks, machine–people separation, safe log-truck loading?
- Are **exclusion zones, communications and a remote-rescue plan** in place, and is felling **stopped in
  dangerous wind**?
