# High-Risk Work Specialist

## Purpose

Determine **what competency or licence a high-risk task requires** and **who is allowed to do it**,
and point to the applicable **AS/NZS standard**. Covers cranes, dogging, rigging, scaffolding,
elevating work platforms (EWP), forklifts and hoists. The answer depends heavily on **jurisdiction**,
because Australia and New Zealand handle high-risk-work competency in fundamentally different ways.

## When to use

- "What licence / ticket / competency is needed to **operate this crane / sling this load / build
  this scaffold / use this EWP**?"
- "**Who** is allowed to carry out this high-risk work?"
- Identifying the **HRWL class** (AU) or the **unit standard / Certificate of Competence** (NZ).
- Finding the **AS/NZS standard** that governs the plant or activity.

## When NOT to use

- **Building the lift/task risk assessment** (the bow tie for a crane lift) → `../critical-risk-manager/`.
- **Writing the procedure/JSA** for the work → `../sop-author/` *(planned)* / `../task-analysis-author/`
  *(planned)*.
- **The underlying duty** (who is the PCBU, overlapping duties, notifiable events) →
  `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` (NZ) / `../safework-au-specialist/` (AU).
- Detailed plant design/certification engineering — out of scope; refer to a competent engineer.

## The jurisdiction split (read this first)

| | **Australia** | **New Zealand** |
|---|---|---|
| System | National **High Risk Work Licence (HRWL)** with set **classes** (Schedule 3, model WHS Regs) | **No HRWL.** Competency shown via **NZQA unit standards**, **Certificates of Competence**, and WorkSafe **Approved Codes of Practice** |
| Recognition | Nationally recognised; valid **5 years**; issued by the state/territory regulator | Unit standards/certificates recognised nationally; holding a unit standard is **one way** of demonstrating competency, not the only way |
| Victoria | Same HRWL classes (WorkSafe Victoria) | — |

**Establish the jurisdiction first.** Do not tell an NZ operator they need an "HRWL class C6", and do
not tell an AU operator that "NZQA US 3789" is their requirement. See `references/hrwl-classes.md`
(AU) and `references/nz-competency.md` (NZ).

## Method

1. **Identify the work and the plant** — what exactly is being done (operate / sling-and-direct /
   erect-scaffold / use-EWP), with what plant and capacity.
2. **Establish the jurisdiction** (AU state/territory or NZ). If unsure, route via `../hse-advisor/`.
3. **Determine the requirement:**
   - **Australia** → the **HRWL class(es)** from `references/hrwl-classes.md` (and note training/
     assessment must be done through a registered training organisation; licences are issued by the
     regulator and valid 5 years).
   - **New Zealand** → the **unit standard(s) / Certificate of Competence / ACOP** from
     `references/nz-competency.md` (e.g. dogger/rigger minimum NZQA US 3789; certificated scaffolder
     for scaffolds with a fall of 5 m or more).
4. **Identify the governing AS/NZS standard(s)** from `references/as-nzs-standards.md`.
5. **State the residual obligations** — competency is necessary but **not sufficient**: the PCBU
   still owes the primary duty, the work still needs a risk assessment and safe system of work, and
   plant still needs to be compliant and maintained. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

AU HRWL classes are set by the **model WHS Regulations Schedule 3** and adopted by each
state/territory (including Victoria). NZ has **no equivalent licence** — never transplant HRWL class
codes onto an NZ matter, or NZ unit standards onto an AU matter. Class codes and unit-standard numbers
change; treat them as **verify-pointers** and confirm with the regulator (AU) or WorkSafe/NZQA (NZ).

## Output format

- **Competency determination** — the task → jurisdiction → required HRWL class (AU) **or** unit
  standard / Certificate of Competence / ACOP (NZ) → governing AS/NZS standard → "verify with
  regulator".
- **Who-may-do-it** statement — the role(s) authorised, and any supervision conditions.

Every output is a **draft for a competent person to confirm** against the current regulator
requirements.

## Hand-offs

- **The procedure / JSA** → `../sop-author/` *(planned)* / `../task-analysis-author/` *(planned)*.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces competency/licensing guidance — **not legal advice and not a substitute for the
regulator's current requirements**. Licence classes, unit standards and standard editions change;
always confirm the current requirement with the relevant regulator (AU state/territory; NZ WorkSafe /
NZQA) before relying on it. Holding a licence or unit standard does not by itself make work safe — a
competent person must ensure the task, plant and system of work are right.
