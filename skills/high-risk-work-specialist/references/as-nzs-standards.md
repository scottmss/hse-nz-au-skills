# AS/NZS standards for high-risk work

Key Australian/Australian-New-Zealand standards governing the design and safe use of plant and
equipment in high-risk work. Standards are **referenced** by regulations, Approved Codes of Practice
and good-practice guidance, and indicate what is reasonably practicable. **Not legal advice**; **always
confirm the current edition and whether it is called up in the relevant jurisdiction** — editions and
numbers change, and a standard is only mandatory to the extent law makes it so.

## Cranes, hoists and lifting

| Standard | Covers |
|---|---|
| **AS 1418 / AS/NZS 1418 (series)** | Cranes, hoists and winches — **design and construction** (parts by crane type) |
| **AS 2550 / AS/NZS 2550 (series)** | Cranes — **safe use** (parts by crane type) |
| **AS 4991** | Lifting devices |
| **AS 1353 / AS 3775 / AS 4497 (series)** | Flat synthetic webbing slings / chain slings / fibre-rope slings — selection and use |
| **AS 3776 / AS 2317** | Lifting components / collared eyebolts |

## How to use this

- Identify the **plant/activity**, then cite the **design** standard (how it must be built) and the
  **safe-use** standard (how it must be operated) — e.g. for a slewing mobile crane, AS 1418 (design)
  and AS 2550 (safe use).
- State the standard as a **verify-pointer**: name it, say what it covers, and tell the user to
  **confirm the current edition** and whether it is called up by the regulation/ACOP in their
  jurisdiction (NZ vs the relevant AU state/territory).
- Standards sit **alongside** the competency requirement (`hrwl-classes.md` / `nz-competency.md`) and
  the duty (`../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../../safework-au-specialist/`) — not instead of them.
