# New Zealand high-risk work competency

New Zealand has **no High Risk Work Licence system**. Competency for cranes, rigging, scaffolding,
EWPs and forklifts is demonstrated through **NZQA unit standards / national certificates**, **WorkSafe
Certificates of Competence** (where required), and the relevant **WorkSafe Approved Codes of Practice
(ACOPs)** and good-practice guidelines. **Not legal advice**; unit-standard numbers and guidance
change — treat as verify-pointers and confirm with WorkSafe / NZQA.

## The principle

- The PCBU must ensure work is done by **competent** people (part of the primary duty and management
  of risks — see `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/`).
- **Holding a unit standard or national certificate is *one way* of demonstrating competency — not
  the only way.** Competency can also be shown through verified training and experience appropriate to
  the plant and task. The duty is to ensure competency, however demonstrated.

## Cranes

- Governed by the WorkSafe **Approved Code of Practice for Cranes** (an ACOP — admissible evidence of
  good practice). It sets out operator, dogger and supervisor duties (see the Crane Operators Duties
  Matrix).
- Operators demonstrate competency through recognised crane unit standards / national certificates
  appropriate to the crane type, plus type/familiarisation on the specific crane.

## Dogging and rigging (load-lifting)

- Governed by the **Approved Code of Practice for Load-Lifting Rigging**.
- A **dogger/rigger** slinging loads and directing a crane should, at minimum, hold **NZQA Unit
  Standard 3789** — *"Sling varied regular loads and safely direct a crane during crane operations."*
- For complex loads, **NZQA Unit Standard 3801** — *"Prepare and sling complex loads for crane
  operations"* (US 3789 is a prerequisite).

## Scaffolding

- A **certificated scaffolder** (holding a current **WorkSafe Certificate of Competence** appropriate
  to the scaffold type) is required where a person could **fall 5 metres or more**. Below that height,
  the scaffold must still be erected by a competent person.
- Certificate of Competence levels: **Elementary, Intermediate, Advanced**, and **Suspended**.
- See WorkSafe's **good-practice guidelines for scaffolding** and **working at height**.

## Elevating work platforms (EWP)

- **No statutory licence.** Operators must be **trained and competent** for the EWP type (e.g. boom,
  scissor, vertical), typically via a recognised EWP operator training/certificate (industry schemes
  exist) and machine familiarisation. A harness/rescue plan is required for boom-type EWPs.

## Forklifts

- **No HRWL**, but:
  - To operate a forklift **on a road**, the operator needs an **F (forklift) endorsement** on their
    driver licence.
  - In all cases the operator must be **trained and certified competent** (recognised forklift
    operator unit standards / certificate) for the forklift type.

## Putting it together

For an NZ task: identify the activity → find the **ACOP / good-practice guideline** and the **unit
standard(s) / Certificate of Competence** that evidence competency → confirm any **endorsement**
(forklift on road) → confirm the operator is competent **on the specific machine**. Competency is
necessary but not sufficient — the PCBU still owes the primary duty and must have a safe system of
work and compliant, maintained plant.
