# Electricity supply industry (ESI) — good practice (NZ/AU)

Methodology reference for `electricity-supply-specialist`. Grounded in the **NZ ESI safety stack** —
the **Safety Manual – Electricity Industry (SM-EI)** (EEA), **NZECP 34:2001** (safe distances),
**NZECP 35:1993** (power-system earthing), the **Electricity (Safety) Regulations 2010** / Electricity
Act 1992, **AS/NZS 4836**, and (hydro) the **Building (Dam Safety) Regulations 2022** — plus **StayLive**
sector critical-risk and technical guidance. Regulator/industry guidance is summarised with
attribution; AS/NZS standards and the SM-EI are **copyright / licence-controlled — verify-pointers
only**. Confirm current editions and the network owner's rules.

> **Not legal advice; not engineering design.** A competent/authorised person validates the work-control
> system and controls against SM-EI and the asset owner's rules. Dam safety is a managed engineering
> discipline; live-line and switching are authorised work.

---

## 1. What makes the ESI different

The electricity supply industry is distinctive because the **electrical hazard is the core business, not
an incidental** — and the work is frequently **remote, weather-exposed, at height and on or near the
live network**. Safety rests on **two pillars**:

1. **A disciplined work-control system** — no one works on the network without a permit / access
   authority, a confirmed isolation, the asset earthed and proved dead (or an authorised live-line
   method), and a competency authorisation for the task and voltage.
2. **Control of the sector's physical fatal risks** — each routed to its hazard SME.

This skill owns pillar 1 and the **generation/network activities**; it routes pillar 2.

### The sector critical-risk lens (StayLive)

The NZ electricity industry's own critical-risk set is a useful screen — **driving, hazardous substances
and materials, working at height (incl. dropped objects), mobile plant and equipment, stored energy,
electricity, confined space, working around water, and mental health**. Three critical controls are
common to nearly all of them and are the highest-leverage place to focus: **work control procedures,
training and competency, and PPE**.

## 2. The ESI work-control system

### Permit / access authority (SM-EI)
- A formal system controls **who may work on which asset, when**. The isolation point is identified,
  **locked and tagged**; the asset is **earthed** and **proved dead** (test before touch); a permit is
  **issued, returned and cancelled** by authorised people.
- Treat every conductor as **live until isolated, earthed and proved dead**.

### Switching and earthing
- Planned switching by **authorised switching operators**; apply **working earths** at the worksite;
  verify with the network owner's switching schedule. **NZECP 35** for earthing of works.

### Live-line work
- Only where de-energising is **not reasonably practicable**, by **specifically authorised** workers
  using approved **glove-and-barrier or hot-stick** methods and **tested live-line tools**, under a
  **live-line permit**. Minimum approach distances per **NZECP 34** and the owner's rules.

### Competency authorisations
- Sector roles — **switching operator, permit issuer, live-line worker, cable jointer, safety observer**
  — are individually **authorised**, recorded and re-assessed. Confirm via `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## 3. Generation & network activities

- **Hydro / dams** — **dam safety** is a managed engineering discipline (dam safety assurance programme,
  surveillance, emergency action plan; **Building (Dam Safety) Regulations 2022** for classifiable dams).
  Gates, **penstocks**, intakes, and **sudden water-level/flow change**; work over/near water →
  `../../water-safety-specialist/`.
- **Geothermal** — **hydrogen sulphide (H2S)** (toxic, heavier-than-air, deadens smell at high
  concentration), high-pressure **steam and two-phase fluid**, scalding, well/pipeline integrity →
  `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.
- **Wind** — tower climbing, **blade/nacelle work**, stored mechanical/hydraulic energy, rescue at
  height → `../../working-at-height-specialist/`.
- **Solar / battery (BESS)** — **DC arc flash**, **thermal runaway and fire**, stored energy when "off"
  → `../../electrical-energy-specialist/`.
- **Substations / switchyards** — HV exposure, **SF6** insulating gas (asphyxiant; arc by-products
  toxic), **oil-filled plant**, **step-and-touch potential**, legacy **asbestos**.
- **Distribution** — poles, overhead and underground, **cable jointing** (proving, spiking),
  **vegetation/tree work near lines**, and managing **public / third-party** contact with the network.

## 5. Critical questions to consider

- Is the asset **isolated, earthed and proved dead** for the scope of work — or is this an **authorised
  live-line** task under permit?
- Is there a **permit / access authority** in place, and is everyone authorised for their role?
- How were **all sources** of supply identified (including back-feed, generation, embedded solar/BESS)?
- For **live-line / close approach** — correct **minimum approach distance** for the voltage, tested
  tools, authorised workers?
- For **generation** — dam-safety surveillance current? **H2S** monitoring and rescue for geothermal?
  **DC / thermal-runaway** controls for BESS?
- Is the **public** kept clear of the network and the worksite?
- Are **rescue** arrangements (height, confined space, water, electrical) available and rehearsed?
