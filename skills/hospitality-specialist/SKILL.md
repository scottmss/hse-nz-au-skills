# Hospitality Specialist (hospitality & accommodation SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **health & safety in hospitality** — restaurants,
cafés, **bars/pubs/clubs**, **hotels/motels and accommodation**, catering and fast food. There is **no
separate regime** — it runs under the **general HSWA/WHS duties** — and its defining feature is a
**young, casual, part-time, high-turnover workforce** (so training is easily rushed) in a
**high-frequency-injury** setting: **burns, knives and slips** dominate, with **occupational violence**
and **psychosocial harm (including sexual harassment)** as the serious tail, especially in **licensed,
late-night** venues. Owns the hospitality view, the young-worker frame and the common injuries; routes
the deeper hazard methods to the relevant SME.

## When to use

- Any **hospitality / food-service / accommodation** workforce — restaurants, cafés, **bars/pubs/clubs**,
  **hotels/motels**, catering, fast food/QSR, takeaways, event catering.
- The sector's common injuries — **burns and hot surfaces**, **knives and cuts**, **slips/trips on wet
  floors**, and **manual handling** (kegs, stock, housekeeping) (flagship — `references/good-practice.md`).
- The **young-worker** induction/supervision challenge and the **licensed-venue** violence/alcohol frame.

## When NOT to use

- **Food *manufacturing*** (abattoirs, meatworks, factories, dairy/seafood processing) →
  `../food-processing-specialist/` (this skill is food *service* + accommodation).
- The **deeper hazard method** belongs to its SME (this skill routes to them, in the hospitality context):
  - **Occupational violence & aggression** (intoxicated/aggressive patrons, robbery, lone close-up,
    glassings) → `../violence-aggression-specialist/`.
  - **Psychosocial hazards** — long/unsocial hours, high demands, bullying, **sexual harassment**,
    customer abuse → `../psychosocial-risk-specialist/`.
  - **Cleaning/sanitising chemicals (dishwasher, degreaser, caustic) and SDS** →

    isolation** → `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
  - **Coolrooms/freezers as confined spaces / entrapment** → `../confined-space-specialist/`; **delivery
    driving** → `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.
- **Who holds the duty / notifiable events** → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## The hospitality risk picture

Hospitality is **fast, hot, wet, busy and customer-facing**, staffed largely by **young and casual**
people working **late nights and weekends** with **high turnover** — so health-and-safety induction is
easily rushed. The harm is mostly **high-frequency** with a **violence/psychosocial tail**:

1. **Burns & hot surfaces** — fryers and **hot oil**, grills, ovens, stovetops, **steam and hot
   liquids/fat**, hot plates and pass; the signature kitchen injury.
2. **Knives & cuts** — kitchen knives, **slicers/mandolines**, broken glass behind the bar.
3. **Slips, trips & falls** — wet and greasy floors and spills are a leading hospitality injury.
4. **Manual handling** — **kegs**, stock and deliveries (food & beverage); **housekeeping** (beds,
   linen, repetitive room servicing) is the dominant injury in the **accommodation** subsector.
5. **Occupational violence & psychosocial** — **aggressive/intoxicated patrons**, **robbery** and
   **lone close-up** in licensed/late-night venues; and **psychosocial harm** — unsocial hours, pace,
   bullying and **sexual harassment** (a recognised hospitality risk) → the violence and psychosocial SMEs.
6. **Plus** cleaning chemicals, machinery (slicers/mixers/glass washers), **gas/LPG** cooking and fryer
   fires, coolroom/freezer **entrapment** and cold, noise (music venues), electrical-in-wet, and fatigue.

## The young-worker frame (own this)

A large share of the workforce is **young, new and casual**, often in a **first job**, working at
**pace** under **high turnover** — the conditions in which training gets skipped and people don't speak
up. Treat **induction and supervision as a primary control**:

- **Induct before the first shift** — the real hazards (hot oil, knives, slips, what to do if a patron is
  aggressive), not just a sign-here form; don't let pace or turnover erode it.
- **Supervise and buddy** new and young workers; make it **safe and expected to ask** and to stop if
  unsure; clear escalation for an aggressive customer.
- **Match task to competence and age** (e.g. equipment, late-night lone work, alcohol service), and
  refresh as people churn.

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction** (NZ/AU) and the **operation** (kitchen/restaurant, bar/licensed venue,
   accommodation/housekeeping, catering, QSR) — operation drives the mix.
2. **Get induction & supervision right** for a young/casual workforce (owned here).
3. **Control the common injuries** — burns/hot surfaces, knives, **wet-floor slips**, manual handling.
4. **Address the violence/psychosocial tail** — licensed-venue aggression and robbery →
   violence SME; hours, bullying and **sexual harassment** → psychosocial SME.
5. **Route the rest** — chemicals, machinery, coolroom confined space, delivery driving.
6. **State residual obligations** — the PCBU owes the primary duty; competent supervision, training that
   isn't rushed, and a risk plan are required. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**NZ:** **HSWA 2015** general duties (no separate hospitality regime), regulated by **WorkSafe NZ**, with
industry guidance for **cafés and restaurants**, **hotels and accommodation services**, **worker
accommodation**, and hospitality-sector risk-factor research. **AU:** the **model WHS** primary duty
(Victoria OHS Act 2004; WA), with **state WHS** accommodation-and-food-services guidance, the model Code
**_Sexual and gender-based harassment_** and the psychosocial Regs (→ `../psychosocial-risk-specialist/`),
and state Respect-at-Work / hospitality resources (NSW added sexual-harassment/violence content to **RSA**
training). Jurisdiction variations → `../safework-au-specialist/`. Confirm current editions via
`../hse-advisor/`.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice hospitality-safety guidance — **not legal advice and not a substitute
for a competent person or the current HSWA/WHS requirements**, which change. Burns, knife cuts, slips,
and patron violence and harassment cause real harm — and a **young, casual workforce** needs induction
and supervision that pace and turnover must not erode. Validate every control against the actual venue,
tasks and people before relying on it.
