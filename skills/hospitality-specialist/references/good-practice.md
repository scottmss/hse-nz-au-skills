# Hospitality — good practice, common injuries & the young-worker frame

Detailed good-practice controls, the risk profile and named guidance for the hospitality workforce —
restaurants, cafés, bars/pubs/clubs, hotels/motels/accommodation, catering and fast food. Generic and
company-agnostic, distilled from WorkSafe NZ hospitality guidance and the Australian model WHS framework.
**Not legal advice**; guidance changes — treat references as verify-pointers and confirm against the
current rules and a competent person.

> No bespoke regime — the **general HSWA/WHS primary duty** applies, managed **SFAIRP**. The value here is
> the **risk profile** of a young/casual/high-turnover workforce and the controls that bite — most of the
> harm is **high-frequency** (burns, cuts, slips), with a serious **violence/psychosocial** tail.
>
> **Boundary:** **food *manufacturing*** (abattoirs, factories, dairy/seafood processing) is
> `../../food-processing-specialist/`. This file owns food *service* + accommodation.

## The young-worker frame (the lens for everything below)

Hospitality is staffed largely by **young, casual, part-time** workers — often a **first job** — working
**late nights and weekends** with **high turnover**, which means **induction and training are easily
rushed or skipped**. So the first control is the **system around the worker**:

- **Induct before the first shift** on the *real* hazards (hot oil, knives, wet floors, what to do with an
  aggressive patron) — not just a sign-here form.
- **Supervise and buddy** new/young workers; make it **safe and expected to ask** and to **stop if
  unsure**; give a clear way to escalate a difficult customer.
- **Match the task to competence and age** (equipment, lone late-night work, alcohol service); re-induct
  as people churn.

## The common injuries (and good-practice control)

### Burns & hot surfaces
- Fryers and **hot oil**, grills, ovens, stovetops, **steam and hot liquids/fat**, hot plates/pass.
- Controls: safe layouts and clearances; **don't overfill/move hot oil when hot**; lids/splash guards;
  heat-resistant gloves/cloths; "hot behind/hot pan" call-outs; no rushing through the pass; first-aid for
  burns; manage **fryer oil change/disposal** when cool.

### Knives & cuts
- Kitchen knives, **slicers/mandolines**, and **broken glass** behind the bar.
- Controls: **sharp, well-maintained knives** and correct technique; bull-nose over pointed knives where
  practicable; **guards on slicers**, fixed to the bench; magnetic strip/safe storage; cut-resistant glove
  for mandoline/cleaning blades; safe glass handling and quick clean-up of breakages; train every worker.

### Slips, trips & falls
- **Wet and greasy floors** and spills — a leading hospitality injury.
- Controls: **clean up spills immediately**, drainage and anti-slip surfaces, **slip-resistant footwear**,
  good lighting, clear walkways, and "wet floor" signage; manage ice/condensation around coolrooms.

### Manual handling
- **Kegs**, stock, deliveries and cellar work (bar); **housekeeping** (beds, linen, repetitive room
  servicing) is the **dominant injury in the accommodation subsector**.
- Controls: trolleys and aids, two-person/keg lifts, delivery placement, realistic room quotas and
  rotation, and task/workstation design.

## The violence & psychosocial tail

### Occupational violence (→ `../../violence-aggression-specialist/`)
- **Aggressive/intoxicated patrons**, **robbery** (cash, late trading), **lone open/close**, and
  **glassings** in licensed/late-night venues.
- Owned by the violence SME; hospitality context: crowd/RSA management, security, cash-handling design,
  lone-worker/duress systems, safe close-up, and post-incident support.

### Psychosocial, incl. sexual harassment (→ `../../psychosocial-risk-specialist/`)
- **Unsocial hours**, pace and high demands, bullying, **customer abuse**, and **sexual harassment** — a
  recognised hospitality risk (close customer interaction, alcohol, late-night shifts, busy/confined
  spaces).
- Controls: manage as a **work-health hazard** (not just conduct) — design, policy, reporting, training,
  bystander action and leadership; in AU see the model Code *Sexual and gender-based harassment*.

## Critical questions to consider

- Which **operation** (kitchen/restaurant, bar/licensed venue, accommodation/housekeeping, catering, QSR)?
- **Young/casual workforce:** is there **real induction before the first shift**, supervision/buddying,
  and is it **safe to ask/stop** — not eroded by pace or turnover?
- **Burns:** hot-oil handling, layouts, guards and gloves controlled?
- **Knives:** sharp knives, technique, slicer guards, safe storage, training?
- **Slips:** immediate spill clean-up, anti-slip floors and footwear, signage?
- **Manual handling:** kegs/stock aids; realistic **housekeeping** quotas and rotation?
- **Violence/psychosocial:** patron aggression, robbery and **lone close-up** controlled; **sexual
  harassment** managed as a work-health hazard? → violence / psychosocial SMEs.
