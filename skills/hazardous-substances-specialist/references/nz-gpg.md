# Hazardous substances & asbestos — New Zealand regulatory & GPG anchor

The **NZ-specific** layer for `hazardous-substances-specialist`: the two-regulator structure, what
WorkSafe NZ points duty-holders to, the thresholds that trigger duties, and the exposure standards. The
hierarchy-of-controls method is jurisdiction-neutral and lives in `good-practice.md`; this file is the
NZ anchor for it.

> figure below as a **verify-pointer** — confirm the current value before relying on it. Deep statutory
> duty / approval / licensing detail lives in `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.
>
> **Sources last verified: 2026-06-30.**

## The NZ frame — two regulators, and asbestos sits apart

NZ splits hazardous-substances regulation across **two regulators** — get the right one for the question:

- **EPA (Environmental Protection Authority) — HSNO.** The EPA **approves** a substance under the
  Hazardous Substances and New Organisms Act and sets its **HSNO classification** and the controls in the
  approval (before it can be imported/manufactured/used).
- **WorkSafe NZ — the workplace regs.** WorkSafe enforces the **Health and Safety at Work (Hazardous
  Substances) Regulations 2017** in the workplace: inventory, SDS, signage, segregation, **certified
  handlers**, **location test certificates**, tracking and emergency management.
- **Asbestos is a separate regime** — **not** HSNO/EPA. It is governed by the **Health and Safety at Work
  (Asbestos) Regulations 2016** and WorkSafe's asbestos licensing system (below).

## Key NZ thresholds & duties (the 2017 regs)

- **Inventory + SDS** for every hazardous substance held; **GHS labelling and signage**.
- **Certified handler** — required to handle certain higher-hazard substances (e.g. acutely toxic, and
  substances needing a **controlled substance licence**). The 2017-regs term; replaced the old HSNO
  "approved handler".
- **Location (compliance) test certificates** — required where substances are stored/used above
  threshold quantities; plus **secondary containment/bunding**, emergency-management and signage duties
  that switch on at defined quantities.
- **Tracked substances** — a subset must be tracked through their lifecycle.
- Confirm which duties apply to *your* substances and quantities via the Toolbox and
  `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.

## Exposure standards — WES vs PES (an NZ distinction)

- A **WES (Workplace Exposure Standard)** is **advisory** — the airborne concentration most workers can
  be exposed to without harm; you still control **SFAIRP** and below the WES.
- A **Prescribed Exposure Standard (PES)** is a **mandatory** limit (set via a safe work instrument under
  the General Risk and Workplace Management Regs 2016 / specific regs) that **must not be exceeded**.
- **Respirable crystalline silica (RCS):** the NZ **WES-TWA is 0.025 mg/m³** (lowered from 0.05 in
  November 2023; itself down from 0.1 in 2019). Significant risk remains *at* the WES — control to as low
  as reasonably practicable, don't treat the number as "safe". **Verify the current value before use.**

## Asbestos — the NZ licensing & removal specifics

- **Licensed removal classes:** **Class A** licence = any amount of **friable** *and* non-friable
  asbestos; **Class B** = **non-friable only** (a licence is needed to remove **more than 10 m²** of
  non-friable ACM).
- **Notify WorkSafe at least 5 days before** licensed asbestos removal work (HSW (Asbestos) Regs 2016,
  **reg 34** — verify), via the licensed-removal notification form. Emergencies may start immediately.
- **Clearance / air monitoring:** a **licensed asbestos assessor** must do air monitoring and issue the
  **clearance certificate** for **Class A (friable)** sites; a **Class B** site may be cleared by an
  **independent competent person**.
- **Asbestos register + management plan** for any workplace that may contain ACM; don't disturb —
  identify (survey or assume), label and manage.

## How NZ differs from AU (quick contrast)

- **NZ:** two regulators (**EPA/HSNO** approval + **WorkSafe** workplace regs); asbestos under the **2016
  Regs**; **engineered stone** managed under the **general** hazardous-substances/dust duties with a
  lowered RCS WES — **no outright product ban**.
- **AU:** model **Code "Managing risks of hazardous chemicals in the workplace"** + the asbestos Codes
  (Victoria differs), and a **prohibition on the manufacture, supply, processing and installation of
  engineered stone from 1 July 2024**. Confirm via `../../safework-au-specialist/`.
