# Hazardous substances & asbestos — good practice, regulation & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, governing regulation, qualifications and critical questions for
hazardous substances/chemicals and asbestos. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from the NZ HSW
(Hazardous Substances) and (Asbestos) Regulations and WorkSafe guidance, and the AU model Codes. **Not
legal advice**; regulations and SDS change — treat references as verify-pointers and rely on the
**current SDS** for any specific substance.

## Governing regulation & guidance

- **NZ — chemicals:** **Health and Safety at Work (Hazardous Substances) Regulations 2017**. Uses
  **GHS** classification (with HSNO); requires an **inventory**, accessible **SDS**, labelling/signage,
  **certified handlers** for certain substances, segregation, secondary containment, emergency
  management, and (for some substances/locations) **compliance/location test certificates**. EPA sets
  approvals/classifications.
- **NZ — asbestos:** **Health and Safety at Work (Asbestos) Regulations 2016** — asbestos management
  plans, licensed removal, and licensed assessors.
- **AU — chemicals:** model **Code of Practice "Managing risks of hazardous chemicals in the
  workplace"** — **GHS** (3rd revised edition), **SDS**, register, **manifest** and **placarding** for
  threshold quantities, health monitoring.
- **AU — asbestos:** model Codes **"How to manage and control asbestos in the workplace"** and **"How
  to safely remove asbestos"** (adopted per jurisdiction; Victoria differs).

## The foundations (get these right first)

- **Inventory** — know every hazardous substance on site, its quantity and location.
- **SDS** — a current Safety Data Sheet for every hazardous substance, **accessible to everyone who
  could be exposed** (workers and emergency services). The SDS drives the controls.
- **Classification & labelling (GHS)** — substances classified and labelled; signage at storage.

## Critical controls (hierarchy order)

### Eliminate / substitute (most effective)
- Remove the substance from the task, or **substitute a less hazardous alternative** that is fit for
  purpose. (For asbestos: do not disturb; manage or remove under the asbestos regime.)

### Isolate / engineering
- **Enclose or isolate** the process; **local exhaust ventilation (LEV)** at the source.
- **Segregate** incompatible substances by class; **secondary containment / bunding** (e.g. ≥ 110% for
  bulk liquids); drains isolated before filling.
- **Dust controls** — on-tool extraction and **wet methods**, **never dry-cut** (critical for
  **respirable crystalline silica** — incl. **engineered stone**, fibre-cement, concrete, brick/block —
  and asbestos). **AU prohibits engineered stone (manufacture/supply/processing/installation) from 1 Jul
  2024**; prefer low-/zero-silica substitution. NZ manages RCS under the general dust/substance duties.
- **Ignition-source control** for flammables; appropriate fire protection.

### Administrative
- Procedures for **use, storage, transport and disposal** per the SDS; correct labelling and signage.
- **Exposure monitoring** where needed, and **health monitoring** for relevant substances (e.g. lead,
  isocyanates, silica, asbestos).
- **Spill/emergency response** (kit on site, trained people, reporting) and fire precautions.
- Training and supervision; restrict access.

### PPE (last line)
- Gloves, eye protection, coveralls, and **respiratory protective equipment (RPE)** selected for the
  substance and **fit-tested**; inspected before use. PPE protects one person and depends on correct use.

## Asbestos — the special regime

- **Identify** asbestos (survey by a competent person, or assume present in pre-2000 materials);
  maintain an **asbestos register** and an **asbestos management plan**; **label** and **do not
  disturb** known/presumed ACM.
- **Removal is licensed work:**
  - **Class A** licence — **friable** asbestos (and any friable work).
  - **Class B** licence — **non-friable** asbestos (NZ: removal of **more than 10 m²** of non-friable
    ACM requires a Class B licence).
- Removal controls: **negative-pressure enclosure** (friable), **wet methods**, restricted area,
  decontamination, and proper **waste disposal**.
- **Air monitoring & clearance** by a **licensed asbestos assessor** (Class A: independent assessor).
- AU mirrors the Class A/B licence + asbestos assessor model.

> Asbestos exposure causes irreversible, often fatal disease decades later. When in doubt, **assume
> asbestos** and manage accordingly.

## Qualifications & competency

- A **competent person** to assess and control hazardous substances; users trained on the substance and
  its SDS controls.
- NZ **certified handler** for higher-hazard substances (e.g. acutely toxic 6.1A/6.1B, controlled-
  substance-licence substances); compliance/location test certificates where required →
  `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.
- **Asbestos removalists** licensed (Class A/B); **asbestos assessors** licensed; supervisors competent.
- **RPE users fit-tested** and trained.

## Critical questions to consider

- Do we have a complete **inventory** and a current, accessible **SDS** for every hazardous substance?
- Can the substance be **eliminated or substituted**?
- Are substances **classified, labelled, segregated and contained**, with ventilation/dust controls at
  source?
- Is **exposure** (and where required **health**) monitored? Is **RPE** the right type and **fit-tested**?
- Are **emergency/spill** response and fire precautions in place and known?
- Is a **certified handler / compliance certificate** required (NZ)?
- For **asbestos**: is it identified, registered and in a management plan; is removal **licensed**
  (Class A/B) with assessor **clearance**?
