# Hazardous Substances Specialist (chemicals & asbestos SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for hazardous substances and chemicals — preventing
**exposure** (toxicity, respiratory disease, sensitisation), **fire/explosion**, and **reactive/
corrosive** harm, across storage, handling, use and disposal. Covers **asbestos** as a distinct,
heavily-regulated subset (management and licensed removal). Provides the good-practice controls, the
**inventory/SDS/GHS** foundations, the **competency/licensing** required, and the governing NZ/AU
regulations.

## When to use

- Storing, handling, using or disposing of **hazardous chemicals/substances**.
- Building a **hazardous-substances inventory**, sourcing/using **SDS**, or understanding **GHS**
  classification.
- Managing **exposure** to fumes, vapours, dusts (incl. **respirable crystalline silica**) or
  contaminated materials.
- **Asbestos** — identification, management plans, and (licensed) removal.

## When NOT to use

- A substance hazard **inside a confined space** (atmosphere) → also `../confined-space-specialist/`.
- **Hot work / flammable-atmosphere ignition** as a stored-energy matter → `../critical-risk-manager/`
  + duty specialists.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/` (this SME supplies controls/questions).
- The **legal duty / notifiable / approvals** detail → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` /
  `../safework-au-specialist/`.

## The risk

- **Exposure** — acute poisoning, chronic disease (e.g. occupational asthma, silicosis, **asbestos-
  related disease/mesothelioma**, cancers), skin/eye damage, sensitisation.
- **Fire / explosion** from flammables; **reactive/corrosive** incidents; **uncontrolled release/spill**
  to people or the environment.

## Critical controls — apply the hierarchy

See `references/good-practice.md` for detail.

1. **Eliminate / substitute** — remove the substance, or substitute a **less hazardous** alternative
   fit for purpose (the most effective control). For asbestos, **do not disturb** unless managed/removed
   under the asbestos regime.
2. **Isolate / engineering** — enclose or **isolate** the process; **local exhaust ventilation**;
   substance segregation by class; **secondary containment/bunding**; on-tool dust extraction and **wet
   methods** for dusts; ignition-source control for flammables.
3. **Administrative** — a **hazardous-substances inventory** and accessible **Safety Data Sheets
   (SDS)**; correct **GHS labelling and signage**; safe procedures for use/storage/transport/disposal
   per the SDS; exposure **monitoring** and (where relevant) **health monitoring**; spill/emergency
   response and fire precautions; training.
4. **PPE** — gloves, eye protection, coveralls, and **respiratory protective equipment** selected and
   fit-tested for the substance and exposure (last line; depends on correct use).

**Asbestos (special regime):** identify (survey/assume), maintain an **asbestos register and
management plan**, label and don't disturb; removal is **licensed work** with negative-pressure
enclosure/wet methods, decontamination, and **air monitoring & clearance** by a licensed assessor. See
`references/good-practice.md`.

## Qualifications & competency

- A **competent person** to assess and control; users trained for the substance and its SDS controls.
- NZ: a **certified handler** is required for certain higher-hazard substances (e.g. acutely toxic
  6.1A/6.1B and controlled-substance-licence substances); some substances/locations need **compliance/
  location test certificates**. Confirm via `../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.
- **Asbestos:** licensed removalist — **Class A** (friable) or **Class B** (non-friable, NZ: > 10 m²);
  air monitoring/clearance by a **licensed asbestos assessor** (Class A independent). AU mirrors this
  (Class A/B licence + asbestos assessor).
- RPE users **fit-tested** and trained.

## Jurisdiction note

NZ: **Health and Safety at Work (Hazardous Substances) Regulations 2017** (inventory, SDS, GHS,
certified handlers, signage, segregation, emergency management; EPA approvals/HSNO classification) and
the **Health and Safety at Work (Asbestos) Regulations 2016** (management plans, licensed removal,
assessors). AU: model **Code "Managing risks of hazardous chemicals in the workplace"** (GHS, SDS,
register/manifest, placarding) and the asbestos Codes **"How to manage and control asbestos"** + **"How
to safely remove asbestos"** (adopted per jurisdiction; Victoria differs). Treat as verify-pointers;
confirm via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

A **hazardous-substance control plan** (inventory & SDS → classification/labelling → storage &
segregation → exposure controls (eliminate/substitute/ventilate/contain) → emergency/spill → health
monitoring → competency → critical questions), or — for asbestos — a **management-plan / removal-
control** outline. A draft for a competent person to validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice guidance and structure — **not legal advice and not a substitute for
a competent person, the SDS, or the current regulations**. Substance hazards (especially asbestos and
carcinogens) are heavily regulated and can cause irreversible disease; a competent person must validate
the classification, controls, monitoring and (for asbestos) licensing against the actual substances and
work before it proceeds.
