# Manufacturing Specialist (factory & processing SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **health & safety in manufacturing and processing** —
the factory **production system** and the risks that define it: the **catastrophic** (process safety,
major hazard facilities, combustible dust and fire/explosion) and the **chronic** (occupational noise,
hazardous manual tasks). Owns the manufacturing-system view and those sector risks, and routes the
machine-level and chemical hazards to the relevant SME.

## When to use

- **Process safety / major hazard facilities** — large quantities of hazardous chemicals, the **MHF
  safety-case** regime, loss of containment, fire/explosion/toxic release (flagship —
  `references/process-safety-and-dust.md`).
- **Combustible dust** — flour, sugar, wood, metal, plastics dust; **hazardous-area** classification and
  dust-explosion prevention (flagship).
- **Occupational noise** — the signature manufacturing health risk; noise-induced hearing loss.
- **Hazardous manual tasks / ergonomics** — repetitive production-line work, sprains/strains.
- The **production system** — machine lines, conveyors, **robotics/automation**, hot work/molten metal,
  shift work and fatigue, and plant design/pre-start (commissioning).

## When NOT to use

- The **physical/specific hazard detail** belongs to its SME (this skill routes to them):
  - **Guarding a machine / isolation & lockout (LOTO)** → `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
  - **The chemicals themselves / SDS / welding fume / asbestos** → `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.
  - **Forklifts & internal traffic** → `../forklift-specialist/` + `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.
  - **Silos, vessels, tanks (confined spaces)** → `../confined-space-specialist/`; **electrical/arc flash**
    → `../electrical-energy-specialist/`.
- **Who holds the duty / notifiable events** → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## The manufacturing risk picture

A factory concentrates **acute** and **chronic** risk in one place:

- **Acute** — machinery contact (entanglement/crush → `../machinery-safety-specialist/`), **fire and
  explosion** (combustible dust, flammable liquids/gases), **loss of containment** at a major hazard
  facility, hot work/molten metal **burns**, and internal **mobile-plant** strikes.
- **Chronic (the quiet majority of harm)** — **noise** (hearing loss), **hazardous manual tasks**
  (musculoskeletal injury — the most common manufacturing injury), and **exposure** to fume/dust/mist
  (→ `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`).

This skill owns the **production-system view**, **process safety/MHF**, **combustible dust**, **noise**
and **manual tasks**; it routes machine guarding and the chemicals to their SMEs.

## Catastrophic risks — process safety, MHF & combustible dust (flagship)

- **Major Hazard Facilities (MHF)** — sites holding **hazardous chemicals above threshold quantities**
  are a **licensed, safety-case regime**: **NZ HSW (Major Hazard Facilities) Regulations 2016**; **AU
  WHS Regulations (major hazard facilities, Schedule 15 chemicals)**. Loss of containment → fire,
  explosion, toxic release affecting workers and the community.
- **Combustible dust** — many manufacturing dusts (flour, sugar, wood, metal, plastics) **explode** when
  suspended and ignited; manage **hazardous areas**, ignition control, housekeeping (no dust
  accumulation), and explosion protection/venting.
- Full detail (including the dust-explosion pentagon and process-safety management) in
  `references/process-safety-and-dust.md`.

## Chronic risks — noise & hazardous manual tasks (owned here)

- **Noise** — design out at source first (quieter plant, enclosure, isolation), then administrative
  controls, then hearing protection as the **last** line; manage to the **exposure standard** (commonly
  **85 dB(A) over 8 h and 140 dB(C) peak**) with monitoring and audiometry.
- **Hazardous manual tasks** — design the task and workstation to remove force, repetition and awkward
  posture (mechanical aids, automation, job rotation, layout); the **most common source of manufacturing
  injury**.

## Other production-system controls

- **Machine lines, conveyors, robotics/automation** — guarding, interlocks and **isolation/LOTO** at
  scale → `../machinery-safety-specialist/`; safe integration, collaborative-robot risk assessment, and
  exclusion during maintenance.
- **Hot work / molten metal / foundries** — burns, fire, fume.
- **Shift work & fatigue**, amenities, and a **competent, inducted workforce**.
- **Plant design & pre-start (commissioning)** and **purchasing** — design out hazards upstream (safe
  design / the designer-supplier duties).

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction** (NZ/AU) and the operation (discrete vs process manufacturing; is it an MHF?).
2. **Screen for catastrophic risk first** — MHF status, flammable/combustible inventories, combustible
   dust → flagship controls and the safety-case obligations.
3. **Manage the chronic risks** — noise and hazardous manual tasks (owned here).
4. **Route the machine and chemical hazards** to their SME (guarding/LOTO, substances/fume, traffic,
   confined space, electrical).
5. **Run the production-system controls** — automation/robotics, hot work, shift/fatigue, plant pre-start.
6. **State residual obligations** — the PCBU owes the primary duty; competency, supervision and a risk
   register/plan are required. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**NZ:** **HSWA 2015** with the **HSW (Major Hazard Facilities) Regulations 2016** (safety-case regime),
the **HSW (General Risk and Workplace Management) Regulations** (incl. the **noise exposure standard**),
WorkSafe **manufacturing / noise / manual-handling** guidance, and the **HSW (Hazardous Substances)
Regulations 2017** for flammables/dust (→ `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`). **AU:** the **WHS
Regulations — major hazard facilities (Schedule 15)**, the model Codes **_Managing noise and preventing
hearing loss at work_**, **_Hazardous manual tasks_**, **_Managing risks of plant in the workplace_**,
and **_Managing the risks of hazardous chemicals_** (combustible dust / hazardous areas). Jurisdiction
variations (Victoria OHS Act, WA) → `../safework-au-specialist/`. Confirm current editions via
`../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

Either a **manufacturing safety plan / risk screen** (operation → catastrophic risk (MHF/dust) →
chronic risk (noise/manual tasks) → routed machine/chemical hazards → production-system controls →
critical questions), or the **manufacturing-specific controls and questions** to feed into a bow tie
(`../critical-risk-manager/`), task analysis (`../task-analysis-author/`) or SOP (`../sop-author/`). A
draft for a competent person to validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice manufacturing-safety guidance — **not legal advice and not a
substitute for a competent person or the current MHF/safety-case, noise, manual-task and chemicals
requirements**, which change. Major-hazard and combustible-dust controls require **competent process-
safety engineering**; validate every control against the actual plant, substances, processes and people
before relying on it.
