# Manufacturing & processing safety — good practice (NZ/AU)

Methodology reference for `manufacturing-specialist`. Grounded in **NZ HSWA 2015 + the HSW (Major
Hazard Facilities) Regulations 2016 + WorkSafe manufacturing/noise guidance** and the **AU WHS
Regulations (major hazard facilities) + model Codes (noise, hazardous manual tasks, plant, hazardous
chemicals)**. Government/regulator guidance is summarised with attribution; AS/NZS standards are
**copyright — verify-pointers only**. Confirm current editions.

> **Not legal advice; not process-safety engineering.** A competent person validates controls; major-
> hazard and combustible-dust controls require competent process-safety engineering.

---

## 1. Why manufacturing is its own sector

A factory **concentrates acute and chronic risk in one place** and runs it continuously:

- **Acute** — machinery contact, **fire and explosion** (combustible dust, flammables), **loss of
  containment** at a major hazard facility, hot work/molten-metal burns, internal mobile-plant strikes.
- **Chronic (the quiet majority of harm)** — **noise** (hearing loss), **hazardous manual tasks**
  (musculoskeletal injury — the most common manufacturing injury), and **exposure** to fume/dust/mist.

This skill owns the **production-system view**, **process safety/MHF**, **combustible dust**, **noise**
and **manual tasks**; it routes machine guarding and the chemicals to their SMEs. The strongest controls
are **upstream** — **safe design, purchasing and plant pre-start** remove hazards before the line runs.

---

## 2. The catastrophic risks → flagship

**Process safety, Major Hazard Facilities and combustible dust** — see `process-safety-and-dust.md`. Screen
for these **first**: an MHF or a combustible-dust hazard is a low-frequency / catastrophic-consequence
risk that a routine risk assessment can miss.

---

## 3. Noise (own — the signature manufacturing health risk)

- **Design out at source first** — quieter plant/processes, enclosure, damping, isolation, distance;
  then **administrative** controls (limit exposure time, rotate, signage/zones); **hearing protection is
  the last line**, not the first.
- **Manage to the exposure standard** — commonly **85 dB(A) LAeq over 8 hours and 140 dB(C) peak**;
  **monitor noise**, identify over-exposed roles, and provide **audiometry** (hearing tests).
- Noise-induced hearing loss is **permanent and preventable** — treat it as a real risk, not a nuisance.

---

## 4. Hazardous manual tasks / ergonomics (own)

- The **most common source of manufacturing injury** (sprains/strains, musculoskeletal disorders) — and
  largely **designed in**.
- Control by **task and workstation design**: remove or reduce **force, repetition, awkward/sustained
  posture, and vibration**; use **mechanical aids/automation**, adjust heights/reach, rotate jobs, and
  design the line so the work fits the person.

---

## 5. The production system

- **Machine lines, conveyors, presses, robotics/automation** — guarding, interlocks and **isolation/LOTO
  at scale** → `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`. Integrate machines safely, risk-assess
  **collaborative robots**, and enforce **exclusion during maintenance/clearing**.
- **Hot work / molten metal / foundries** — burns, fire, and fume; permits and shielding.
- **Hazardous substances** — process chemicals, solvents, **welding fume**, metalworking fluids, dusts →
  `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/` (and confined-space entry to vessels/silos →
  `../../confined-space-specialist/`).
- **Shift work & fatigue**, amenities, and a **competent, inducted workforce** (incl. labour-hire/temps).
- **Upstream duties** — **safe design**, **purchasing** specifications, and **plant pre-start/
  commissioning** so hazards are removed before production.

## 7. Critical questions to consider

- Is the site a **Major Hazard Facility** or near a threshold, and are **combustible-dust** hazards
  identified and controlled? (→ flagship)
- Is **noise** designed out at source and managed to the **exposure standard**, with monitoring and
  audiometry?
- Are **hazardous manual tasks** designed out (force/repetition/posture), not just "lift correctly"?
- Are **machines guarded and isolated (LOTO)**, robots risk-assessed, and maintenance exclusion enforced?
- Are **chemicals/fume**, **internal traffic**, **confined spaces** and **electrical** risks routed and
  controlled? (links above)
- Were hazards designed out **upstream** (safe design, purchasing, plant pre-start)?
- Could an event be **notifiable**? → `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../../safework-au-specialist/`.

- **NZ:** **HSWA 2015**; **HSW (Major Hazard Facilities) Regulations 2016**; **HSW (General Risk and
  Workplace Management) Regulations** (noise exposure standard); WorkSafe **manufacturing / noise /
  manual-handling** guidance; **HSW (Hazardous Substances) Regulations 2017** (flammables/dust).

  preventing hearing loss at work_**, **_Hazardous manual tasks_**, **_Managing risks of plant in the
  workplace_**, **_Managing the risks of hazardous chemicals_** (combustible dust / hazardous areas).
  Jurisdiction variations → `../../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Machine-level and chemical standards live with their SME. **AS/NZS standards are copyright — verify-
  pointers only.** Confirm current editions before relying on any figure or limit.
