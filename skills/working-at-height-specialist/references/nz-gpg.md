# Working at height — New Zealand regulatory & GPG anchor

The **NZ-specific** layer for `working-at-height-specialist`: what WorkSafe NZ actually points
duty-holders to, the thresholds that matter, and who is competent. The hierarchy-of-controls method is
jurisdiction-neutral and lives in `good-practice.md`; this file is the NZ anchor for it.

> **Not legal advice.** Guidance and standards change. Treat every document below as a **verify-pointer**
> — confirm the current edition before relying on it. Deep statutory duty detail lives in
> `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.
>
> **Sources last verified: 2026-06-30.**

## The NZ frame — manage *every* fall, no minimum height

- Falls are governed by the **HSWA 2015** primary duty: a PCBU must ensure health and safety **so far as
  is reasonably practicable (SFAIRP)**. There is **no height threshold that switches the duty on.**
- **The "three-metre rule" is a myth.** It came from the old **HSE Regulations 1995 reg 21** and is
  frequently misread as "no controls needed under 3 m". That is wrong: WorkSafe NZ is explicit that
  **any fall that could cause harm must be actively managed** — falls from **under 1.8 m** routinely cause
  serious injury. Do not let a number replace a risk assessment.
- The numbers that *do* carry legal weight in NZ are **notification and competency thresholds** (below),
  **not** a trigger for whether controls are required.

## Notifiable work (particular hazardous work)

Notify WorkSafe **at least 24 hours before** the work starts, on the *particular hazardous work*
notification form, where:

- **Construction work** is carried out with a **risk of a person falling 5 m or more** (with the usual
  defined exclusions — e.g. roof work over the edge, certain residential exceptions); **and**
- **Scaffolding** is erected or dismantled from which a person **could fall 5 m or more**.

Notification gives an inspector the chance to review the plan first. Confirm the current list and
exclusions via `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.

## NZ competency (who may do the work)

| Activity | NZ requirement |
|---|---|
| General work at height | Trained/competent for the task and equipment (harness, anchors, **rescue**) — no licence, but competence must be demonstrable |
| **Scaffolding where a person could fall ≥ 5 m** | Erected/altered/dismantled **by or under the direct supervision of a Certificated Scaffolder** — WorkSafe/SARNZ **Certificate of Competence (CoC)**, underpinned by the **NZ Certificate in Scaffolding (Level 3/4)** |
| Scaffolding where fall < 5 m | No CoC required, but a **competent person** to good practice (footings, bracing, edge protection, loading) |
| Boom-type / mobile EWP | Recognised **EWP operator certificate** + machine-type familiarisation (NZ has **no HRWL** — competency, not a licence) |
| Rope access | Certified technicians (industry scheme, e.g. IRATA/SPRAT) |

NZ has **no High Risk Work Licence** system — never transplant an AU HRWL class onto NZ work. The
licensing/competency split is in `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## Standards (verify-pointers — AS/NZS are copyright)

- **AS/NZS 1891** (fall-arrest systems; **1891.4** = selection/use/maintenance, annual recertification of
  anchors/equipment).
- **AS/NZS 1657** (fixed platforms, walkways, stairways, ladders).
- **AS/NZS 4576** (scaffolding guidelines) / **AS/NZS 1576** (scaffolding design).
- **AS 1418.10 / AS 2550.10** (MEWP design / safe use).

Standards are **copyright** — cite and apply, do not reproduce. Confirm the current edition each time.

## How NZ differs from AU (quick contrast)

- **NZ:** GPGs + HSWA SFAIRP duty; **competency-based** (CoC, certificates), **no HRWL**; manage falls
  at **all heights**; 5 m = notification & scaffolder-CoC threshold only.
- **AU:** model **Code of Practice — *Managing the risk of falls at workplaces*** (Victoria runs its own
  compliance code) + **HRWL** for scaffolding (SB/SI/SA) and boom EWP ≥ 11 m (WP). See `au` detail in
  `good-practice.md` and the licence system in `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.
