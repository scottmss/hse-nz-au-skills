# Working at height — good practice, standards & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, governing guidance, qualifications and critical questions for working
at height. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from NZ WorkSafe working-at-height guidance and the
AU model Code *Managing the risk of falls at workplaces*. **Not legal advice**; standard editions and
guidance change — treat references as verify-pointers and confirm the current version.

## Governing guidance & standards

- **NZ:** the WorkSafe **Best Practice Guidelines for Working at Height in New Zealand**, **Working on
  roofs GPG**, and **Scaffolding in New Zealand** (SARNZ). NZ manages falls at **all heights** (no
  minimum — the "three-metre rule" is a myth); 5 m is only the **notifiable-work and Certificated-
  Scaffolder threshold** (≥ 24 h notice to WorkSafe for construction fall risk ≥ 5 m and for erecting/
  dismantling scaffolding with a ≥ 5 m fall). Full NZ regulatory anchor, thresholds and competency →
  `nz-gpg.md`.
- **AU:** model **Code of Practice — *Managing the risk of falls at workplaces*** (adopted per
  state/territory; Victoria runs its own compliance code). The WHS Regs require managing the risk of
  falls, with specific duties where a fall could cause injury.
- **Standards (AS/NZS):**
  - **AS/NZS 1891** (series) — industrial fall-arrest systems and devices; **1891.4** = selection, use
    and maintenance (harnesses, lanyards, anchors, lifelines).
  - **AS/NZS 1657** — fixed platforms, walkways, stairways and ladders.
  - **AS/NZS 4576** — guidelines for scaffolding; **AS/NZS 1576** — scaffolding (design).
  - **AS 1418.10 / AS 2550.10** — mobile elevating work platforms (design / safe use).

## Critical controls (hierarchy order)

### Eliminate (highest priority)
- **Do the work at ground level** wherever reasonably practicable — lower the task, use extendable
  tools or remote methods (e.g. inspection cameras/drones), prefabricate at ground level and lift into
  place. The best fall control is **not being at height**.

### Substitute / isolate — passive protection
- **A safe working platform** — scaffold, EWP, or a properly designed work platform with full edge
  protection.
- **Edge protection / guardrails** — top-rail (≈900–1100 mm), mid-rail (≈450 mm) and toe-board, to
  load-rated requirements; barriers around voids and openings.
- **Covers** over penetrations, voids and **fragile surfaces** (e.g. fragile roofing) — secured,
  load-rated, and clearly marked. Passive controls work without the worker having to do anything.

### Active engineering — restraint before arrest
- **Fall restraint** (a system that physically prevents the worker reaching a position from which they
  could fall) is **preferred** over fall arrest.
- **Fall arrest** (stops a worker before they hit the ground/structure after a fall) only where
  restraint and passive controls are not reasonably practicable. Requires adequate clearance below,
  rated anchors, and shock-absorbing connection.
- **Anchors and equipment** must be **fit for purpose**, withstand the applied forces, be installed by
  a competent person, inspected before each use, and **recertified annually** per AS/NZS 1891.4.

### Administrative
- Work at height **briefed at the pre-start**, with everyone aware of the work and the **drop/exclusion
  zones**. A **drop zone** (access forbidden, barriered and signed) under any work where objects could
  fall; an **exclusion zone** beyond it for high-risk areas.
- Safe-work procedures, permits where used, and weather/wind stop-work thresholds.

### PPE / individual
- Harness + lanyard for restraint/arrest, correctly fitted and attached to a suitable anchor; helmet.
- **Tool tethering / containment** to eliminate dropped tools and equipment during transit and use.

## Dropped objects
Eliminate the drop first (tether tools, contain/secure materials, tool trays kept inside platforms),
then protect people below (exclusion zone, overhead protection / catch platforms or nets). Mobile
phones and small tools are a common, overlooked drop hazard.

## Suspension trauma & rescue
Wherever **fall arrest** is used, an **emergency rescue plan is mandatory** — a person hanging in a
harness can suffer suspension trauma quickly. The plan must be **prepared and discussed with all
participants before work**, the rescue equipment available, the rescuer **trained and competent**, and
the plan **rehearsed**. Never rely on emergency-services response alone.

## Mobile elevating work platforms (MEWP/EWP)
- Operated only by **trained, competent** people for the **machine type**, per the manufacturer's
  instructions.
- Used within **platform weight/load limits**, on suitable ground, for the designed purpose.
- **Pre-start inspection** each shift (tyres/condition, fluid levels, brakes, hydraulics, controls,
  emergency lowering, communications); faults rectified before use; current **log book** maintained.
- Occupants wear **fall-protection** appropriate to the EWP type (boom-type: harness with restraint
  lanyard to the basket anchor); tools secured against dropping.
- **No climbing out of an elevated platform** except under a specific, risk-assessed, supervisor-
  approved procedure. (AU: boom-type EWP with a **boom length ≥ 11 m** needs HRWL class **WP**.)

## Qualifications & competency

| Activity | NZ | AU |
|---|---|---|
| General work at height | Recognised working-at-height training (harness, anchors, rescue) | Training to the *Managing the risk of falls* Code |
| Scaffolding (fall ≥ 5 m) | **Certificated scaffolder** (WorkSafe Certificate of Competence) | HRWL scaffolding class **SB/SI/SA** |
| Boom-type EWP (≥ 11 m boom) | Recognised EWP operator certificate | HRWL class **WP** |
| Rope access | Certified technicians (e.g. IRATA/SPRAT) | Certified technicians |

## Critical questions to consider

- What other means of access would **eliminate** the need to work at height (ground-level work,
  scaffold, EWP)?
- How has **competence** been demonstrated for the people and the equipment?
- Is **fall restraint** possible, so arrest isn't needed? If arrest is used, is there enough clearance?
- How were the **drop and exclusion zones** determined, and are all access points controlled?
- What stops **tools or materials** from falling (tethers, boards, covers, trays)?
- Are anchors **rated, installed by a competent person, and in-date**?
- Is there a **rescue plan**, is the equipment available, is the rescuer competent, and has it been
  **rehearsed**?
- Is this **notifiable work** (NZ: fall risk ≥ 5 m)? → `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.
