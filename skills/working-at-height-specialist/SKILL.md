# Working at Height Specialist (falls SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for working at height and preventing falls — of people
*and* objects. Provides the good-practice **critical controls** (hierarchy-ordered), the
**qualifications and competency** required, and the governing NZ/AU guidance and standards. Falls are
a perennial fatal risk; this SME helps eliminate the need to work at height where possible and, where
not, get the controls and competency right.

## When to use

- Planning or reviewing any **work at height** (roof, edge, scaffold, EWP, ladder, tower, void).
- Choosing **fall protection** (the right control up the hierarchy; restraint vs arrest).
- Confirming **who is competent/qualified** to do the work or operate the equipment.
- A **dropped-object** risk from height.

## When NOT to use

- The **crane lift / suspended load** itself → `../critical-risk-manager/` + `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.
- A **confined space** entered from height → also `../confined-space-specialist/`.
- The **legal duty / notifiable-work** detail → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** for the fall risk → `../critical-risk-manager/` (this SME supplies the
  controls and questions that go into it).

## The risk

Impact from a **fall from one level to another**, a **dropped object** striking someone below, or
**suspension trauma** after an arrested fall. Present wherever someone works above the ground/floor or
near an edge, void or fragile surface — and wherever tools/materials are used or stored at height.

## Critical controls — apply the hierarchy

Work top-down; **eliminate before you protect** (see `references/good-practice.md` for detail):

1. **Eliminate** — do the task at ground level; lower the structure; use extendable tools, drones, or
   prefabrication so no one goes up.
2. **Substitute / Isolate (passive engineering)** — a safer place to work: scaffolds, EWPs, edge
   protection (guardrails: top-rail, mid-rail, toe-board), covers over voids/fragile surfaces. Passive
   protection (it works without the worker acting) beats active.
3. **Active engineering** — **fall restraint** (stops you reaching the edge — preferred) before
   **fall arrest** (stops you hitting the ground after a fall). Rated, inspected, in-date anchors.
4. **Administrative** — safe work procedures, exclusion/**drop zones**, pre-start briefing, permits.
5. **PPE** — harness/lanyard for restraint/arrest; helmet; **tool tethering** to stop drops.

**Dropped objects:** eliminate the drop (tether tools, contain materials), then protect below
(exclusion zone, catch platforms/nets, overhead protection).
**Suspension trauma:** an **emergency rescue plan** is mandatory wherever arrest is used — a suspended
person must be recovered fast; the plan must be rehearsed and the rescuer competent.

## Qualifications & competency

Competency is part of the control set — confirm it before work starts:

- **Working-at-height training** appropriate to the task and equipment (harness use, anchor selection,
  rescue).
- **EWP/MEWP operators** trained and competent for the machine type (NZ: recognised EWP operator
  certificate; AU: **boom-type EWP ≥ 11 m boom = HRWL class WP** → `../high-risk-work-specialist/`).
- **Scaffolding:** NZ requires a **certificated scaffolder** where a person could fall **5 m or more**
  (WorkSafe Certificate of Competence); AU uses HRWL scaffolding classes (SB/SI/SA).
- **Rope access** by certified technicians (industry schemes, e.g. IRATA/SPRAT).
- Anchors and fall-arrest equipment: set up by a **competent person**, inspected before use, and
  recertified per AS/NZS 1891.4.

Detail in `references/good-practice.md`; the licensing system itself is in `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## Jurisdiction note

NZ: WorkSafe **working-at-height good practice** guidance; **construction work with a risk of falling
5 m or more is notifiable work** (24 h notice to WorkSafe), as is scaffolding ≥ 5 m. AU: the model
**Code of Practice "Managing the risk of falls at workplaces"** (adopted per jurisdiction; Victoria
differs). Standards: **AS/NZS 1891** (fall-arrest), **AS/NZS 1657** (platforms/walkways/ladders),
**AS/NZS 4576** (scaffolding), **AS 1418.10 / AS 2550.10** (MEWP). Treat all as verify-pointers and
confirm the current edition/jurisdiction via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

Either a **height-work control plan** (task → fall/drop risks → controls in hierarchy order →
qualifications required → rescue plan → critical questions), or the **height-specific controls,
competency and questions** to feed into a bow tie (`../critical-risk-manager/`) or task analysis
(`../task-analysis-author/`). A draft for a competent person to validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice guidance and structure — **not legal advice and not a substitute for
a competent person or the current standards/guidance**, which change. Validate every control,
qualification and rescue arrangement against the actual site, structure, equipment and people before
work proceeds.
