# Drilling — good practice, drill-floor killers & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, the drilling risk profile and competency for the rotary-drilling rig and
operation, across oil & gas, geothermal, water-bore and mineral-exploration drilling. Generic and
company-agnostic, distilled from NZ WorkSafe/MinEx and Australian drilling good practice. **Not legal
advice**; guidance and the sector regime change — treat references as verify-pointers and confirm against
the current rules and a competent person.

## The drill-floor killers (and good-practice control)

### Entanglement in the rotating drill string — the signature killer
- A worker caught by the **rotating string / rotary table / kelly** and **crushed against the mast or a
  structure** — fatalities and amputations. Highest when people **work close** to the rotation —
  adding/removing string by hand, taking core/samples, clearing spoil/cuttings, or passing by.
- Controls (top-down):
  - **Mechanise the handling** so people don't reach into rotation — **top drive** (no kelly/rotary
    table), **iron roughneck**, pipe spinners, automated pipe racking.
  - **Guard** the rotary table/turntable and rotating parts; **presence-sensing / e-stops** in reach.
  - A strict **exclusion** around rotating parts; **stop rotation** before anyone approaches; permit only.
  - **No loose clothing, gloves, jewellery or long hair** near rotation; trained, supervised crews.

### Making / breaking connections
- **Tongs, iron roughneck, slips, elevators, spinning chains/catheads** — struck-by, caught-between,
  fingers, and **line-of-fire**.
- Controls: controlled tong/iron-roughneck operation; **stand clear of the line of fire**; agreed
  communication and roles; retire worn lines, chains and slips; keep hands out of pinch points.

### Hoisting & dropped objects (and the derrick)
- Draw works, travelling block, crown, wire rope and elevators; **objects falling from the derrick**;
  **derrick/monkeyboard work at height**.
- Controls: **inspected** wire rope and hoisting gear; **dropped-object** prevention (tethering, secured
  tools, **exclusion below**, no-go zones); fall protection and rescue for **derrick work** →
  `../../working-at-height-specialist/`.

### Rig-up / rig-down — raising and lowering the mast
- A classic fatal event — **mast collapse**, **overhead-line contact**, crush and pinch points.
- Controls: a **planned mast raising/lowering procedure**, **exclusion zone**, **overhead-line
  clearance/isolation**, competent supervision, firm/level ground, and the right sequence/pins.

### High-pressure circulating (mud) system
- Mud pumps, **standpipe and hoses** — **hose whip**, **high-pressure fluid injection** (a small
  pin-hole jet can inject through skin), and mud in the eyes.
- Controls: pressure-rated, inspected hoses **restrained** (whip checks/safety pins); **pressure-test
  exclusion**; high-pressure-injection awareness and first aid; eye protection.

### Well control / kick / blowout (generic awareness)
- Generic: **kick detection**, a **tested BOP** where fitted, and barrier discipline. The **sector-
  specific** well-control regime is owned elsewhere — petroleum → `../../oil-gas-specialist/`,
  geothermal (steam/two-phase, H2S) → `../../geothermal-specialist/`.

## Plus

- **Stuck pipe / fishing** (stored energy when freeing); **pipe & casing handling** (manual handling,
  struck-by, fingers — mechanise where possible); **drilling fluids & cuttings** (caustic/barite
  chemicals, **H2S in returns**, **silica** dust) → `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`; **mud tanks
  / pits as confined spaces** → `../../confined-space-specialist/`; **noise and vibration**; and
  **fatigue** on long tower/shift rosters in remote camps.

## Competency

- A **competent rig crew** for the roles (driller, derrickhand, floorhand/roughneck, offsider) and the
  rig type; **supervision** of new crew on the drill floor. **Well-control certification** (e.g. IWCF /
  IADC WellSharp) where the sector requires it — route petroleum to `../../oil-gas-specialist/`.
  **Mineral-exploration / water-bore** drilling competency per the NZ extractives / MinEx framework (and
  state requirements in AU). Inductions cover the **rotating-string** rule and the line of fire.

## Critical questions to consider

- Which **sector** is this (oil & gas / geothermal / water-bore / mineral exploration) — and is the
  **regime** routed to its SME?
- **Rotating string:** is handling **mechanised**, the rotary table **guarded**, an **exclusion** enforced,
  rotation **stopped** before anyone approaches, and loose clothing/gloves banned?
- **Connections:** are crews clear of the **line of fire**, with controlled tongs/iron roughneck?
- **Hoisting/derrick:** inspected gear, **dropped-object** control, and fall protection/rescue for derrick
  work?
- **Rig-up/down:** a planned mast procedure with **exclusion** and **overhead-line** clearance?
- **Circulating system:** hoses restrained, pressure-test exclusion, injection awareness?
- **Generic well control** in place, with the sector-specific regime owned by oil-gas/geothermal/mining?
- Are **drilling-fluid chemicals/H2S/silica**, **mud-tank confined spaces** and **fatigue** managed?
