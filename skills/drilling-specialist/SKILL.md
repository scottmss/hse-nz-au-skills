# Drilling Specialist (drill rig & rotary-drilling operation SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for the **drilling rig and the rotary-drilling operation
itself** — the rig and mast, the **drill floor**, hoisting, the **rotating drill string**, making/breaking
connections, pipe/casing handling and the circulating (mud) system. It is **cross-sector**: the same rig
hazards run across **oil & gas, geothermal, water-bore and mineral-exploration** drilling. The defining
killer is **entanglement in the rotating drill string**. Owns the **rig and the operation**; routes the
sector **regime** to its SME — petroleum well-control/safety-case to `../oil-gas-specialist/`, the
geothermal field/H2S/steam to `../geothermal-specialist/`, the mining/exploration regime to
`../mining-quarrying-specialist/`.

## When to use

- The **drilling rig and operation**, in any sector — rig-up/rig-down, **derrick/mast**, drill floor,
  hoisting, **rotating string**, connections, pipe/casing handling, the **mud/circulating** system.
- The drill-floor killers — **rotating-string entanglement**, **making/breaking connections**, hoisting
  and dropped objects, and **mast raising/lowering** (flagship — `references/good-practice.md`).
- Generic **well-control awareness** (kick detection, BOP) — routing the sector-specific regime onward.

## When NOT to use

- The **petroleum well-control regime / safety case / blowout (MAE) / offshore** → `../oil-gas-specialist/`.
- The **geothermal field** — H2S, high-temp/pressure steam & fluid, well discharge, hot ground →
  `../geothermal-specialist/`; the **mining/quarrying** regime (exploration as a sector) →
  `../mining-quarrying-specialist/`.
- **Directional drilling / boring near buried services and cable strike**, and shallow ground disturbance
  → `../excavation-specialist/`.
- The **deeper hazard method** belongs to its SME:
  - **Machine guarding & isolation of rotating plant** → `../machinery-safety-specialist/`; **derrick /
    monkeyboard / working at height** → `../working-at-height-specialist/`.
  - **Drilling fluids/chemicals, H2S in returns, silica in cuttings** → `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`;
    **mud tanks/pits as confined spaces** → `../confined-space-specialist/`.
  - **Pipe-handling cranes / rig moves** → `../crane-specialist/` + `../lifting-rigging-specialist/` +
    `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## The drilling risk picture

A drill floor concentrates **rotating power**, **hoisted loads**, **high-pressure fluid** and **heavy
tubulars** in a tight, busy, often remote and fatigued workplace:

1. **Entanglement in the rotating drill string (the signature killer)** — a worker caught by the rotating
   string/rotary table/kelly and **crushed against the mast or a structure** (amputations, fatalities).
   The risk is highest when people **work close by** — adding/removing string by hand, taking samples,
   clearing spoil/cuttings, or just walking past.
2. **Making/breaking connections** — **tongs, iron roughneck, slips, elevators, spinning chains/catheads**
   — struck-by, caught-between and **line-of-fire** on the drill floor.
3. **Hoisting & dropped objects** — draw works, travelling block, crown, wire rope, elevators; objects
   from the **derrick**; **derrick/monkeyboard work at height**.
4. **Rig-up / rig-down — raising and lowering the mast** — a classic fatal event (mast collapse, overhead
   lines, crush, pinch points).
5. **High-pressure circulating (mud) system** — mud pumps, standpipe, hoses — **hose whip**, **high-
   pressure fluid injection**, and mud in the eyes; pressure-testing.
6. **Plus** **well control / kick / blowout** (generic awareness; sector regime onward), **stuck
   pipe/fishing** stored energy, **pipe & casing handling** (manual handling, struck-by, fingers),
   **drilling fluids & cuttings** (chemicals, **H2S in returns**, silica), **mud-tank confined spaces**,
   **noise/vibration**, and **fatigue** on long tower/shift rosters in remote camps.

## Core controls (owned here)

Summary; detail in `references/good-practice.md`:

- **Rotating string** — **guard** the rotary table/turntable; a strict **exclusion** around rotating
  parts; **mechanise** handling (top drive, **iron roughneck**, pipe spinners, automated racking) so
  people don't reach into the rotation; **no loose clothing/gloves/jewellery**; emergency stops in reach.
- **Connections** — controlled tong/iron-roughneck use, **stand clear of the line of fire**, communication
  and competent crews; retire worn lines/chains.
- **Hoisting & derrick** — inspected wire rope and hoisting gear, **dropped-object** prevention (tethering,
  exclusion below), and fall protection for **derrick/monkeyboard** work → `../working-at-height-specialist/`.
- **Rig-up/rig-down** — a planned **mast raising/lowering** procedure, exclusion zone, **overhead-line**
  clearance, competent supervision, and firm ground.
- **Circulating system** — pressure-rated hoses **restrained**, pressure-test **exclusion**, and
  high-pressure-injection awareness.
- **Well control (generic)** — kick detection, a tested **BOP** where fitted, and barrier discipline —
  **routing the sector-specific regime** to oil-gas / geothermal / mining.

## Method

1. **Identify the sector** (oil & gas / geothermal / water-bore / mineral exploration) — and **route the
   regime** to its SME while this SME owns the rig/operation.
2. **Control the drill-floor killers** — rotating-string entanglement, connections, hoisting/dropped
   objects, **rig-up/rig-down**, and the high-pressure circulating system.
3. **Cover well-control awareness** (BOP/kick) and route the sector-specific well-control/MAE onward.
4. **Route the generic hazards** — machine guarding, height (derrick), drilling-fluid chemicals/H2S/
   silica, confined spaces (mud tanks), pipe-handling cranes/rig moves.
5. **State residual obligations** — competent rig crew, exclusion from rotating/hoisting hazards, and
   sector-regime compliance are mandatory. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

Drilling is an **operation that sits within whichever sector regime applies** — so the *regime* comes
from the sector SME, while the **rig and operation** are owned here. **NZ:** **HSWA 2015** general duties;
WorkSafe **extractives** and **MinEx** (the extractives health & safety council) drilling good practice;
**petroleum** drilling under the **HSW (Petroleum Exploration and Extraction) Regs 2016** (→
`../oil-gas-specialist/`); **mineral-exploration** drilling under the **mining/quarrying** regime (→
`../mining-quarrying-specialist/`); **water-bore** under general duties. **AU:** the **model WHS** primary
duty, with **state petroleum/mining** regimes and (offshore) **NOPSEMA** via `../oil-gas-specialist/`.
Jurisdiction variations → `../safework-au-specialist/`. Confirm current editions via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

Either a **drilling-operation safety plan or risk screen** (sector & regime routed → drill-floor killers
(rotating string, connections, hoisting, rig-up/down, circulating) → well-control awareness → routed
generic hazards → critical questions), or the **rig-specific controls and questions** to feed into a bow
tie (`../critical-risk-manager/`), task analysis (`../task-analysis-author/`) or SOP (`../sop-author/`).
A draft for a competent person to validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice drilling-rig guidance — **not legal advice and not a substitute for a
competent person, the rig manufacturer's instructions, or the current sector regime**. **Rotating-string
entanglement**, dropped objects, mast raising and high-pressure fluid kill quickly; validate the guarding,
exclusion zones, hoisting, rig-up/down and circulating-system controls against the actual rig, operation
and crew before relying on them.
