# Construction Specialist (construction-project & activities SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **how a construction project is run safely** and for
the **construction-specific activities** that no hazard SME owns — the **principal-contractor / SWMS /
high-risk-construction-work** regime, site coordination and induction, **temporary works**,
**demolition**, and **precast/tilt-up concrete erection**. Owns that regime and those activities, and
routes the physical hazards (falls, excavation, cranes, plant, dust) to the relevant SME.

## When to use

- The **construction safety-management regime** — **principal contractor** duties, **WHS management
  plan**, **SWMS for high-risk construction work (HRCW)**, **site induction / white card**, multi-
  contractor coordination.
- **Temporary works** — formwork, **falsework**, propping, shoring, bracing (the structure-while-being-
  built).
- **Precast / tilt-up concrete erection** — panels, lifting inserts, concrete strength at lift,
  temporary bracing, erection sequence (flagship — `references/precast-and-tiltup.md`).
- **Demolition** — sequence, structural collapse, hazardous materials.
- Pulling a construction site's risk picture together and **routing each hazard** to the right SME.

## When NOT to use

- The **physical hazard detail** belongs to its SME (this skill routes to them):
  - **Falls / scaffolding / edge protection / MEWPs** → `../working-at-height-specialist/`.
  - **Excavation / trenching / underground services** → `../excavation-specialist/`.
  - **The crane lift** → `../crane-specialist/`; **slinging/rigging the load or panel** →
    `../lifting-rigging-specialist/`.
  - **Mobile plant & site traffic** → `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`; **machinery/guarding** →
    `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
  - **Silica/concrete dust, solvents, asbestos** → `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`; **services /
    live electrical** → `../electrical-energy-specialist/`; **confined spaces** → `../confined-space-specialist/`.
- **Who holds the duty / notifiable events / upstream duties** → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` /
  `../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## The construction risk picture

Construction is high-hazard and **multi-PCBU** — many contractors on one site, overlapping duties, a
fast-changing workplace. Two things define the safety approach:

1. **A coordinated management regime** — someone (the principal contractor / main PCBU) plans and
   coordinates the site; high-risk work is controlled by **SWMS**; everyone is **inducted**; duties
   between contractors are coordinated (→ `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` overlapping duties).
2. **The physical fatal risks** — **falls** (the biggest construction killer), **being struck by**
   plant/loads/collapsing structure, **buried** (excavation/trench), **electrocution** (services), and
   **dust/health** (silica) — each routed to its SME below.

For the **critical-control verification** lens — identifying the few controls that must be present and
effective for each fatal risk, and how to verify them in the field — use `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## Segment shifts the picture — residential vs commercial vs civil

The same fatal risks are present across construction, but their **weighting, the management regime, and
who carries the duty** shift by segment. Set the segment early and tune the advice:

- **Residential** (housing, light timber-frame, fit-out) — **falls dominate even more** (roof edges,
  ladders, incomplete scaffold); sites are often **below the AU principal-contractor / WHS-management-
  plan threshold** (A$250k), with many **small PCBUs, sole traders and owner-builders** and lighter,
  dispersed supervision; plant is **ladders/MEWPs/nail guns/saws** rather than cranes; the signature
  health risk is **respirable crystalline silica, especially engineered-stone benchtops and fibre-
  cement/concrete cutting** (→ `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`). Emphasise fall prevention,
  competent scaffold/edge protection, on-tool dust control, and the fact that **every small PCBU still
  holds the full duty** even with no principal contractor.
- **Commercial** (multi-storey, fit-out at scale) — the **full regime** (principal contractor + WHS
  management plan, heavy multi-PCBU coordination), plus **cranes, precast/tilt-up, structural steel,
  façade/MEWP, lift shafts and voids, hot works and plant-room confined spaces**.
- **Civil / infrastructure** — **deep excavation and trenching, services, mobile plant and live
  traffic, working over water, temporary works** at scale; principal-contractor regime throughout.

Commercial and civil match the classic "construction project" model; **residential is the segment most
often under-served by it** — don't assume a principal contractor, a white card, or a site office exists.

## The construction safety-management regime

- **Principal contractor / main contractor** coordinates the site, the interfaces between trades, and
  the shared facilities; **AU:** a principal contractor is required for a **construction project ≥
  A$250,000** and must prepare a **written WHS management plan** before work starts.
- **High Risk Construction Work (HRCW) → SWMS.** **AU:** the WHS Regulations list **18 HRCW activities**
  (e.g. risk of a fall > 2 m, work near services, demolition, trenches, tilt-up/precast, mobile plant,
  confined spaces) — each requires a **Safe Work Method Statement** prepared **before** work starts,
  followed, and reviewed. **NZ:** no identical statutory SWMS list, but a **task analysis / SWMS / JSA**
  is expected good practice for the same high-risk activities (→ `../task-analysis-author/`).
- **Site induction & competency** — **AU:** general **construction induction ("white card",
  CPCWHS1001)** for everyone before they start; **NZ:** site-specific induction and recognised
  competency (industry prequalification e.g. SiteWise). Confirm via `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.
- **Consultation, cooperation & coordination** across all PCBUs on site — the legal heart of multi-
  contractor safety (→ `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`).

## Construction-specific activities (owned here)

- **Temporary works** — formwork, **falsework**, propping and shoring must be **engineer-designed,
  loaded only as designed, and not struck/removed until the permanent structure can carry the load**.
- **Precast / tilt-up concrete erection** — a high-consequence activity with its own standard (AS 3850):
  engineered panels and **temporary bracing**, **lifting inserts**, **concrete strength at lift**, the
  **signed brace plan**, erection sequence and exclusion zones. Full detail in
  `references/precast-and-tiltup.md`.
- **Demolition** — engineered sequence, structural-collapse control, services isolated, hazardous
  materials (asbestos) managed first; **AU** demolition is HRCW/often licensed.

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction** (NZ/AU) and the project — **segment (residential / commercial / civil)**,
   size, principal contractor, trades, phase. The segment re-weights the regime and which hazards lead.
2. **Stand up the regime** — principal contractor + WHS management plan (AU ≥ $250k), induction,
   SWMS/TA for the high-risk activities, consultation/coordination across PCBUs.
3. **Screen the activities** — identify HRCW and construction-specific work (temporary works, precast,
   demolition) and apply their controls (precast → flagship reference).
4. **Route each physical hazard** to its SME (falls, excavation, crane, lifting, plant, dust, services,
   confined space).
5. **Coordinate** — interfaces between trades, the changing site, services, exclusion zones.
6. **State residual obligations** — every PCBU keeps its own duty; competency, supervision and a
   site/risk plan are required. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**NZ:** **HSWA 2015** general duties with **WorkSafe construction** guidance and the sector body
**CHASNZ**; **notifiable/particular hazardous work** (e.g. scaffolding where a person could fall ≥ 5 m,
certain excavations) → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/`; SWMS/task analysis used as good practice; precast
via WorkSafe's **"Safe work with precast concrete"** GPG. **AU:** the **WHS Regulations Chapter 6
(Construction work)** and the model **Code of Practice _Construction Work_** — **HRCW + SWMS (Reg 291,
18 activities)**, **principal contractor + WHS management plan (projects ≥ A$250k)**, **construction
induction (white card)**; precast via **AS 3850 / AS 3600** and the **National Code of Practice for
Precast, Tilt-up and Concrete Elements**. Jurisdiction variations (Victoria OHS Act, WA) →
`../safework-au-specialist/`. Confirm current editions via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice construction-safety guidance — **not legal advice, not engineering
design, and not a substitute for a competent person or the current Codes/standards and project
documentation**, which change. Temporary works and precast bracing/lifting must be **engineer-designed
and certified**; validate every control against the actual project, design, plant and people before
relying on it.
