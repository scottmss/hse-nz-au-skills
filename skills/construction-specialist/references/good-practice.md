# Construction safety — good practice (NZ/AU)

Methodology reference for `construction-specialist`. Grounded in **NZ HSWA 2015 / WorkSafe construction
guidance / CHASNZ** and the **AU WHS Regulations Chapter 6 (Construction work) + model Code of Practice
_Construction Work_**. Government/regulator guidance is summarised with attribution; AS/NZS standards
are **copyright — verify-pointers only**. Confirm current editions.

> **Not legal advice; not engineering design.** A competent person validates the regime and controls;
> temporary works and precast bracing/lifting must be engineer-designed and certified.

---

## 1. What makes construction different

Construction is **high-hazard, multi-PCBU and fast-changing** — many contractors share one site, the
workplace changes daily, and the fatal risks (falls, struck-by, buried, electrocution, dust) are all
present at once. Safety therefore rests on **two pillars**:

1. **A coordinated management regime** — someone plans and coordinates the whole site, high-risk work is
   controlled by method statements, everyone is inducted, and the duties between contractors are
   actively coordinated.
2. **Control of the physical fatal risks** — each routed to its hazard SME.

This skill owns pillar 1 and the **construction-specific activities** (temporary works, precast,
demolition); it routes pillar 2.

## 2. The construction safety-management regime

### Principal contractor / main PCBU
- Coordinates the site, the **interfaces between trades**, shared facilities, access/egress, and the
  overall risk picture.
- **AU:** a **principal contractor** is required for a **construction project valued ≥ A$250,000** and
  must prepare a **written WHS management plan** before work starts (roles/responsibilities, consultation
  and coordination arrangements, incident arrangements, and how SWMS are collected/assessed/monitored).
- **NZ:** HSWA places the duty on each **PCBU** with the main/principal contractor typically coordinating;
  a site safety plan is expected good practice.

### High Risk Construction Work (HRCW) → SWMS
- **AU:** the **WHS Regulations (Reg 291)** list **18 HRCW activities** — including a risk of a **fall
  > 2 m**, work **on or near energised services**, **demolition**, **trenches/excavations**, **tilt-up
  and precast concrete**, work near **mobile plant**, **confined spaces**, and others. Each requires a
  **Safe Work Method Statement (SWMS)** that is **prepared before work starts, complied with, reviewed**,
  and given to the principal contractor.
- **NZ:** no identical statutory SWMS list, but a **task analysis / SWMS / JSA** is expected good practice
  for the same activities → `../../task-analysis-author/`.

### Induction & competency
- **AU:** general **construction induction ("white card", CPCWHS1001)** for **everyone before they start
  on site** (valid nationally), plus site-specific induction.
- **NZ:** **site-specific induction** and recognised competency; industry **prequalification** (e.g.
  SiteWise) is common. Competency-system framing → `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

### Consultation, cooperation & coordination
- The legal heart of multi-contractor sites: PCBUs who share a workplace must **consult, cooperate and
  coordinate** so far as is reasonably practicable. Overlapping-duties detail →
  `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` (NZ) / `../../safework-au-specialist/` (AU).

## 3. Construction-specific activities (owned here)

- **Temporary works** — **formwork, falsework, propping, shoring, back-propping**: the part-built
  structure carrying construction loads. Must be **engineer-designed**, installed by competent people,
  **loaded only as designed**, inspected, and **not struck or removed until the permanent structure can
  carry the load**. Falsework/formwork collapse is a recognised killer.
- **Precast / tilt-up concrete erection** — see `precast-and-tiltup.md` (the flagship): AS 3850 design,
  lifting inserts, concrete strength at lift, temporary bracing, the signed brace plan, erection
  sequence and exclusion zones.
- **Demolition** — engineered demolition sequence, **structural-collapse** control, **services isolated**
  first, **hazardous materials (asbestos, lead, silica)** identified and managed before work →
  `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`. **AU** demolition is HRCW and often **licensed**.

## 5. Baseline controls (all construction work)

- **Plan and coordinate** — principal contractor/main PCBU, WHS management plan (AU ≥ $250k), program of
  high-risk activities and their SWMS/TA, interfaces between trades.
- **Induct everyone** (white card + site-specific) and confirm competency for the task.
- **SWMS/TA before high-risk work**, followed and reviewed; stop and re-plan when conditions change.
- **Engineer temporary works and precast bracing/lifting**, with certification on site.
- **Coordinate the changing site** — services located/isolated, exclusion zones, edge protection up
  before the edge exists, housekeeping, public protection (hoarding, overhead protection).
- **A live risk register / site plan**; report and learn from incidents and near-misses.

---

## 6. Critical questions to consider

- Is there a **principal contractor / coordinating PCBU**, and (AU ≥ $250k) a **written WHS management
  plan** in place before work starts?
- Is every **HRCW activity covered by a current SWMS/TA**, followed and reviewed?
- Is **everyone inducted** (white card + site-specific) and competent for their task?
- Are **temporary works and precast bracing/lifting engineer-designed and certified**, and not removed
  early?
- Are the **physical hazards routed and controlled** — falls, excavation, crane/lifting, plant, dust,
  services, confined space (links above)?
- Are **overlapping duties between contractors** actively coordinated? → `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/`
  / `../../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Could an event be **notifiable**? (Same regulators.)
