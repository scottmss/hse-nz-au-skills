# Geothermal — good practice, H2S & steam/fluid hazards

Detailed good-practice controls, the geothermal risk profile and competency for the geothermal energy
sector — exploration, well drilling, the steamfield, generation and direct use. Generic and
company-agnostic, distilled from WorkSafe NZ general duties + geothermal/H2S guidance (a world-leading NZ
sector). **Not legal advice**; guidance changes — treat references as verify-pointers and confirm against
the current rules and a competent person.

> No bespoke geothermal regime — the **general HSWA/WHS primary duty** applies. The value here is the
> distinctive geothermal hazards. Routing: **generation/network** → `../../electricity-supply-specialist/`;
> the **drilling rig/operation** → `../../drilling-specialist/`; the **H2S exposure method** →
> `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`; **confined spaces** → `../../confined-space-specialist/`.

## Hydrogen sulfide (H2S) — the signature killer

- **Toxic and heavier than air** — it **collects in low and confined areas** (cellars, sumps, pits,
  drains, trenches, vessels). At high concentration it causes **"knockdown" — unconsciousness in 1–2
  breaths** — and it **paralyses the sense of smell**, so **odour is not a warning**. A would-be rescuer
  who enters unprotected becomes the next casualty.
- Controls:
  - **Detection** — **fixed** monitors with alarms across the field/plant, and **personal H2S monitors**
    on workers; calibrated and bump-tested.
  - **A site H2S management plan** — exposure limits, alarm levels, muster, exclusion zones, and roles.
  - **Ventilate / exclude** low and confined areas; **never enter** an accumulation area without
    monitoring and protection.
  - **Escape sets / SCBA** available and worn where required; **wind-direction** awareness and refuges;
    a **rescue plan** that does not create a second casualty.
  - The exposure-control method, SCBA and monitoring detail → `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.

## High-temperature / high-pressure steam & geothermal fluid

- **Burns and scalds** from steam, **hot brine** and **two-phase** fluid; **failures** of wellheads,
  valves, pipelines, separators and silencers; **blowdown/discharge** energy.
- Controls: **integrity** of pressure systems (design, inspection, corrosion/scaling management);
  **isolation, depressurise and cool** before work, with positive isolation and **blowdown** to a safe
  point; **exclusion** from discharge paths, silencer and blow-down areas; **burn protection** and first
  aid for thermal injury. High-pressure release / stored energy method → `../../electrical-energy-specialist/`.

## Geothermal well drilling, testing & discharge

The **generic drill rig and operation** (derrick, mast rig-up/down, rotating string, hoisting,
connections, mud system) is `../../drilling-specialist/`. The **geothermal-specific** drilling, well
control and discharge hazards are owned here — and conventional **oil-and-gas methods do not transfer
unmodified**:

- **Geothermal well control is different.** Kicks are **high-temperature brine or steam** and can
  **escalate rapidly (steam kicks)**. The geothermal response is generally to **keep the well full of
  cold water and cool/quench it**, using a **diverter / rotating BOP** — *not* simply weighting up mud as
  in petroleum. **Relying on conventional oil-and-gas well control unmodified can be dangerous.** Use
  competent geothermal well-control procedures, equipment and trained crews.
- **Total lost circulation.** Geothermal reservoirs are fractured and permeable, so drilling fluid is
  often **lost to the formation and does not return to surface** ("drilling blind"), with **crossflow**
  between fracture zones — cuttings aren't returned and MWD/pulse telemetry can be unfeasible. **Aerated-
  water / underbalanced / managed-pressure drilling** is used to lift cuttings, which introduces
  **compressed air, a blooey/discharge line and hot returns** at surface.
- **High temperature.** Limits **drill bits, casing material, drilling mud and cement** formulations and
  causes **wellbore instability/collapse** in hot fractured rock; the well is usually **cooled/quenched**
  before logging, running casing and cementing; manage thermal cycling and casing integrity.
- **H2S and hot returns at surface.** Monitor **H2S** at the rig, shakers and mud pits; guard against
  **burns/scalds** from hot two-phase returns at the shakers, blooey line and discharge.
- **Well testing / discharge / work-over.** Competent **bleed and quench**, wellhead and **master-valve**
  integrity, exclusion zones, silencer management, and **blowout** control. Uncontrolled discharge of
  steam/two-phase fluid is a major-energy event.

## Hydrothermal eruptions & hot ground

- **Sudden steam/hydrothermal eruptions**, **thin ground crust** over boiling pools and hot ground,
  scalding ground/water, **subsidence** and **induced seismicity**.
- Controls: **survey and barricade** hot ground and thin crust; **defined, tested walkways and access**;
  avoid eruption-prone features; monitoring; brief and supervise people new to the field.

## Silica scaling, non-condensable gases & confined spaces

- **Silica scaling** builds up in pipes/vessels/reinjection lines; **descaling and cleaning** often means
  **confined-space entry** with residual H2S and hot/chemical hazards. **Non-condensable gases** (mostly
  **CO2 + H2S**) collect in **condensers and gas-extraction** systems.
- Controls: treat scaling-clean and vessel entry as a **permit-controlled confined space** — **test for
  H2S, CO2 and oxygen**, ventilate, **standby** and **non-entry rescue** → `../../confined-space-specialist/`;
  manage descaling chemicals via `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.

## Plus

- **Noise** (vents, silencers, well discharge); **chemicals** (biocides, antiscalants, H2S abatement);
  **hot work**; **turbines/rotating plant** → `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`; **electrical /
  generation** → `../../electricity-supply-specialist/`; and **lone/remote** work. **NZ:** fields often
  sit on/near **Māori land/taonga** — engage **mana whenua** (a relationship/consent matter, separate
  from worker-safety controls).

## Competency

- **H2S-trained** workers (recognition, monitors, escape sets/SCBA, rescue); competent **well-test /
  discharge** and **steamfield** operators; confined-space and high-pressure-system competency. New
  workers **inducted to the field** (hot ground, H2S, steam) and supervised.

## Critical questions to consider

- Is **H2S** monitored (fixed + personal), with an **H2S plan**, low/confined-area control, **escape
  sets** and a **rescue plan** that won't create a second casualty — and does everyone know **smell is
  not a warning**?
- Are the **steam/fluid systems** isolated, depressurised and cooled before work, with exclusion from
  discharge/silencer areas and **burn** protection?
- Are **well testing/discharge** and **blowout** risks controlled (with the drilling SME during drilling)?
- Is **hot ground / thin crust** surveyed and barricaded, with defined walkways?
- Is **descaling / vessel entry** run as a **permit confined space** (test H2S/CO2/O2, ventilate,
  standby, rescue)?
- Are **generation/electrical** and **drilling** routed to their SMEs; is **mana whenua** engaged (NZ)?
