# Waste & recycling — good practice, killer risks & guidance

Detailed good-practice controls, the sector risk profile and named guidance for waste management and
recycling — collection, transfer stations, MRFs, landfills, composting and hazardous/clinical waste.
Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from WorkSafe NZ + WasteMINZ guidance and the Australian
state-WHS waste codes. **Not legal advice**; guidance changes — treat references as verify-pointers and
confirm against the current rules and a competent person.

> No bespoke waste regime — the **general HSWA/WHS primary duty** applies, managed **SFAIRP**. The value
> here is the **risk profile** of a very high-fatality sector and the controls that bite.

## Why waste & recycling is a high-fatality sector

Historically the sector runs at **several times the all-industry fatality rate**. The deaths cluster in
two places — **being struck by mobile plant/vehicles** and **being caught/crushed in machinery** — and a
third, growing catastrophe sits in the stream itself: **lithium-ion battery fires**. Layered on top is the
**uncertainty** of what the waste contains (chemicals, asbestos, cylinders, sharps, bioaerosols).

## The killer risks (and good-practice control)

### 1. Struck by mobile plant & vehicles (owned context; method → `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`)
- **Collection rounds** — the loader/runner on foot at the kerb and the **reversing/manoeuvring** truck;
  roadside traffic; lone work.
- **On site** — **front-end loaders, compactors, telehandlers, trucks** reversing in tip faces, MRF
  yards and transfer stations; the **public** mixing with heavy plant at transfer stations/tips.
- Controls: **separate people from plant** (one-way layouts, exclusion zones, pedestrian routes,
  barriers); **eliminate reversing** (drive-through, banksman only as a last resort); **proximity
  detection/cameras**; high-vis; **manage the public** at transfer stations (segregation, supervision,
  signage); **no one on foot in the operating zone**. Collection-round design and roadside controls via
  the traffic SME; the **heavy-vehicle / Chain-of-Responsibility** side via
  `../../transport-logistics-specialist/`.

### 2. Machinery entanglement & crush (method → `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`)
- **Static/mobile compactors, balers, shredders, granulators, conveyors and picking lines** — drawing-in,
  crushing and entanglement; the **highest-risk moments are clearing blockages and maintenance**.
- Controls: **guarding and presence-sensing**; **isolation/lockout-tagout (LOTO)** before any
  unblocking/maintenance — never reach into a running machine; emergency stops and pull-cords on picking
  lines; trained, authorised operators.

### 3. Fire & explosion — lithium-ion batteries the signature risk
- **Lithium-ion batteries** wrongly placed in kerbside recycling/rubbish are **damaged by the sorting
  process and ignite** — now a leading cause of **MRF and collection-truck fires** (thousands a year).
  Plus **aerosols and gas cylinders**, combustible **dust**, **self-heating** organics, and **landfill
  gas (methane)**.
- Controls: **keep batteries out of the stream** (public education, **separate battery/e-waste
  drop-off**, clear acceptance criteria, gate inspection); **early fire detection** (thermal imaging,
  smoke/heat) and **suppression**; safe **isolation/quarantine** of suspect items; housekeeping to limit
  fuel load; landfill-gas management; hot-work control.

### 4. Hazardous & biological exposure (method → `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`)
- **Unknown/mixed waste** — chemicals, **asbestos in waste**, **clinical waste and sharps/needlestick**,
  leachate, **dust (incl. respirable crystalline silica)**, and **bioaerosols** from composting/organics.
- Controls: **assume contamination** — design out hand contact, RPE/gloves, hygiene and washing,
  **immunisation** and an exposure-response (PEP) pathway for sharps; dust suppression and ventilation;
  procedures to identify and quarantine asbestos, cylinders and chemicals; safe clinical-waste handling.

### 5. Plus
- **Confined spaces** — silos, hoppers, pits, tanks and **landfill-gas** spaces → `../../confined-space-specialist/`.
- **Working at height** — on vehicles, balers, silos and picking platforms → `../../working-at-height-specialist/`.
- **Manual handling** (bins, sorting), **slips/trips**, **noise**, and **lone work** on collection rounds.

  practice and **battery/hazardous-waste collection** guidance. Heavy-vehicle collection also engages

  Practice** for the **Chain-of-Responsibility** side of collection.
- Standards and codes are **verify-pointers** — confirm the current instrument and jurisdiction.

## Critical questions to consider

- Which **operation** is it (collection, transfer station, MRF, landfill, composting, hazardous/clinical)?
- **Mobile plant:** are people separated from plant/vehicles, is **reversing eliminated**, and is the
  **public** controlled at transfer stations? → traffic SME / CoR via transport SME.
- **Machinery:** are compactors/balers/shredders **guarded and isolated (LOTO)**, especially for
  **unblocking and maintenance**? → machinery SME.
- **Fire/batteries:** are **lithium-ion batteries kept out of the stream**, with **fire detection and
  suppression** and quarantine of suspect items?
- **Unknown waste:** is hand contact designed out, contamination assumed (RPE, hygiene, immunisation,
  sharps PEP), and asbestos/cylinders/chemicals identified and quarantined?
- **Confined space / height / manual handling / lone work** — covered and routed?
