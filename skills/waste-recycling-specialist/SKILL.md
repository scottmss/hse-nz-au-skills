# Waste & Recycling Specialist (waste-sector SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **health & safety in waste management and recycling** —
**kerbside collection**, **transfer stations**, **materials recovery facilities (MRFs)**, **landfills**,
**composting/organics** and **hazardous/clinical waste**. It is **one of the highest fatality-rate
sectors** (several times the all-industry average), run under the **general HSWA/WHS duties** (no bespoke
regime). Two things define it: a deadly **mobile-plant + machinery** combination, and the **"you don't
know what's in it"** problem — mixed, unknown waste streams that hide fire, chemical and biological
hazards. Owns the waste-sector view and those distinctive risks; routes the hazard methods to the
relevant SME.

## When to use

- Any **waste or recycling** operation — **collection rounds**, transfer stations, **MRFs/sorting**,
  landfills, composting, scrap/e-waste, hazardous and **clinical/medical waste**.
- The sector's killer risks — **struck-by mobile plant/vehicles** (collection + on-site) and
  **machinery entanglement/crush** (compactors, balers, shredders) (flagship — `references/good-practice.md`).
- The **unknown/mixed-waste** problem — **fire/explosion** (esp. **lithium-ion batteries**), chemical,
  asbestos, sharps/clinical, dust and **bioaerosols**.
- Scoping a waste-facility or collection risk profile and engaging the right specialist SMEs together.

## The waste & recycling risk picture

Waste work combines **heavy mobile plant**, **powerful machinery**, a **public interface**, and an
**uncertain hazard stream** — which is why its fatality rate runs **several times the average**:

1. **Struck-by mobile plant & vehicles (the #1 killer)** — **refuse/collection trucks** and the
   worker-on-foot at the kerb; **front-end loaders, compactors, telehandlers** reversing and
   manoeuvring on site; the **public** at transfer stations/tips among heavy plant. (Recent AU
   fatalities: workers struck by a reversing loader and by a refuse compactor.)
2. **Machinery entanglement & crush** — **balers, static/mobile compactors, shredders, granulators,
   conveyors and picking lines**; the worst moments are **clearing blockages** and maintenance.
3. **Fire & explosion** — **lithium-ion batteries** wrongly placed in kerbside recycling and crushed by
   sorting gear are now the **signature catastrophic risk** (thousands of MRF/truck fires); plus
   aerosols, **gas cylinders**, combustible dust, self-heating organics, and **landfill gas (methane)**.
4. **Hazardous & biological exposure** — **unknown/mixed waste**, chemicals, **asbestos in waste**,
   **clinical waste & sharps/needlestick**, leachate, dusts (incl. RCS), and **bioaerosols** from
   composting/organics.
5. **Plus** confined spaces (silos/hoppers/pits/landfill gas), working at height, manual handling
   (bins/sorting), slips/trips, lone work on collection rounds, and noise.

## The defining challenge — you don't know what's in it

Unlike a controlled production line, a waste stream's contents are **uncertain and contaminated**. Build
the system around that:

- **Design out hand contact** — mechanise sorting; positive-sort/negative-sort design; keep people out of
  the line of fire of plant and machinery.
- **Keep dangerous items out of the stream at the source** — public education, separate collection and
  **drop-off for batteries, gas cylinders, chemicals, e-waste and clinical/sharps**; clear acceptance
  criteria and inspection at the gate.
- **Plan for the hazard you can't see** — fire detection/suppression and thermal imaging for **lithium-ion
  battery** ignition; procedures for finding asbestos, cylinders, chemicals and **sharps**; assume
  contamination and protect accordingly (RPE, gloves, immunisation, hygiene).

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction** (NZ/AU) and the **operation** — collection, transfer station, MRF, landfill,
   composting, hazardous/clinical — each has a different risk mix.
2. **Control the two killers first** — **mobile-plant/vehicle separation** (collection + on-site) and
   **machinery guarding/isolation** (compactors/balers/shredders) → route to those SMEs.
3. **Manage the unknown-waste stream** — battery/fire risk, hazardous and clinical waste, source
   separation and gate acceptance (owned here).
4. **Route the hazard methods** to their SME (traffic, machinery, substances/sharps, confined space,
   height, CoR/heavy vehicle).
5. **Cover** confined spaces, height, manual handling, lone work and the public interface.
6. **State residual obligations** — the PCBU owes the primary duty; competency, supervision, plant
   maintenance and a risk plan are required. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**NZ:** **HSWA 2015** general duties (no bespoke waste regime), regulated by **WorkSafe NZ** — general
risk-management plus topic guidance including **safe use of lithium-ion batteries**; industry good
practice via **WasteMINZ**; battery/hazardous-waste collection guidance. Heavy-vehicle collection also
engages **Land Transport** work-time (→ `../transport-logistics-specialist/`). **AU:** the **model WHS**
primary duty (Victoria OHS Act 2004; WA), with **state WHS guidance and codes** for the sector — e.g.
**SafeWork NSW _Collection and transport of waste_ Code of Practice** and waste-management/recycling
industry guidance, **WorkSafe Victoria** waste & recycling — and the **NHVR Waste & Recycling Industry
Code of Practice** for the **Chain-of-Responsibility** side of collection (→
`../transport-logistics-specialist/`). Jurisdiction variations → `../safework-au-specialist/`. Confirm
current editions via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice waste- and recycling-safety guidance — **not legal advice and not a
substitute for a competent person or the current HSWA/WHS requirements**, which change. Mobile-plant
strikes, machinery crush and **lithium-ion battery fires** can kill quickly and at scale; validate every
control — vehicle separation, machine isolation, fire detection/suppression and unknown-waste handling —
against the actual operation, plant and people before relying on it.
