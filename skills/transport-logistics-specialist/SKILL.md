# Transport & Logistics Specialist (road transport / heavy-vehicle SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **road transport, trucking and logistics safety** —
the **heavy-vehicle supply-chain regime**, **driver fatigue and work-time**, **loading/unloading**, and
**load restraint**. Transport harm reaches across the whole **supply chain** (not just the driver), and
the regulatory model reflects that — explicitly in Australia (Chain of Responsibility), and through
upstream duties in New Zealand. Owns the transport regime and activities; routes the physical hazards to
the relevant SME.

## When to use

- The **transport safety regime** — **Chain of Responsibility (CoR)** parties and duties (AU); the
  **HSWA upstream/PCBU duties + Land Transport work-time** model (NZ).
- **Driver fatigue & work-time** — work/rest hours, logbooks, fatigue-management accreditation.
- **Loading & unloading** — falls from vehicles, struck-by, load shift, the dock interface (flagship —
  `references/loading-and-load-restraint.md`).
- **Load restraint** — securing loads to the performance standard.
- **Vehicle roadworthiness, journey/route management, speed, and driver health/lone work.**

## The transport risk picture

Road transport is **high-energy, dispersed and time-pressured**, and the people who create the risk are
often **not the driver** — schedulers, loaders, consignors and receivers shape speed, fatigue and load
safety. So the safety model is a **supply-chain** one:

1. **A shared supply-chain duty** — everyone who influences the transport task owes a duty.
2. **The physical fatal risks** — **vehicle crashes** (speed, fatigue, distraction, roadworthiness),
   **loading/unloading** (falls, struck-by, load shift), and **load failure on the road**.

## The transport safety regime (Chain of Responsibility & equivalents)

- **AU — Chain of Responsibility (CoR) under the Heavy Vehicle National Law (HVNL):** parties **other
  than the driver** (consignor/consignee, packer, **loader/unloader**, operator/prime contractor,
  **scheduler**, loading manager…) each owe the **Primary Duty** — *eliminate or minimise the risks of
  your transport activities so far as is reasonably practicable*. It mirrors the WHS primary duty and
  carries heavy penalties. The **NHVR "Master Code"** (a registered industry Code of Practice) is the
  practical guide, organised around the HVNL risk areas: **speed, fatigue, mass/dimension/loading, and
  vehicle standards**.
- **NZ — no CoR law, but the same effect via HSWA:** a **PCBU** that consigns, loads, schedules or
  receives freight owes **upstream and overlapping duties**; **vehicles are a workplace**. Operating
  rules sit under **Land Transport** law (NZTA / Waka Kotahi) — operator licensing and the **work-time &
  logbook** rules. Use HSWA duties (→ `../worksafe-nz-specialist/`) to achieve the supply-chain outcome.

## Driver fatigue & work-time

- **AU:** **standard hours** under the HVNL, with **Basic Fatigue Management (BFM)** and **Advanced
  Fatigue Management (AFM)** accreditation modules (NHVAS) for more flexible, risk-managed hours; **work
  diaries** record work/rest.
- **NZ:** **Land Transport work-time rules** — a **break of at least 30 minutes after 5½ hours** of work
  time and a maximum **13-hour cumulative work day**, recorded in a **logbook**.
- Beyond the rules: **manage fatigue as a hazard** — realistic schedules (don't bake in speeding/fatigue),
  night-work and circadian effects, journey planning, and a culture where a fatigued driver can stop.

## Other core controls

- **Loading & unloading** — the most injury-dense activity; falls from trucks/trailers, being struck,
  and load shift → flagship `references/loading-and-load-restraint.md`.
- **Load restraint** — secure every load to the **performance standard** (Load Restraint Guide) → flagship.
- **Vehicle roadworthiness** — pre-trip/daily checks, maintenance, defect reporting; the right vehicle
  for the task.
- **Journey/route management** — distances, breaks, conditions, communications for remote/lone driving.
- **Speed & impairment** — speed management; alcohol/drugs/distraction.

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction** (NZ/AU) and the role (operator, consignor, loader, scheduler, receiver).
2. **Map the supply-chain duties** — CoR parties (AU) or the HSWA upstream/overlapping duties (NZ); who
   influences speed, fatigue and load safety.
3. **Manage fatigue & work-time** to the rules *and* as a hazard.
4. **Control loading/unloading and load restraint** (flagship).
5. **Cover the vehicle, journey, speed and driver-health** controls.
6. **Route the physical hazards** to their SME (falls, forklift, on-site traffic, HIAB lifting,
   dangerous goods).
7. **State residual obligations** — each party keeps its duty; competency, scheduling and a risk plan
   are required. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**AU:** the **Heavy Vehicle National Law (HVNL)** and **Chain of Responsibility**, regulated by the
**NHVR**, with the registered **Master Code** and the **NHVR Load Restraint Guide**; fatigue via
**BFM/AFM (NHVAS)**; **dangerous goods** via the **ADG Code**. WHS law applies **in parallel** (→
`../safework-au-specialist/`). **NZ:** **HSWA 2015** (vehicles as a workplace; upstream/overlapping
PCBU duties) plus **Land Transport** rules administered by **NZTA / Waka Kotahi** — operator licensing
and **work-time & logbooks (Land Transport Rule 2007)**; **dangerous goods** via the Land Transport DG
rule / HSNO. Jurisdiction variations (Victoria OHS Act, WA; WA is not in the HVNL) →
`../safework-au-specialist/`. Confirm current editions via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice transport-safety guidance — **not legal advice and not a substitute
for a competent person or the current HVNL/CoR, Land Transport and WHS/HSWA requirements**, which
change. The **HVNL/CoR regime is a separate body of law** from WHS — validate duties, work-time limits,
fatigue accreditation and load-restraint methods against the current rules and the actual operation
before relying on them.
