# Food, meat & dairy processing — good practice, distinctive risks & guidance

Detailed good-practice controls, the distinctive risk profile and named guidance for the **workforce** in
food, meat, dairy and seafood processing. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from WorkSafe NZ
food/meat guidance (including leptospirosis) and the Australian model WHS framework (including Q fever).
**Not legal advice and not food-safety advice**; guidance changes — treat references as verify-pointers
and confirm against the current rules and a competent person.

> **Lane check:** this is **worker** health & safety, not **food safety / product hygiene** (consumer
> protection under MPI/NZ Food Safety and FSANZ). Keep the two apart.
>
> **Boundary:** the general factory system, **process safety / MHF**, **combustible dust** (e.g.
> milk-powder dryers), noise and hazardous manual tasks are `../../manufacturing-specialist/`. This file
> owns the food/meat-processing specifics and routes the general methods there.

## The distinctive risk profile

Most food-processing harm is **high-frequency and chronic** (cuts, slips, strains, cold), with one
**catastrophic tail** — **ammonia refrigeration**. The mix below is what a generic factory lens misses.

### Knives & hand tools — the highest-frequency injury
- Lacerations and stab wounds in **boning and slaughter** — typically the non-knife hand and the thigh.
- Controls: **sharp, well-maintained knives** and correct **technique** (a blunt knife needs more force
  and slips); **cut-resistant mesh gloves and aprons**, guards/scabbards; **mechanical cutting** where
  practicable; trained, supervised, paced work; safe steeling and storage.

### Processing machinery — bandsaws and the rest
- **Bandsaws** are a signature **amputation** hazard; plus **mincers, mixers, bowl choppers, slicers,
  dehiders, augers and conveyors** — entanglement and drawing-in. The worst moments are **cleaning and
  unblocking**.
- Controls: **guarding** and **isolation/lockout-tagout (LOTO)** before cleaning/unblocking; trained,
  authorised operators; never reach into a running machine. Method → `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`.

### Ammonia refrigeration — the catastrophic tail
- **Anhydrous ammonia** is the dominant industrial refrigerant in meat/dairy/cold storage — **toxic,
  corrosive and flammable**. A leak is a **major-accident toxic-release** event; large inventories meet
  **Major Hazard Facility** thresholds.
- Controls: **competent refrigeration engineering** and maintenance; **leak/gas detection and alarms**;
  isolation and ventilation; **emergency response, escape and evacuation**; machinery-room design; and
  the **MHF safety case** where the threshold is met. Chemical method →
  `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`; MHF/process-safety method → `../../manufacturing-specialist/`.

### Slips, trips & falls
- **Wet, greasy floors** are a leading injury cause.
- Controls: floor and **drainage** design, anti-slip surfaces, **anti-slip footwear**, spill and cleaning
  regimes, and good lighting.

### Cold environments
- Chillers, freezers and boning rooms — **cold stress** and reduced dexterity (which worsens knife risk).
- Controls: cold-environment **PPE**, **rotation** and warm-up breaks, and warm welfare facilities.

### Repetitive manual handling / occupational overuse
- Boning, cutting and packing drive **MSK / occupational overuse syndrome (OOS)**; plus heavy carcass
  handling.
- Controls: **task and workstation design**, **job rotation**, mechanical aids and hoists, line speed and
  pacing, and early reporting/management of discomfort.

### Zoonoses — leptospirosis (NZ) & Q fever (AU)
- **Leptospirosis** is **NZ's most common work-related infectious disease** — abattoir/meat workers and
  meat inspectors are high-risk; spread from animal urine through cuts or mucous membranes; severe cases
  cause **kidney/liver damage**. **Q fever** is the AU abattoir analogue and is **vaccine-preventable**.
- Controls: **cover cuts**, hygiene and washing, **PPE** (gloves, eye/face protection), technique that
  **reduces aerosols** (careful handling of bladder/gut/udder), **Q fever vaccination (AU)**, vermin and
  effluent control, and **health monitoring** with early medical advice for a flu-like illness (and tell
  the doctor about the work). Method → `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.

### Plus
- **Hot processes & burns** (rendering, cooking, steam, hot water, **CIP caustic**); **chemicals** (CIP
  acids/caustics, chlorine, CO2) → `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`; **noise**; **confined
  spaces** (silos, tanks, rendering vessels, CA coolstores, effluent) → `../../confined-space-specialist/`;
  **live-animal handling** at the lairage/yards (crush/kick) → `../../agriculture-specialist/`; and
  **forklifts/internal traffic** → `../../forklift-specialist/` + `../../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.

## Critical questions to consider

- Which **operation** (abattoir/boning, rendering, dairy, seafood, bakery)? Is this clearly **worker**
  safety, not **food** safety?
- **Knives:** sharp knives, correct technique, **cut-resistant mesh** PPE, supervision and pacing?
- **Machinery:** are **bandsaws and other machines guarded and isolated (LOTO)** for cleaning/unblocking?
- **Ammonia:** competent refrigeration, **leak detection/alarms**, isolation, emergency response — and an
  **MHF safety case** if above threshold?
- **Slips/cold:** drainage and anti-slip controls; cold PPE and rotation?
- **Manual handling/OOS:** task design, rotation, mechanical aids, early reporting?
- **Zoonoses:** cuts covered, hygiene, PPE, aerosol-reducing technique, **Q fever vaccination (AU)**, and
  **health monitoring** with work-aware medical advice?
