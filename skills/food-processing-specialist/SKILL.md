# Food Processing Specialist (food, meat & dairy processing SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **worker health & safety in food, meat, dairy and
seafood processing** — abattoirs/meatworks, boning rooms, rendering, dairy factories, seafood plants and
general food manufacturing. It is a **manufacturing sub-sector**, but with a **distinctive harm profile**
that a general factory lens misses: **knives and processing machinery** (lacerations and amputations),
**ammonia refrigeration** (a major-accident toxic-release risk), **wet and cold environments**
(slips/cold), **repetitive cutting** (occupational overuse), and **zoonoses** (leptospirosis, Q fever).
Owns that food/meat-processing view; routes the general factory and hazard methods to the relevant SME.

## When to use

- Any **food/meat/dairy/seafood processing** workforce — **abattoirs/meatworks**, boning/slaughter,
  rendering, smallgoods, **poultry**, **dairy factories** (milk powder, cheese), seafood, bakery and
  beverage.
- The sector's distinctive risks — **knives**, **processing machinery** (bandsaws, mincers, slicers),
  **ammonia refrigeration**, **wet-floor slips**, **cold**, **repetitive manual handling**, and
  **zoonoses** (flagship — `references/good-practice.md`).
- Scoping a processing-plant risk profile and engaging the right specialist SMEs together.

## When NOT to use

- **Food safety / product hygiene** is a **different domain** — that protects the *consumer* (MPI / NZ
  Food Safety, FSANZ, food control plans). **This skill is the WORKERS' health & safety.** Say so.
- The **general factory system / process-safety / Major Hazard Facility / combustible-dust / noise /
  hazardous-manual-task method** → `../manufacturing-specialist/` (e.g. **milk-powder dryer dust
  explosion** is an MHF/dust matter; this skill owns the food/meat context and routes the method there).
- The **physical/specific hazard method** belongs to its SME (this skill routes to them):
  - **Guarding & isolation/LOTO of bandsaws, mincers, mixers, conveyors (and unblocking)** →
    `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
  - **The ammonia / CIP / sanitiser chemical method, SDS, and the zoonoses/biological-exposure method** →
    `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.
  - **Silos, tanks, rendering vessels, CA coolstores, effluent (confined spaces)** →
    `../confined-space-specialist/`; **live-animal handling at the lairage/yards** (crush/kick) →
    `../agriculture-specialist/`; **forklifts & internal traffic** → `../forklift-specialist/` +
    `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`.
- **Who holds the duty / notifiable events** → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## The food & meat-processing risk picture

A processing plant concentrates **sharp tools, powerful machinery, a wet and cold environment, animal
material, and repetitive work** — producing a high, mostly **chronic and high-frequency** injury load
with one **catastrophic** tail (ammonia):

1. **Knives & hand tools** — the **highest-frequency** injury in meat processing: lacerations and stab
   wounds in boning/slaughter; non-dominant hand and thigh.
2. **Processing machinery** — **bandsaws** (a signature amputation hazard), mincers, mixers, bowl
   choppers, slicers, dehiders, augers and conveyors — entanglement and amputation, worst at **cleaning
   and unblocking**.
3. **Ammonia refrigeration (the catastrophic tail)** — **anhydrous ammonia** is the dominant industrial
   refrigerant in meat/dairy/cold storage; **toxic, corrosive and flammable** — a leak is a
   **major-accident toxic-release** event; large inventories meet **MHF** thresholds.
4. **Slips, trips & falls** — **wet, greasy floors** are a leading injury cause.
5. **Cold** — chillers, freezers and boning rooms; cold stress and reduced dexterity.
6. **Repetitive manual handling / occupational overuse** — boning, cutting, packing; MSK/OOS, and heavy
   carcass handling.
7. **Zoonoses** — **leptospirosis** (NZ's most common work-related infectious disease; abattoir/meat
   workers) and **Q fever** (AU abattoirs — vaccine-preventable); plus hot processes/burns (rendering,
   cooking, steam, CIP caustic), chemicals, noise, and confined spaces.

## Core controls (owned here)

Summary; detail in `references/good-practice.md`:

- **Knives** — sharp, maintained knives and correct technique; **cut-resistant mesh gloves/aprons** and
  guards; mechanical cutting where practicable; supervision and training; safe knife storage/steeling.
- **Machinery** — guarding and **isolation/LOTO** for cleaning and unblocking (bandsaws especially) →
  `../machinery-safety-specialist/`.
- **Ammonia refrigeration** — competent refrigeration engineering, **leak detection and alarms**,
  isolation, ventilation, **emergency response and evacuation**, and MHF safety-case where threshold met
  → `../hazardous-substances-specialist/` + `../manufacturing-specialist/`.
- **Wet floors & cold** — drainage and floor design, anti-slip surfaces and footwear, cleaning regimes;
  cold-environment PPE and rotation.
- **Zoonoses** — **cover cuts**, hygiene and washing, PPE, technique to reduce aerosols, **Q fever
  vaccination (AU)**, and **health monitoring / early medical advice** for lepto-like illness.
- **Manual handling/OOS** — task and workstation design, job rotation, mechanical aids, pacing.

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction** (NZ/AU) and the **operation** (abattoir/boning, rendering, dairy, seafood,
   bakery) — operation drives the risk mix.
2. **Confirm the lane** — this is **worker** safety, not **food** safety.
3. **Work the distinctive risks** — knives, machinery, **ammonia**, wet/cold, zoonoses, repetitive
   handling (owned here).
4. **Route the methods** — machine guarding/LOTO, the chemical/ammonia/zoonoses method, confined space,
   livestock handling, traffic — and the general factory/MHF/dust/noise method → manufacturing.
5. **State residual obligations** — the PCBU owes the primary duty; competency, supervision, plant
   maintenance, **vaccination/health monitoring** and a risk plan are required. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**NZ:** **HSWA 2015** general duties (food *worker* safety has no separate regime — **food safety** under
MPI/NZ Food Safety is a different, consumer-facing law), regulated by **WorkSafe NZ**, with guidance for
manufacturing/meat and a dedicated **Leptospirosis: information for meat processing workers** resource;
**ammonia** refrigeration above threshold engages the **HSW (Major Hazard Facilities) Regulations 2016**
(→ `../manufacturing-specialist/`). **AU:** the **model WHS** primary duty (Victoria OHS Act 2004; WA),
with **state WHS** guidance for the meat/food industry and **Q fever** prevention (and **vaccination**)
resources; ammonia above threshold engages the **WHS MHF (Schedule 15)** regime. Jurisdiction variations
→ `../safework-au-specialist/`. Confirm current editions via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice guidance for the **food/meat/dairy/seafood-processing workforce's**
health & safety — **not legal advice, not food-safety/product-hygiene advice, and not a substitute for a
competent person or the current HSWA/WHS (and MHF) requirements**, which change. Knives, machinery,
**ammonia** and zoonoses can cause serious or fatal harm; validate every control against the actual
plant, processes, animals and people before relying on it.
