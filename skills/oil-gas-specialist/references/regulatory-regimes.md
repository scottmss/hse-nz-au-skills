# Oil & gas / petroleum — the regulatory regimes (NZ + AU)

Petroleum safety runs on a **safety case** — the operator must demonstrate that **Major Accident Events
(MAEs)** are identified and their **barriers** are adequate and maintained. But **who regulates that
safety case differs sharply**: NZ uses **one national regulator** (onshore and offshore); Australia
**splits by location**, and its **offshore** regime sits under a **Commonwealth regulator entirely
outside the model WHS**.

## New Zealand — one national regime (WorkSafe High Hazards Unit)

- **Law:** **HSWA 2015** + the **Health and Safety at Work (Petroleum Exploration and Extraction)
  Regulations 2016** (in force 4 April 2016 — part of the post-Pike-River high-hazard reform package).
- **Regulator:** **WorkSafe NZ — the High Hazards Unit** (also regulates **well design and integrity**),
  for **both onshore and offshore** petroleum.
- **Safety case:** the operator submits a **safety case** (a living document built on the installation's
  SMS, reflecting its risk profile) — generally **at least ~90 days before operating an installation** —
  plus a **Major Accident Prevention Policy (MAPP)**.
- **Offshore installations:** must hold a **certificate of fitness**; offshore production and
  non-production installations are covered.

## Australia — split by location (offshore vs onshore)

| Location | Regime & regulator | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Offshore** (Commonwealth waters, generally **beyond 3 nautical miles**; plus coastal waters where a state/NT has **conferred** powers) | **NOPSEMA** under the **Offshore Petroleum and Greenhouse Gas Storage Act 2006 (OPGGS Act)** + the **Offshore Petroleum and GGS (Safety) Regulations** | A **safety-case / MAE** regime that is **OUTSIDE the model WHS** — NOPSEMA regulates OHS, well integrity and environment. Operator must have an **accepted safety case** for the facility. |
| **Onshore** petroleum (wells, fields, processing) | **State/territory petroleum & resources regulators** + the **state/territory WHS regulator**, and the **Major Hazard Facility** regime where thresholds are met | Not NOPSEMA. The WHS Act/MHF and the state petroleum legislation apply; **Victoria** uses the OHS Act 2004. |

**The key trap:** an **offshore** worker (NOPSEMA/OPGGS) and an **onshore** worker (state + WHS/MHF) on
the same company's assets are under **different regulators and different law**. Always establish
**onshore vs offshore**, and for offshore in coastal waters, whether powers have been **conferred** to
NOPSEMA.

## NZ vs AU at a glance

| | New Zealand | Australia |
|---|---|---|
| **Offshore** | **WorkSafe NZ (High Hazards Unit)** — HSWA + Petroleum E&E Regs 2016; certificate of fitness | **NOPSEMA** — OPGGS Act 2006 + Safety Regs (**outside model WHS**) |
| **Onshore** | **Same** WorkSafe regime | **State** petroleum/resources + **state WHS** + MHF |
| **Safety instrument** | Safety case + **MAPP** | Safety case (offshore, NOPSEMA-accepted) / MHF safety case (onshore) |
| **Regulators** | **One** (WorkSafe) onshore & offshore | **Several** (NOPSEMA offshore; state petroleum + WHS onshore) |
