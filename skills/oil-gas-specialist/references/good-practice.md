# Oil & gas / petroleum — good practice, major-accident risks & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, catastrophic risks and the safety-case discipline for the oil & gas /
petroleum sector — upstream exploration, drilling, production, offshore installations and gas processing.
Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from the NZ HSW (Petroleum Exploration and Extraction)
Regulations 2016 + WorkSafe High Hazards Unit guidance and the Australian NOPSEMA (offshore) + state
(onshore) regimes. **Not legal advice**; the regime and rules differ by jurisdiction and change — treat
references as verify-pointers and confirm against the current law and a competent person.

> The regulatory regime, the NZ/AU split and the offshore/onshore divide live in `regulatory-regimes.md`.
> This file is the **risk method**: the major-accident risks and how good operations control them.

## How petroleum safety is managed — the safety case

Petroleum is a **major-accident-hazard** sector: the events that matter are **low-frequency but
catastrophic** (Piper Alpha, Deepwater Horizon). The whole model is built on a **safety case** — the
operator demonstrates that:

1. the **Major Accident Events (MAEs)** for the facility/activity are **identified**;
2. each has defined **critical controls/barriers** (prevention *and* mitigation) with **performance
   standards**; and
3. those barriers are **assured** — tested, inspected and maintained so they will work on demand.

Think in **barriers**: a major accident happens only when multiple barriers fail together, so the job is
to keep every barrier healthy (a bow tie sits naturally here → `../../critical-risk-manager/`).

## The catastrophic risks (and good-practice control)

### Loss of containment → fire & explosion
- Release of **hydrocarbons under pressure** → jet fire, flash fire, **vapour cloud explosion**, BLEVE —
  the defining MAE.
- Controls: **integrity of pressure systems** (design, inspection, corrosion management); **leak/gas
  detection**; **hazardous-area classification** and ignition control; **hot-work permits**; **emergency
  shutdown (ESD)** and **blowdown**; fire & gas systems and passive/active fire protection. Ignition and
  hazardous-area electrical → `../../electrical-energy-specialist/`.

### Well control / blowout
- Uncontrolled flow from a well (Deepwater Horizon).
- Controls: competent crews and **well-control certification**; **kick detection** and monitoring; a
  **well-integrity / two-barrier policy**; a **tested blowout preventer (BOP)**; managed pressure;
  cementing and barrier verification.

### H2S (hydrogen sulfide / sour gas)
- **Toxic and rapidly fatal** at low concentrations; heavier than air.
- Controls: **detection and alarms**, **personal monitors**, **escape sets (EEBA)**, wind-direction
  awareness, exclusion and rescue planning, and training. Exposure method →
  `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.

### Offshore — escape, evacuation, helicopters & SIMOPS
- You can't simply walk away from an offshore installation.
- Controls: **temporary refuge**, muster and **POB** control, **lifeboats/TEMPSC** and life-saving
  appliances, **helicopter safety** (a leading offshore fatality source — HUET, flight following),
  managed **simultaneous operations (SIMOPS)**, and **dropped-object** prevention. Marine/vessel ops →
  `../../maritime-ports-specialist/`.

### Plus
- **Confined spaces** (tanks, vessels, cellars) → `../../confined-space-specialist/`; **working at
  height** on the derrick/structure → `../../working-at-height-specialist/`; **rotating drilling
  equipment** (drawworks, iron roughneck, tongs) → `../../machinery-safety-specialist/`; **noise**,
  **NORM**, **fatigue** on offshore rosters, and **lone/remote** work.

## Competency

- **Well-control certification** (e.g. IWCF/IADC) for drilling crews; competent **process-safety** and
  operations personnel; **H2S** and **offshore survival (HUET/BOSIET)** training for offshore.
- The operator runs a **safety management system** under the safety case; contractors brought into it.
- Confirm the competency/high-risk-work side via `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## Critical questions to consider

- Which **regime** applies — NZ (WorkSafe High Hazards Unit), or AU **offshore (NOPSEMA/OPGGS)** vs
  **onshore (state + WHS/MHF)**? Upstream/offshore or processing?
- Is there a **current safety case**, with the **MAEs identified**, **critical barriers** defined to
  **performance standards**, and **barrier assurance** (testing/maintenance) demonstrated?
- **Containment/fire:** pressure-system integrity, gas detection, ignition/hazardous-area control, ESD
  and blowdown, fire & gas protection?
- **Well control:** competent crews, kick detection, a two-barrier policy and a **tested BOP**?
- **H2S:** detection, alarms, escape sets and rescue?
- **Offshore:** temporary refuge, evacuation (lifeboats), **helicopter** safety, SIMOPS and dropped
  objects covered?
- Are the **generic hazards routed** (substances, confined space, height, electrical, machinery, marine)?
