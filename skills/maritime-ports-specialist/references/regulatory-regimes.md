# Maritime & ports — the regulatory regimes (NZ + AU)

Maritime and port work is **co-regulated**: **maritime law** governs the *vessel and harbour
navigation*, and **work-health-and-safety law** governs the *people*. The regulator map is where **NZ
and Australia diverge** — NZ has consolidated around **Maritime NZ** (now including the major ports on
land); Australia is a **layered, multi-regulator** picture (AMSA, Seacare, and the state WHS regulators).

## New Zealand — Maritime NZ at the centre

- **Vessels** — Maritime NZ administers the **Maritime Transport Act 1994** and the Maritime Rules.
  Domestic commercial vessels operate under the **Maritime Operator Safety System (MOSS)** (with an
  approved **MTOP**) or a **Specified Limits Permit**. Maritime NZ is also the **designated HSWA 2015
  regulator for work on board ships**.
- **Ports — a 2024 change.** **From 1 July 2024, Maritime NZ is the H&S regulator at New Zealand's 13
  major ports — both on land and on ships** (the port area inside the security boundary and its
  associated buildings/installations). It was prompted by two port-worker deaths (Lyttelton and Auckland,
  2022). **WorkSafe retains** inland ports, **major hazard facilities**, HSWA authorisations/exemptions,
  and the Electricity/Gas Acts.
- **Harbour navigational safety** — the **NZ Port & Harbour Marine Safety Code** is the national
  best-practice framework for managing marine activities in ports and harbours, used by port operators
  and **regional-council harbourmasters** (appointed under the MTA).
- **Crew competency** — **SeaCert** certificates appropriate to the vessel and its limits.

## Australia — a layered, multi-regulator picture

Establish **which layer** applies — they overlap:

| Layer | Who/what it covers | Regulator / instrument |
|---|---|---|
| **Domestic Commercial Vessels (DCV)** | Most domestic ships (ferries, tugs, workboats, tourism) — the **vessel** | **AMSA** under the **Marine Safety (DCV) National Law Act 2012** (survey, Certificate of Operation, SMS) |
| **People on a DCV (WHS)** | The crew/workers on the vessel-as-workplace | **State/territory WHS regulator** (AMSA holds **MOUs** with them) |
| **Prescribed seafarers** | Defined seafarers on **prescribed (interstate/international-trading) vessels** | **Seacare scheme** — **Occupational Health and Safety (Maritime Industry) Act 1993** + Seafarers Act 1992; **AMSA = OHS inspectorate**; Comcare supports the **Seacare Authority** |
| **Ports / wharves (landside)** | The port as a land workplace | **State/territory WHS regulator** |
| **Pilotage / harbour** | Navigational/port-marine safety | State **port authorities** / port legislation |
| **International ships** | SOLAS-class vessels | **SOLAS / ISM Code / STCW**; **AMSA** port-state control |

**The trap:** complying with one layer does **not** mean you comply with the others — e.g. AMSA survey ≠
WHS compliance; a DCV crew sits under state WHS while a prescribed seafarer sits under Seacare.

## NZ vs AU at a glance

| | New Zealand | Australia |
|---|---|---|
| **Vessel safety** | Maritime NZ — MTA 1994 + **MOSS** | **AMSA** — Marine Safety (DCV) National Law 2012 (+ SOLAS/ISM for international) |
| **People (work H&S) at sea** | **Maritime NZ** (designated HSWA agency) | **State WHS** (DCV) or **Seacare / OHS(MI) Act 1993** (prescribed seafarers) |
| **Ports (landside)** | **Maritime NZ** at the 13 major ports (from 1 Jul 2024); WorkSafe for inland ports/MHF | **State/territory WHS regulator** |
| **Harbour navigation** | **NZ Port & Harbour Marine Safety Code** + council harbourmasters | State port authorities |
| **Regulators involved** | Largely **one** (Maritime NZ) | **Several** (AMSA, Seacare, state WHS, port authorities) |
