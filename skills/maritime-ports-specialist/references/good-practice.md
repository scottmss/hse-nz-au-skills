# Maritime & ports — good practice, fatal risks & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, fatal risks, competency and critical questions for non-fishing
commercial vessel operations and port/harbour marine operations. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled
from Maritime NZ (ships + the 13 major ports) + the NZ Port & Harbour Marine Safety Code, and the
Australian AMSA / Seacare / state-WHS regimes. **Not legal advice**; the co-regulated regime and rules
differ by jurisdiction and change — treat references as verify-pointers and confirm against the current
law and a competent person.

## The fatal risks (and good-practice control)

### Mooring & towlines — snap-back
- A **parted mooring or tow line** releases enormous stored energy and **snaps back** along unpredictable
  paths — a classic multiple-fatality event. Heaving lines, winches and bollards add struck-by and
  drawing-in risk.
- Controls: **identify the snap-back zones** and keep people out of them; retire lines **on condition**
  (no fixed "snap-back arrestor" myth — manage the whole arrangement); controlled, communicated winch
  operation; minimise the number of people on deck; brief every mooring/unmooring; maintain fairleads and
  surfaces.

### Enclosed spaces on ships
- **Cargo holds, tanks, ballast and void spaces** become **oxygen-deficient or toxic** (cargo reactions,
  inerting, rust, fumigation). Entrants collapse — and **untrained rescuers die** going in after them.
- Controls: treat as a **permit-controlled confined space** — isolate, **test the atmosphere**,
  ventilate, continuous monitoring, **standby person**, and a **non-entry rescue** plan; never enter to
  rescue without breathing protection. Method → `../../confined-space-specialist/`.

### Falls into water & the ship-shore interface
- The **quay edge**, **gangways/brows** and **pilot ladders** are where people go into the water — man
  overboard, drowning, or a fall onto the wharf.
- Controls: rig a **safe gangway/brow** (handrails, safety net, secured, correct angle, lit); **safe
  pilot-ladder** rigging and a tended transfer; **PFDs at the water's edge**; man-overboard alarm and a
  rehearsed **recovery**; edge protection and housekeeping at the quay. Immersion/rescue method →
  `../../water-safety-specialist/`.

### Pilotage & towage
- **Pilot transfer** by ladder (boarding/landing a moving ship) and **tug girting/girding** (a tug
  tripped and capsized by its own towline) are the signature pilotage/towage killers.
- Controls: competent pilots and crews; **safe transfer arrangements** to the standard; tug **girting
  awareness**, **quick-release** towing gear, and crew competency; **fatigue management** for harbour
  crews working irregular hours.

### Bunkering & transfer
- Fuel/cargo transfer — **spill, fire, explosion**.
- Controls: agreed **procedure and checklist**, secure connections, **containment/SOPEP**, no ignition
  sources, communication, and an **emergency shutdown**. Chemical/fire method →
  `../../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.

## Competency & the vessel safety system

- The vessel runs under an **approved safety system** — NZ **MOSS (MTOP)** / Specified Limits Permit; AU
  an AMSA **Certificate of Operation/Survey + SMS**; regulated international ships under the **ISM Code**.
- **Crew** hold the right certificates — NZ **SeaCert**; AU AMSA **certificates of competency** / **STCW**
  — and are inducted and drilled. Pilots/harbour crews competent and authorised. See
  `regulatory-regimes.md` and `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## Critical questions to consider

- Which **regime** applies — **NZ** (Maritime NZ for ships *and* the 13 major ports) or **AU** (AMSA +
  state WHS for DCVs; **Seacare** for prescribed seafarers; state landside)? Is this seafaring or
  port-marine — and is it really cargo-handling (→ stevedoring) or fishing (→ fishing)?
- Does the vessel run under an **approved safety system**, and is the **crew certified, inducted and
  drilled**?
- For **mooring/towage**: are **snap-back zones** identified and cleared, lines retired on condition, and
  people minimised on deck?
- For **enclosed spaces**: permit, **atmosphere test**, ventilation, standby and **non-entry rescue** —
  every time?
- Is the **ship-shore interface** safe (gangway/brow, pilot ladder, PFDs, MOB recovery)?
- For **pilotage/towage**: safe transfer, **girting** controls, quick-release, fatigue managed?
- Is **bunkering** done to a checklist with containment and emergency shutdown?
