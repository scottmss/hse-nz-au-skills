# Maritime & Ports Specialist (vessel operations & port/harbour SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for **health & safety in maritime vessel operations and
port/harbour operations** — the **non-fishing** commercial fleet (cargo ships, ferries, passenger
vessels, tugs, workboats, charter/tourism) and the **marine side of ports** (mooring, pilotage, towage,
bunkering, the ship-shore interface, harbour navigational safety). Like fishing, it sits in a
**co-regulated** maritime + work-health-and-safety regime — and **who regulates differs between NZ and
Australia**. Owns the seafaring and port-marine regime and hazards; routes the cargo-handling operation,
fishing, and generic hazards to the relevant SME.

## When to use

- **Commercial vessel operations** (not fishing) — cargo, ferries, passenger, tugs, workboats,
  tourism/charter; the ship as a workplace and the crew.
- **Port & harbour marine operations** — **mooring and line handling**, **pilotage and pilot transfer**,
  **towage**, **bunkering**, the **ship-shore interface** (gangways, access), and harbour navigational
  safety.
- The **co-regulated regime** for ships and ports, and **crewing competency** (flagship —
  `references/regulatory-regimes.md`).
- The marine fatal risks — mooring snap-back, enclosed spaces on ships, falls into water at the
  interface, pilot transfer (flagship — `references/good-practice.md`).

## When NOT to use

- **The cargo-handling operation itself** (stevedoring — straddle carriers, reach stackers, quay cranes,
  lashing, holds as a cargo workplace) → `../stevedoring-specialist/`.
- **Commercial fishing vessels and aquaculture/marine farms** → `../fishing-aquaculture-specialist/`.

  - **Drowning/immersion, lifejackets, in-water rescue, diving** → `../water-safety-specialist/`.

  - **Bunkering fuel / cargo chemicals / fire** → `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`; **landside port
    vehicle-pedestrian traffic** → `../mobile-plant-traffic-specialist/`; **ship's cranes/lifting** →
    `../crane-specialist/` + `../lifting-rigging-specialist/`.
  - **Fatigue/watchkeeping/isolation (work-design)** → `../psychosocial-risk-specialist/`; **falls on
    vessels/structures** → `../working-at-height-specialist/`.
- **Who holds the duty / notifiable events** → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`
  — but note the **maritime regulator** (Maritime NZ / AMSA / Seacare) is central (see below).
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/`.

## The maritime & ports risk picture

Ships and ports combine a **moving workplace over water**, **heavy gear and lines under huge tension**,
a **ship-shore interface** where people and vehicles meet the water's edge, and **enclosed spaces** that
kill silently:

1. **Mooring & line handling** — the **snap-back** of a parted mooring or tow line is a classic
   multiple-fatality event; winches, heaving lines, bollards.
2. **Enclosed spaces on ships** — cargo holds, tanks, ballast and void spaces go **oxygen-deficient or
   toxic**; entry (and would-be rescuers) die — a leading shipboard killer.
3. **Falls into water & the ship-shore interface** — gangways, brows, **pilot ladders**, the quay edge —
   man overboard, drowning, falls onto the wharf.
4. **Pilotage & towage** — **pilot transfer** by ladder; tug **girting/capsize** and towline failure.
5. **Bunkering & transfer** — fuel/cargo **spill, fire, explosion**.
6. **Plus** working at height on vessels, ship's-crane lifting, landside port traffic, fatigue &
   watchkeeping, passenger-vessel evacuation, weather/tide/current.

## The sector regulatory regime (the defining feature)

Maritime and port work is **co-regulated** — maritime law for the *vessel and harbour navigation*, and
work-health-and-safety law for the *people* — and the regulator map **differs by jurisdiction**:

- **NZ — Maritime NZ at the centre.** Maritime NZ administers the **Maritime Transport Act 1994**
  (vessel/navigation; **MOSS**/Specified Limits Permit for domestic commercial vessels) and is the
  **designated HSWA regulator for work on ships**. **From 1 July 2024 it is also the H&S regulator at
  New Zealand's 13 major ports — on land *and* on ships** (WorkSafe retains inland ports, major hazard
  facilities, authorisations, and the Electricity/Gas Acts). Harbour navigational safety runs through the
  **NZ Port & Harbour Marine Safety Code** (port operators + regional-council **harbourmasters**).
- **AU — a layered, multi-regulator picture.** **Domestic Commercial Vessels** → **AMSA** (Marine Safety
  (DCV) National Law 2012) for the vessel + the **state/territory WHS regulator** for the people.
  **Prescribed (mostly interstate/international-trading) seafarers** → the **Seacare scheme** under the
  **Occupational Health and Safety (Maritime Industry) Act 1993** (with the Seafarers Act 1992), where
  **AMSA performs the OHS inspectorate** and Comcare supports the Seacare Authority. **Ports/wharves
  (landside)** → the **state WHS regulator** (AMSA holds MOUs with them); **pilotage/harbour** → state
  port authorities. International ships add **SOLAS / the ISM Code / STCW** and port-state control.

Detail and the NZ/AU contrast in `references/regulatory-regimes.md`.

## Core controls (owned here)

Summary; detail in `references/good-practice.md`:

- **Mooring & towlines** — identify and **keep clear of snap-back zones**; well-maintained lines retired
  on condition; controlled winch operation; brief the team; minimise people on deck during the operation.
- **Enclosed-space entry** — treat every hold/tank/void as **potentially lethal**: permit, **test the
  atmosphere**, ventilate, standby person, **non-entry rescue** — and never enter to rescue without
  protection → `../confined-space-specialist/`.
- **Ship-shore interface** — safe **gangway/brow** rigged with net and handrails; safe **pilot-ladder**
  rigging and transfer; PFDs at the water's edge; man-overboard recovery → `../water-safety-specialist/`.
- **Pilotage & towage** — competent pilots, safe transfer arrangements, tug **girting** awareness and
  quick-release; fatigue management for harbour crews.
- **Bunkering** — procedure, containment, no ignition sources, emergency shutdown →
  `../hazardous-substances-specialist/`.
- **Vessel safety management** — operate under an approved safety system (below); drills; competent,
  rested crew.

## Competency & the vessel safety system

- The vessel runs under an **approved safety system** — NZ **MOSS (MTOP)** / Specified Limits Permit;
  AU an AMSA **Certificate of Operation/Survey + SMS**; regulated international ships under the **ISM
  Code** (Safety Management System) with **STCW** crewing.
- **Crew competency** — NZ **SeaCert**; AU AMSA **certificates of competency** / STCW. Pilots and
  harbour crews competent and authorised. Inductions and drills current.
- Confirm the crewing/competency and high-risk-work side via `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## Method

1. **Set the jurisdiction and regime** — NZ (Maritime NZ for ships *and* the 13 major ports) or AU
   (AMSA + state WHS for DCVs; **Seacare** for prescribed seafarers; state regulators landside)?
2. **Scope the activity** — vessel operation, or port/harbour marine operation (mooring, pilotage,
   towage, bunkering, interface)? Confirm it isn't cargo-handling (→ stevedoring) or fishing (→ fishing).
3. **Confirm the safety system & crew competency** (MOSS/MTOP, AMSA Certificate + SMS, or ISM/STCW).
4. **Work the marine fatal risks** — mooring snap-back, enclosed spaces, the ship-shore interface,
   pilotage/towage, bunkering.
5. **Route the generic hazards** to their SME (water/diving, confined space, substances, traffic,
   lifting, height, psychosocial).
6. **State residual obligations** — duties under **both** maritime and WHS law; an approved safety
   system, competent rested crew and emergency capability are mandatory. Hand off accordingly.

## Jurisdiction note

**NZ:** **Maritime NZ** — MTA 1994 + **MOSS** for vessels, **designated HSWA regulator for ships**, and
(from **1 July 2024**) the **H&S regulator at the 13 major ports, land and ships**; harbour safety via
the **NZ Port & Harbour Marine Safety Code** and regional-council harbourmasters; crew via **SeaCert**.
**AU:** **AMSA** for **Domestic Commercial Vessels** (Marine Safety (DCV) National Law 2012) + the
**state/territory WHS regulator** for the people; **prescribed seafarers** under the **Seacare scheme**
(**OHS(MI) Act 1993**, AMSA as inspectorate); landside **ports/wharves** under the state WHS regulator;
international ships under **SOLAS/ISM/STCW**. Jurisdiction variations (Victoria OHS Act, WA) →
`../safework-au-specialist/`. Detail in `references/regulatory-regimes.md`; confirm current editions via
`../hse-advisor/`.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice maritime- and port-safety guidance — **not legal advice and not a
substitute for a competent person or the current maritime and work-health-and-safety law**, which is
**co-regulated and differs between NZ and Australia** (and, in AU, between AMSA, Seacare and the state
regulators). Mooring snap-back, enclosed-space entry, the ship-shore interface and pilotage/towage are
life-critical; validate every control and the safety system against the actual vessel/port operation,
crew and the current regime before relying on them.
