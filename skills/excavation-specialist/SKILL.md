# Excavation Specialist (ground disturbance SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for excavation, trenching and ground disturbance —
preventing the two killers: **collapse/engulfment** of a person in an excavation, and **contact with
underground services** (electrocution, gas, flooding). Provides the good-practice **critical
controls**, the **competency** required, and the governing NZ/AU guidance and standards.

## When to use

- Planning or reviewing any work that **breaks or penetrates the ground** — trenches, excavations,
  pits, post holes, drilling/boring.
- Managing **underground-service** strike risk (locating, potholing, minimum distances).
- Choosing **collapse prevention** — shoring, benching, or battering.
- Confirming **who is competent** to authorise and supervise the dig.

## When NOT to use

- A deep excavation that has become a **confined space** (atmosphere risk) → also
  `../confined-space-specialist/`.
- **Live electrical work** near struck services → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` /
  `../safework-au-specialist/` and a competent electrical person.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/` (this SME supplies the controls/questions).
- The **legal duty / notifiable-work** detail → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.

## The risk

- **Collapse / engulfment** — unsupported ground caves in; even shallow trenches kill (a cubic metre of
  soil weighs over a tonne and gives no warning).
- **Contact with underground services** — buried electricity, gas, water, sewer or telecommunications,
  causing electrocution, explosion, flooding or asphyxiation.
- **Falling objects / people** into the excavation; **inrush** of water/mud.

## Critical controls — apply the hierarchy

See `references/good-practice.md` for detail.

### Locate & isolate services (before any digging)
- **Identify all underground services first** — obtain service-owner plans (e.g. via the
  **beforeUdig** locating service), use electronic locating equipment, and confirm by **visual
  identification** of surface indicators.
- **Isolate** services where reasonably practicable before work starts (especially close to electrical
  conductors or gas/pressure pipelines).
- **Pothole / hand-dig near services** — expose services by **non-destructive means** (hydro-/vacuum
  excavation or careful hand digging) before machine excavation; no crow bars/picks near services.

### Prevent collapse
- **Don't enter unsupported ground.** Prevent collapse by **benching, battering (sloping)** or
  **shoring/trench boxes** — to a design by a **geotechnical/competent engineer** where there is any
  risk of collapse, accounting for soil type, water, surcharge and vibration.
- **Keep spoil and loads back from the edge** (e.g. ≥ 0.6 m), and assess whether the ground can carry
  the surcharge.
- **Inspect** the excavation regularly — at least daily and after rain, seepage, vibration or any
  change.

### Protect people
- **Exclusion zone / barriers** around the excavation (signed; set back from the edge regardless of
  depth) to keep people, the public and plant out.
- **Safe access/egress** (ladder, ramp) within easy reach — critical for escape and rescue.
- **Prevent falling objects** into the excavation (toe-boards, covers, trench-box sheets above grade).
- **Bridging** for crossings with guardrails and toe-boards.
- A **rescue plan** in place and understood before work begins; communication between plant operators
  and ground crew agreed.

## Qualifications & competency

- A **competent person** authorises and oversees the dig; for any collapse risk, shoring/benching
  designed by a **geotechnical/competent engineer**.
- **Service-locating competency** (plan interpretation, locator use, potholing).
- NZ: ground penetration over a threshold typically requires a **"certificate to excavate" / work
  permit** and consultation with asset/site owners; work near power services may need the network
  operator's consent. Confirm in `../worksafe-nz-specialist/`.
- Plant operators competent for the machine (`../high-risk-work-specialist/`).

## Jurisdiction note

NZ: WorkSafe **Excavation safety good practice guidelines**; **an excavation more than 1.5 m deep that
a person could enter is generally notifiable work** (≥ 24 h notice to WorkSafe). AU: the model **Code
of Practice "Excavation work"** (adopted per jurisdiction; Victoria differs); excavation is **high-risk
construction work** requiring a SWMS. Standard: **AS 5488** (classification of subsurface utility
information). Treat as verify-pointers; confirm via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

An **excavation control plan** (services located & isolated → collapse prevention (batter/bench/shore)
→ exclusion zone & access → falling-object & rescue → competency → critical questions), or the
excavation-specific controls/competency/questions to feed a bow tie (`../critical-risk-manager/`) or
task analysis (`../task-analysis-author/`). A draft for a competent person to validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice guidance and structure — **not legal advice, not a geotechnical
design, and not a substitute for a competent person or current standards**. Collapse and service-strike
risks are site-specific and lethal; a competent person (and a qualified engineer for shoring) must
validate the controls against the actual ground, services, water and loads before work proceeds.
