# Excavation & ground disturbance — good practice, standards & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, governing guidance, qualifications and critical questions for
excavation, trenching and ground disturbance. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from the NZ
WorkSafe **Excavation safety** good-practice guidelines and the AU model Code *Excavation work*. **Not
legal advice**; standard editions and guidance change — treat references as verify-pointers.

## Governing guidance & standards

- **NZ:** WorkSafe **Excavation safety — good practice guidelines**. Under HSWA, **an excavation more
  than 1.5 m deep that a person could enter** is generally **notifiable work** (≥ 24 h notice to
  WorkSafe). Ground penetration beyond a threshold typically needs a **"certificate to excavate"** /
  work permit and consultation with asset/site owners.
- **AU:** model **Code of Practice — *Excavation work*** (adopted per state/territory; Victoria runs
  its own). Excavation is **high-risk construction work** → a **Safe Work Method Statement (SWMS)** is
  required.
- **Standards / services:** **AS 5488** — classification of subsurface utility information; the
  **beforeUdig** service for obtaining service-owner plans (NZ/AU).

## Two ways an excavation kills

1. **Collapse / engulfment** — unsupported ground gives way with no warning; even a shallow trench can
   bury and suffocate. A cubic metre of soil weighs more than a tonne.
2. **Contact with underground services** — electricity (electrocution/arc), gas (explosion), water
   (flooding/inrush), sewer/telco. Plus falls into the excavation and inrush of water/mud.

## Critical controls (hierarchy order)

### Locate & isolate services — *before any ground is broken*
- **Identify all services first:** obtain service-owner plans (**beforeUdig**), use electronic
  locating equipment, and **visually confirm** surface indicators. Don't rely on plans alone.
- **Isolate** services where reasonably practicable before starting — especially near electrical
  conductors or gas/pressure pipelines.
- **Non-destructive excavation near services:** **pothole** to expose services using **hydro/vacuum
  excavation or careful hand digging** before any machine work; near live electrical services use a
  non-conductive nozzle and earth the equipment. **No crow bars or picks** near services; hand-dig
  within close proximity.
- Follow **minimum approach distances** to services and any **work-authority / network-operator
  consent** required near power.

### Prevent collapse
- **Never enter unsupported ground** where collapse is possible. Control by:
  - **Battering / benching** — sloping or stepping the sides to a safe angle for the soil; or
  - **Shoring / trench boxes** — to a **design by a geotechnical or competent engineer**, accounting
    for soil type, groundwater, surcharge (spoil, plant, traffic) and vibration.
- **Keep spoil and plant back from the edge** (commonly ≥ 0.6 m) and check the ground can carry the
  surcharge.
- **Inspect** the excavation **before each shift** and after rain, seepage, vibration, or any change
  in conditions; re-assess if anything changes.

### Protect people
- **Barriers / exclusion zone** around the excavation, signed and set back from the edge **regardless
  of depth**, to keep workers, the public, stock and plant out.
- **Safe access and egress** (ladder or ramp) within easy reach — vital for escape and rescue.
- **Stop objects falling in** — toe-boards, covers, trench-box sheets extending above grade, overhead
  protection for anyone working below.
- **Bridging/crossings** with standard guardrails and toe-boards.
- **Spotter / safety observer** for substantial excavations and where plant works near people; agreed
  **two-way communication** between plant operators and ground crew.
- A **rescue plan** in place and understood **before work begins** — safe egress, rescue equipment and
  first aid accessible.

## Excavation that becomes a confined space
A deep or poorly-ventilated excavation can develop an **unsafe atmosphere** (e.g. heavier-than-air
gases pooling, displacement of oxygen) — at which point it must also be managed as a **confined
space** → `../../confined-space-specialist/` (atmospheric testing, entry permit, standby, rescue).

## Qualifications & competency

- **Competent person** to plan, authorise and supervise; a **geotechnical/competent engineer** for any
  shoring/benching design where collapse is possible.
- **Service-locating competency** — interpreting plans, using locators, potholing.
- **Plant operators** competent for the machine → `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.
- NZ: hold the required **certificate to excavate / permit**; near power, the **network operator's
  consent**.

## Critical questions to consider

- Have **all services** been identified, located, communicated and controlled (plans + locator +
  visual + potholing)?
- Are services **isolated**, and are minimum approach distances and any consents in place?
- What is the **ground/soil type and water** — and is the chosen method (batter / bench / shore)
  suitable and engineer-designed where needed?
- Is **spoil kept back** from the edge, and can the ground carry the surcharge?
- Is there **safe egress**, and a **rescue plan** with equipment and people ready?
- Could the excavation become a **confined space**?
- Where will **people, the public or stock** approach from, and what keeps them out?
- Is this **notifiable work** (NZ: enterable excavation > 1.5 m) and is a **SWMS** in place (AU)?
  → `../../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../../safework-au-specialist/`.
