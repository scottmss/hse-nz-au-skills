# Confined spaces — good practice, standards & competency

Detailed good-practice controls, governing guidance, qualifications and critical questions for confined
space entry and work. Generic and company-agnostic, distilled from NZ WorkSafe confined-spaces guidance
and the AU model Code *Confined spaces*, aligned with **AS/NZS 2865**. **Not legal advice**; standard
editions and guidance change — treat references as verify-pointers.

## Governing guidance & standards

- **NZ:** WorkSafe **"Confined spaces: planning entry and working safely in a confined space."** WorkSafe
  accepts **AS/NZS 2865 *Safe working in a confined space*** as the current state of knowledge — written
  **entry permit** issued by a competent person, atmospheric testing, **standby person**, and rescue.
- **AU:** model **Code of Practice "Confined spaces"** (adopted per state/territory; Victoria differs)
  and the WHS Regulations' specific confined-space duties (entry permit, atmosphere, standby, signage,
  training). **AS 2865** applies.

## Definition

An **enclosed or partially enclosed space** not designed or intended primarily for human occupancy, in
which there is a risk of one or more of: **oxygen outside the safe range**; a **toxic airborne
contaminant**; a **flammable airborne contaminant**; or **engulfment** by free-flowing solid or rising
liquid. Examples: tanks, vessels, silos, pits, sumps, sewers, drains, ducts, manholes, scroll cases,
boilers, and **deep/poorly-ventilated excavations**.

> If in doubt, treat the space as confined and assess. Conditions can change a non-confined space into
> a confined one (e.g. an introduced contaminant, or oxygen displacement).

## Critical controls (hierarchy order)

### Eliminate the entry
- **Don't enter unless necessary** — do the task **from outside** (remote tools, cameras, clean-in-
  place) wherever reasonably practicable.

### Isolate
- **De-energise and lock-out/tag-out** moving parts and machinery in or near the space.
- **Blank/blind, isolate or disconnect** all piping, ducts and vents so steam, water, gas or bulk
  material **cannot enter** during the work.

### Make and keep the atmosphere safe
- **Clean and purge** harmful solids/sludges and gases/vapours; **purge with fresh air, never oxygen**
  (oxygen enrichment is itself a fire/explosion hazard).
- **Atmospheric testing is mandatory** — **oxygen, flammable gases (LEL), and toxic contaminants** —
  with a **correctly calibrated** detector. Test **before entry without entering** where possible, and
  **monitor continuously** during the work.
- **Forced ventilation** to establish and maintain a safe atmosphere. If a safe atmosphere **cannot be
  assured**, entrants must use **supplied-air breathing apparatus** (air-line or SCBA — not filtering
  RPE, which does nothing for oxygen deficiency).

### Authorise & supervise
- **Confined space entry permit** — written authority + safety checklist, issued by a **competent
  person** after pre-entry checks; defines the space, work, controls, atmosphere limits, duration and
  authorised people.
- **Standby person ("hole watch")** outside at all times — trained, in constant **two-way
  communication** with entrants, monitoring conditions, controlling who enters/leaves, and ready to
  raise the alarm and **initiate rescue**. The standby **must not enter to attempt rescue** without
  proper equipment and backup.
- **Exclusion zone** and **safe access/egress** sized for both entry and **rescue/retrieval**.

### Rescue — planned before entry
- A **rescue plan that is in place, resourced and rehearsed before work begins**, favouring **non-entry
  rescue** (retrieval line and full-body harness operated from outside). Rescue team competent and
  equipped for the specific space; first aid and **resuscitation (incl. oxygen)** available.
- **Most confined-space fatalities are would-be rescuers** who entered without protection. Never
  improvise an entry rescue.

### Mandatory pre-entry checks
- Energy isolations complete and **tagged**.
- Gas detector **functional and calibrated** for the gases that could be present.
- Rescue plan **tested and understood** by all parties.
- PPE **inspected and fitted**; communications **tested**.
- All **permits and certificates valid**.

### PPE (per the space and atmosphere)
Respiratory protective equipment / supplied air as required; safety helmet; gloves; hearing
protection; **full-body harness and lifeline** for retrieval.

## Qualifications & competency

- **Entrants, standby and supervisor** trained and **competent** in confined-space entry (NZ: recognised
  confined-space NZQA unit standards; AU: RTO training to the Code) — current and demonstrated.
- **Atmospheric-testing** competency: instrument selection, calibration (bump test), and interpretation.
- **Rescue team** trained and equipped for the specific space and method.
- **Permit issuer** competent to assess hazards and authorise entry. Licensing system:
  `../../high-risk-work-specialist/`.

## Critical questions to consider

- Could the task be done **without entering**?
- Is everything that could enter the space (energy, steam, water, gas, bulk material) **isolated and
  tagged**?
- Is the **gas detector** functional, calibrated and right for the gases possibly present — and is
  monitoring **continuous**?
- Is **ventilation** adequate, or is supplied air required?
- Has the **permit** been issued by a competent person, and are all pre-entry checks done?
- Is there a **trained standby person** with tested communications?
- Is the **rescue plan** practical, resourced and **rehearsed**, and does the rescue team know how to
  use the equipment — favouring **non-entry** rescue?
- Could the work itself (e.g. hot work, solvents) **change the atmosphere**?
