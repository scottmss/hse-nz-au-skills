# Confined Space Specialist (confined-space SME)

## Purpose

Be the **subject-matter expert you consult** for confined space entry and work — where the killers are
an **unsafe atmosphere** (oxygen deficiency/enrichment, toxic or flammable contaminants), **engulfment**,
and **inability to rescue**. Provides the good-practice **critical controls** (testing, isolation,
ventilation, permit, standby, rescue), the **competency** required, and the governing NZ/AU guidance
and standard (**AS/NZS 2865**). Most confined-space deaths are the would-be rescuer — so rescue and
"don't enter unless you must" are central.

## When to use

- Planning or reviewing **entry to or work in** any enclosed/partially enclosed space not built for
  occupancy (tank, vessel, silo, pit, sump, sewer, duct, manhole, scroll case).
- Deciding whether a space **is a confined space**, and how to manage the **atmosphere**.
- Setting up the **entry permit, standby and rescue** arrangements.

## When NOT to use

- The **excavation** itself (collapse/services) → `../excavation-specialist/` (which hands here if the
  dig becomes a confined space).
- **Hazardous-substance** exposure outside a confined space → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` /
  `../safework-au-specialist/` and an SDS-based assessment.
- Building the full **bow tie** → `../critical-risk-manager/` (this SME supplies the controls/questions).
- The **legal duty / notifiable** detail → `../worksafe-nz-specialist/` / `../safework-au-specialist/`.

## What makes a space "confined"

An **enclosed or partially enclosed space** not intended primarily for human occupancy, where there is
a risk of one or more of:
- an **oxygen concentration outside the safe range** (deficiency or enrichment);
- a concentration of **airborne contaminant** causing impairment, loss of consciousness or asphyxiation;
- a concentration of **flammable contaminant** risking fire or explosion; or
- **engulfment** by a free-flowing solid or rising liquid.

If in doubt, treat it as a confined space and assess. A space can *become* confined as conditions or
the work change.

## Critical controls — apply the hierarchy

See `references/good-practice.md` for detail.

### Eliminate the entry
- **Do not enter unless necessary** — perform the task **from outside** wherever reasonably
  practicable (remote tools, cameras, cleaning-in-place). The strongest control is not entering.

### Isolate
- **Isolate all energy and material inflow** — de-energise and **lock-out/tag-out** moving parts;
  **blank/blind, isolate or disconnect** piping/ducts/vents so steam, water, gas or bulk material can't
  enter.

### Make the atmosphere safe
- **Clean and purge** harmful solids/sludges and gases/vapours (purge with **fresh air, never oxygen**).
- **Test and continuously monitor** the atmosphere — **oxygen, flammable gases, and toxic
  contaminants** — with a **correctly calibrated** detector; test **before and throughout** entry,
  ideally without entering to test.
- **Forced ventilation** to maintain a safe atmosphere; if a safe atmosphere can't be assured, use
  **supplied-air breathing apparatus** (not just filtering RPE).

### Authorise & supervise
- A **confined space entry permit** — written authority issued by a **competent person** after the
  pre-entry checks, defining the work, controls, atmosphere limits, duration and people.
- A **standby person ("hole watch")** stationed outside at all times — trained, in constant
  **communication** with entrants, monitoring the atmosphere and ready to **raise the alarm and
  initiate rescue** (and who must **not enter** to attempt rescue without proper equipment/backup).
- **Exclusion zone**, and **safe access/egress** sized for entry and rescue.

### Rescue (plan it before entry)
- A **rescue plan in place, resourced and rehearsed before work begins**, favouring **non-entry
  rescue** (retrieval line/harness from outside). The rescue team competent and equipped; first aid and
  resuscitation (incl. oxygen) available. **Most confined-space fatalities are unprepared rescuers** —
  never attempt an unplanned entry rescue.

### Mandatory pre-entry checks
Energy isolations complete and tagged · gas detector functional and **calibrated** · rescue plan
tested and understood · PPE inspected and fitted · communications tested · all permits/certificates
valid.

## Qualifications & competency

- **Entrants, standby and supervisor** trained and competent in confined-space entry (NZ: recognised
  confined-space NZQA unit standards; AU: RTO training to the Code) — competency demonstrated, current.
- **Atmospheric-testing** competency (instrument use, calibration, interpretation).
- **Rescue team** trained and equipped for the specific space and rescue method.
- **Permit issuer** competent to assess and authorise. (Licensing system: `../high-risk-work-specialist/`.)

## Jurisdiction note

NZ: WorkSafe **"Confined spaces: planning entry and working safely in a confined space"**; WorkSafe
accepts **AS/NZS 2865** as the current knowledge for this work (written entry permit, standby, testing,
rescue). AU: the model **Code of Practice "Confined spaces"** (adopted per jurisdiction; Victoria
differs) + the WHS Regs' specific confined-space duties; **AS 2865** applies. Treat as verify-pointers;
confirm via `../hse-advisor/`.

## Output

A **confined-space entry plan** (is it confined? → isolate → clean/purge → test & ventilate → permit →
standby & comms → rescue plan → pre-entry checks → competency → critical questions), or the
confined-space-specific controls/competency/questions to feed a bow tie (`../critical-risk-manager/`)
or task analysis (`../task-analysis-author/`). A draft for a competent person to validate.

## Disclaimer

This skill produces good-practice guidance and structure — **not legal advice and not a substitute for
a competent person, atmospheric testing, or the current standard (AS/NZS 2865)**. Confined-space work
is high-hazard and unforgiving; a competent person must validate the atmosphere, isolation, permit,
standby and rescue arrangements against the actual space before any entry.
